# Pompom Hills — Project Roadmap

---

## Vision

Pompom Hills is a gentle preschool animation universe designed for children aged 3–4.

The goal is not simply to publish videos.

The goal is to slowly build a believable, reusable world with memorable characters, locations, and stories.

Every episode should strengthen the universe.

Every new asset should become reusable.

Every production decision should prioritize long-term efficiency.

Pompom Hills should become a world that children want to visit again and again — a safe, warm, and memorable place that grows with its audience.

---

## Core Principles

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Reusable assets** | Every character, world, and prop should support dozens of future stories |
| **Calm storytelling** | Low stimulation, slow pacing, gentle emotional arcs |
| **Character consistency** | Characters remain visually and emotionally identical across all episodes |
| **Production quality** | Each episode meets the same gold standard — no shortcuts |
| **Long-term world building** | The world grows naturally, not suddenly |
| **Asset permanence** | Characters and worlds are permanent — they never change |

## Phase 1 — Foundation

**Goal:** Build a reliable production pipeline and establish the core universe.

### Production Focus

- 1-minute episodes
- 4 shots per episode
- Simple emotional stories
- Maximum 2–3 characters per episode
- One world per episode

### Episode Style

- Discovery-based storytelling
- Gentle emotional arcs
- No conflict, no villains, no danger
- Low stimulation throughout
- Warm, safe, and comforting

### Asset Goals

- Master the 4 current production worlds
- Perfect character consistency across shots
- Establish lighting, colour, and camera standards
- Create reusable shot templates

### Success Criteria

- 10+ episodes produced at gold standard quality
- Production pipeline running smoothly
- Character consistency verified across multiple episodes
- World identity locked and maintained

---

## Phase 2 — Expansion

**Goal:** Grow the world naturally while maintaining production quality.

### How the World Grows

New worlds are introduced only after the current pipeline is proven stable.

Each new world must:
- Support at least 5 future episodes
- Be visually distinct from existing worlds
- Maintain the same art style and emotional tone
- Be producible with the existing team and tools

### Episode Evolution

- Stories remain 1 minute
- Emotional complexity increases slightly
- Character interaction becomes more nuanced
- World reuse increases (same world, different stories)

### Asset Goals

- Introduce 2–3 new production worlds
- Expand character roster to 8–10 active characters
- Create world-specific shot libraries
- Establish seasonal content patterns

### Success Criteria

- 20+ episodes produced
- 6+ production worlds active
- 8+ characters appearing regularly
- Production speed increasing without quality loss

---

## Phase 3 — Storytelling

**Goal:** Tell richer stories while keeping the preschool format.

### Richer Stories

- Multi-beat emotional arcs within 1 minute
- Character-driven episodes (not just discovery)
- Gentle humor and personality moments
- Recurring themes and callbacks

### Character Interaction

- Trio dynamics (Kiko + Mimi + Opa) fully established
- Supporting characters bring variety without confusion
- Character relationships deepen over time
- Each character has clear strengths and weaknesses

### World Continuity

- The world feels lived-in and consistent
- Locations have history and personality
- Characters have favorite spots
- The audience feels familiar with the world

### Success Criteria

- 40+ episodes produced
- Character attachment verified (audience feedback)
- World recognition established
- Production team operating at full capacity

---

## Phase 4 — Premium Stories

**Goal:** Create special episodes that celebrate the world and characters.

### Longer Episodes

- 3–5 minute episodes for special occasions
- Multi-world adventures
- Character ensemble stories
- Season premieres and finales

### Holiday Episodes

- Seasonal specials (spring, summer, autumn, winter)

### Large Adventures

- Multi-episode story arcs
- Character growth moments
- World discovery milestones
- Audience celebration episodes

### Success Criteria

- Season 1 complete (26 episodes)
- Season 2 planned and partially produced
- Special episodes produced and well-received
- Brand identity established in the market

## Asset Growth Strategy

### Characters Are Permanent Assets

Every character created becomes part of the permanent universe.

Characters are never retired, redesigned, or replaced.

They may appear less frequently, but they always exist.

### Worlds Are Permanent Assets

Every world created becomes part of the permanent universe.

Worlds are never deleted or redesigned.

They may be used less frequently, but they always exist.

### Every New Asset Should Support Dozens of Future Stories

Before creating a new asset, ask:
- How many future episodes can use this?
- Does this support existing characters?
- Does this expand the world meaningfully?
- Is this producible with current resources?

If the answer to any question is "no," reconsider the asset.

### Asset Priority

| Priority | Asset Type | Reason |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | Core characters (Kiko, Mimi, Opa) | Used in most episodes |
| 2 | Core worlds (Central Square, Opa's Tree) | Used in most episodes |
| 3 | Supporting characters (Luca, Noah, Arda) | Used in many episodes |
| 4 | Secondary worlds (Stone Hill) | Used in some episodes |
| 5 | Background characters | Used rarely |
| 6 | Specialty worlds | Used rarely |

## Production Philosophy

The objective is **not** to produce as many episodes as possible.

The objective is to build a **sustainable animation universe**.

This means:

- Every episode strengthens the world
- Every asset becomes reusable
- Every character becomes memorable
- Every story feels familiar yet fresh
- Every production decision serves the long term

Speed is not the goal.

Quality is not the goal.

**Sustainability is the goal.**

A show that can run for 10 years is more valuable than a show that burns bright for 1 year.

---

## Decision Framework

When facing a production decision, ask:

| Question | If Yes | If No |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Does this strengthen the universe? | Proceed | Reconsider |
| Can this asset be reused? | Proceed | Reconsider |
| Does this maintain character consistency? | Proceed | Reconsider |
| Does this serve the long term? | Proceed | Reconsider |
| Is this producible with current resources? | Proceed | Wait |

**If the answer to any question is "no," the decision should be delayed or reconsidered.**

## Character and World Onboarding Policy

This policy governs how new major characters and reusable worlds enter the regular episode system. It does not change existing canon, existing character designs, existing world designs, or the current publishing cadence.

### 1. Core Rule

No new major character or reusable world should enter the regular episode system without an onboarding video first.

A character or world must be introduced to the audience before it becomes part of normal educational stories.

The goal is to build recognition gradually. Children should know:

- who the character is
- where the world is
- what feeling the character/world represents
- why they should care

### 2. Character Onboarding

Before a new character becomes active in regular episodes, create a character introduction package.

**Required:**

1. **Character Introduction Video**

   Format:
   - 30–60 seconds
   - simple and warm
   - one clear emotional trait
   - one clear visual identity
   - one memorable action or gesture
   - no complex story
   - no multiple new characters

   Example titles: *Meet Kiko!*, *Meet Noah!*, *Meet Mimi!*, *Meet Arda!*, *Meet Opa!*

2. **Character Shorts**

   Each character intro should produce 3–5 Shorts. Shorts should focus on:
   - character name
   - signature movement
   - personality
   - emotional hook
   - one simple line

3. **Character Caption / Description**

   Every intro should clearly explain:
   - character name
   - personality
   - role in Pompom Hills
   - what children may learn with this character

4. **First Story Use**

   After introduction, the character can appear in:
   - one simple story
   - one educational episode
   - one friendship or discovery moment

   Do not immediately place a new character into complex multi-character episodes.

### 3. New Character Cadence

After the first core group is launched, new characters should be introduced slowly.

Default cadence: **one new character every 2–3 months.**

A new character launch should feel like a small event. Recommended launch flow:

| Week | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 1 | Teaser Shorts |
| 2 | Character Introduction Video |
| 3 | First simple story episode |
| 4 | Character joins existing world or existing friend |

### 4. First Core Group Exception

The first core group may be introduced faster because the audience needs to understand the initial cast.

Core launch phase:
- introduce the first 6 characters
- give each one a clear intro video
- use Shorts to reinforce recognition
- then move into educational stories

After the first 6 characters are known, do not continue only with introductions. Shift the main content toward preschool learning stories.

### 5. World Onboarding

Before a reusable world becomes active in regular episodes, create a world introduction package.

**Required before regular use:**

1. **Canon Hero View** — the world must have one approved Hero View.
2. **World Specification** — the world must have a production-ready World Spec.
3. **World Introduction Video**

   Create a short world intro before using the world in larger stories.

   Format:
   - 30–60 seconds for simple worlds
   - 60–90 seconds for important recurring worlds
   - no complex plot
   - one character maximum, or no character
   - the world itself is the hero

   Example titles: *Welcome to Cloud Hill*, *Let's Visit Flower Hill*, *Kiko's Home Tour*, *Welcome to Central Square*, *Meet Opa's Tree*

4. **World Shorts**

   Each world intro should produce 3–5 Shorts. Shorts should show:
   - the main landmark
   - the emotional feeling of the world
   - one simple action
   - one visual identity element
   - one reason children will remember it

### 6. World Introduction Purpose

A world intro video is not just a beautiful environment shot. It must answer:

- What is this place?
- What feeling does it create?
- What can children do here?
- What makes it different from other worlds?
- What visual element should children remember?

Examples:

| World | Feeling to Convey |
|-------|-------------------|
| Cloud Hill | sky, clouds, soft wind, looking up, wonder |
| Flower Hill | colour, flowers, path, beauty, gentle walking |
| Central Square | meeting, playing, sharing, community |
| Kiko's Home | warmth, safety, reading corner, home feeling |

### 7. When a World Does Not Need Introduction

Not every background location needs a world intro. A world intro is required only when the location is:

- reusable
- canonical
- visually distinct
- likely to appear in multiple future episodes
- important for character or story identity

If a location appears only once as a minor background, it does not require a full onboarding video.

### 8. World Rollout Cadence

Do not introduce all worlds early. Worlds should be rolled out gradually.

Recommended early worlds:
- Central Square
- Kiko's Home
- Cloud Hill
- Flower Hill
- Opa's Tree

New worlds should be introduced only when:
- the story needs them
- the production pipeline can support them
- Hero View and World Spec are ready
- there is a clear episode use case

### 9. Character + World Pairing

When possible, introduce a character with the world that best supports them.

| Character | Paired World(s) |
|-----------|------------------|
| Kiko | Kiko's Home / Flower Hill |
| Luca | Cloud Hill / discovery worlds |
| Noah | Central Square / building or play stories |
| Opa | Opa's Tree |
| Mimi | Flower Hill / nature stories |

This helps children associate characters with places.

### 10. Onboarding Order

Recommended order for any new major character or world:

1. Canon documentation complete
2. Hero View or character reference approved
3. Intro video produced
4. Shorts extracted
5. First simple story produced
6. Regular episode use begins

Do not skip the intro stage unless the character or world has already been clearly introduced in previous content.

### 11. Strategic Principle

Pompom Hills grows through recognition. Children need repetition. Parents need clarity.

The audience should not meet too many new characters or worlds at once. Every new character and every important world should feel like a meaningful addition to the universe, not just another asset.

The goal is not to reveal everything quickly. The goal is to build familiarity, trust, and anticipation over time.

## Quality Gates

Every episode must pass these gates before release:

| Gate | Check |
|------|-------|
| **Character consistency** | All characters match their reference sheets |
| **World consistency** | All worlds match their established designs |
| **Lighting continuity** | Lighting matches the continuity reference |
| **Camera continuity** | Camera movement follows the production rules |
| **Emotional arc** | Clear beginning, discovery, interaction, ending |
| **Low stimulation** | No fast cuts, no loud sounds, no visual clutter |
| **Age appropriateness** | Safe for 3–4 year olds |

- A universe that children want to revisit

This does not happen by rushing.
