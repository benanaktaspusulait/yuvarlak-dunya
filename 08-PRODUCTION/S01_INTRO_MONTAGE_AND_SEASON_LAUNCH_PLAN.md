# S01 — Intro Montage & Season Launch Plan

> **Purpose:** Plan how to reuse the already-produced, approved character intro videos to
> build promotional montage content, and how to later create one new "Season Begins" video.
>
> **This is a planning document, NOT a video generation prompt.** No OpenART prompts are
> written here. No episode shot files are rewritten. No exact video filenames are invented.
>
> **Guiding principle:** Protect approved character identity and avoid wasted OpenART credits.
> Editing existing approved footage is low-risk. New full-cast generation is high-risk.

---

## 1. Production Decision

State clearly, up front:

- We will **first** create montage videos from **existing approved intro videos**.
- We will **NOT** begin with a new full-cast OpenART generation.
- The new "Season Begins" video will come **later**.
- This is safer because existing intro footage already preserves approved character identity.

> **Primary decision: Start with existing intro montage edits. Create the new Season Begins
> video later.**

---

## 2. Why This Approach

- **Existing intro videos are already approved.** Their character identity, scale, and world
  look have already passed review.
- **Editing them together has low character drift risk.** No characters are regenerated, so
  there is no chance of drift, object spawning, wrong scale, or identity loss during a montage cut.
- **New full-cast generation is higher risk.** OpenART can easily cause character drift, world
  drift, object spawning, wrong scale, wrong camera movement, and identity loss — especially
  when many characters share one scene.
- **Montage videos can be made quickly for social media.** They reuse footage we already own,
  so turnaround is fast and cheap.
- **Montage performance can guide the later Season Begins video.** Audience response to style,
  character order, and captions tells us what to aim for before spending credits on new generation.

## 7. Editing Rules for Existing Intro Montages

- Use **only approved intro footage**.
- Do not use weak or distorted frames.
- Prefer the clearest character identity moment.
- Prefer clips with a readable face and body language.
- Do not use clips where character design looks inconsistent.
- Do not use clips with text errors, bad hands, extra limbs, strange objects, or awkward motion.
- Each character clip should show **personality**, not just appearance.
- Keep transitions soft and preschool-friendly.

**Allowed transitions:**
- soft crossfade
- warm fade
- gentle cream / white fade
- simple title card

**Avoid:**
- glitch transition
- fast zoom
- spinning transition
- harsh flash
- loud whoosh
- over-edited social media effects

---

## 8. Text Overlay Rules

These are edited social media videos, so text overlays are allowed **during editing**. Keep
text simple, clean, and minimal.

**Possible English labels:**
- Meet Kiko

- Meet Noah
- Meet Arda
- Meet Luca
- Meet Opa

**Possible Turkish labels:**
- Kiko ile tanış
- Mimi ile tanış
- Noah ile tanış
- Arda ile tanış
- Luca ile tanış
- Opa ile tanış

**Recommendation:** Use English titles if the channel is aimed at international preschool audiences.

**Do not:**
- cover faces
- place text over important character action
- use too many words
- use subtitles inside OpenART prompts
- use speech bubbles

---

## 9. Audio Rules

For montage edits:

**Preferred:**
- gentle natural ambience
- soft warm background music, only if added in editing and kept very low
- short character dialogue clips, if audio quality is clean
- simple soft transition sounds

**Avoid:**
- loud music
- fast beat
- harsh sound effects
- chaotic dialogue overlap
- sudden volume changes

> If existing intros have inconsistent audio quality, use a soft unified music / ambience bed
> and keep character dialogue minimal.

## 12. Review Checklist

Run this checklist for each montage edit:

- [ ] Each character appears clearly.
- [ ] Character identity is preserved.
- [ ] Clip selection shows personality.
- [ ] No distorted frames used.
- [ ] No awkward hands / limbs / face glitches.
- [ ] No accidental text / watermark / artifact.
- [ ] Transitions are soft and child-friendly.
- [ ] Audio is gentle and balanced.
- [ ] Video does not feel too long.
- [ ] Final title / caption supports brand identity.
- [ ] Suitable for Facebook.
- [ ] Suitable for Instagram.
- [ ] Suitable for YouTube Shorts / Reels if cropped.
