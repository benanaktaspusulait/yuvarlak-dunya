# Pompom Hills — Character & Naming Refinement Document

---

## 1. Overall Assessment: Pompom Hills as a Brand Name

### Strengths

- **Visual clarity**: The name immediately evokes the world's most distinctive visual element — the pompom trees
- **Sound quality**: "Pompom" is soft, bouncy, and fun to say. Toddlers can say it. Adults can remember it
- **Uniqueness**: Not close to any major children's brand (Peppa, Pocoyo, Cocomelon, Bluey)
- **Merchandise potential**: Pompom shapes work beautifully for plush toys, stickers, clothing
- **Visual identity**: The double-O letters create round, friendly typography
- **Domain/app availability**: Likely available (not a common phrase)

### Possible Risks

- **"Pompom" association**: Some audiences may associate "pompom" with cheerleading pom-poms, which has an American sports connotation. This is minor but worth noting
- **"Hills" limitation**: The word "Hills" suggests a rural, nature-only setting. If the show ever expands to include water, sky adventures, or indoor scenes, the name may feel restrictive
- **Phonetic length**: "Pompom Hills" is four syllables. For very young toddlers, shorter names (2-3 syllables) are easier to remember and repeat
- **International pronunciation**: While "pompom" is fairly universal, "Hills" may not translate well in non-English markets

### Verdict

**Pompom Hills is a strong name.** It works visually, phonetically, and commercially. The risks are minor and manageable. I recommend **keeping it** but also documenting alternatives in case the team wants options.

**Recommendation: KEEP "Pompom Hills"**

## 3. Recommended World Name

### **Pompom Hills**

**Why this should be the final name:**

1. **Visual anchor**: The name directly references the world's most distinctive element — the pompom trees. When someone hears "Pompom Hills," they immediately imagine round, fluffy trees on soft green hills. No other name creates this instant visual connection.

2. **Sound quality**: "Pom-pom" is onomatopoeic — it sounds like what it describes. The double consonants create a bouncy, playful rhythm. Toddlers love repeating it. Adults find it memorable.

3. **Brandability**: The name is unique, not generic, and not close to existing brands. It works as a logo, a hashtag, a channel name, and a merchandise line. The round letters (O, O) create friendly typography.

4. **Global accessibility**: "Pompom" is understood in many languages. Even in markets where "hills" doesn't translate perfectly, the "pompom" part carries the brand.

5. **Merchandise potential**: Pompom-shaped plush toys, round stickers, soft clothing — the name directly translates to product design.

6. **Risk is low**: The cheerleading association is minor and doesn't compete with children's content. The rural limitation is manageable — the show can expand to "pompom clouds" or "pompom waves" without breaking the brand.

**Final Decision: Pompom Hills**

## 4. Character Naming Principles

### Maximum Syllables
- **Main characters**: 2 syllables maximum
- **Supporting characters**: 2-3 syllables maximum
- **Rationale**: Toddlers aged 3 to 4 years can reliably pronounce 1-2 syllable words. Three syllables is the absolute maximum.

### Sound Style
- **Prefer**: Soft consonants (M, N, L, B, P, S, F, H, W)
- **Prefer**: Open vowels (A, O, U)
- **Prefer**: Bouncy, playful sounds
- **Avoid**: Hard consonants (K, G, T, D, C) — too sharp
- **Avoid**: Sibilants (S, Z, SH) — too hissy
- **Avoid**: Complex vowel combinations (EA, OO, AI) — confusing

### Letters/Sounds to Prefer
- **M, N, L**: Warm, soft, humming sounds
- **B, P**: Bouncy, playful sounds
- **A, O, U**: Open, warm vowels
- **Repeated sounds**: "Mimi", "Bibi" — easy to remember

### Letters/Sounds to Avoid
- **K, G, T, D**: Sharp, hard sounds
- **X, Q, Z, J**: Rare, confusing for toddlers
- **Complex combinations**: "Th", "Sh", "Ch" — hard to pronounce
- **Long vowels**: "Ee", "Ay" — less warm than "Ah", "Oh"

### Global/Multicultural Considerations
- Names should work in English, Spanish, Mandarin, Arabic, Hindi, and other major languages
- Avoid names that are offensive or awkward in other languages
- Prefer names that feel "international" rather than tied to one culture
- Names like "Mia", "Luna", "Kai" work across cultures

### Toddler Pronunciation Rules
- **Maximum 2 syllables** for main characters
- **Repeat consonants**: "Mimi", "Bibi" — toddlers love repetition
- **Open syllables**: "Lu-na", "Co-ver" — easier than closed syllables
- **No consonant clusters**: "Str", "Blt" — too complex
- **Visual clarity**: Names should be easy to read on screen

### YouTube/App/Merchandise Considerations
- Names should be **searchable** (unique, not common words)
- Names should be **spellable** (toddlers and parents can type them)
- Names should be **trademarkable** (not generic terms)
- Names should work as **hashtags**: #Luna #Clover #Sage
- Names should work as **channel names**: "Pompom Hills with Luna"

## 8. Character Dynamics

### How the Three Work Together

**In Every Episode:**

1. **Kiko asks or discovers**
   - "What's that?"
   - "Look!"
   - "Let's see!"
   - Kiko is the initiator, the curious one

2. **Mimi explores or reacts**
   - Hops closer to look
   - Tilts head curiously
   - Says "Let's see!"
   - Mimi is the companion, the loyal one

3. **Opa gently supports**
   - Appears nearby (never far)
   - Says "Well done!"
   - Tells a short story or gives a hint
   - Opa is the guide, the wise one

### Story Flow

```
Kiko: "What's that?" (curiosity)
    ↓
Mimi: Hops over to look (exploration)
    ↓
Opa: "Well done! Let me tell you..." (guidance)
    ↓
Together: They learn something new (discovery)
    ↓
Opa: "Well done!" (celebration)
```

### What Never Happens

- No scolding
- No punishment
- No danger
- No intense conflict
- No one is wrong or bad
- No one is left out
- No one is sad for long

### Emotional Balance

| Character | Energy | Role |
|-----------|--------|------|
| Kiko | Medium-High | Initiates, asks, discovers |
| Mimi | Medium-Low | Follows, observes, supports |
| Opa | Low | Guides, teaches, celebrates |

This creates natural story dynamics without conflict:
- Kiko's curiosity drives the episode
- Mimi's loyalty provides companionship
- Opa's wisdom provides resolution

## 9. Revised Series Pitch

**Pompom Hills** is a gentle animated series for toddlers aged 3 to 4 years, set in a soft, rounded valley where everything feels like a cuddly toy come to life. The world is made of round hills, pompom trees, curved paths, and cosy houses with sticker-like doors. The show follows Kiko, a curious little girl, and Mimi, her rabbit best friend, as they discover simple things in their valley — finding a lost toy, sharing a snack, hearing rain, planting a flower — always guided by Opa, a wise old owl who watches over them with warmth and patience. Each episode is 7 minutes long, with very simple English, repeated phrases, and soft music. The visual style is warm, pastel, and toy-like — richer than Pocoyo but simpler than Disney — with no sharp corners, no scary moments, and no fast movement. Every episode teaches one small emotional skill through gentle storytelling, repetition, and kindness. Pompom Hills is designed to be visually consistent across AI-generated episodes, with clear character designs, a defined colour palette, and a world that is easy to recreate. It is a safe, warm, and lovable world that toddlers will want to visit again and again.

## 10. First Season Character Strategy

### Recommendation: Main Trio Only

**Season 1 should use ONLY the main trio: Kiko, Mimi, and Opa.**

> **Production Update (v2.1):** Bu öneri, projenin sonraki genişletilmiş 13 karakterlik
> kadrosundan (bkz. `characters/pompom-hills/`) önce yazılmıştır. Güncel karar: ana trio
> duygusal çıpa ve bölüm lideri olarak kalır; ek karakterler S01E01'de kısa cameo tanıtımları
> olarak yer alır. Aşağıdaki gerekçe orijinal tasarım mantığı olarak korunmuştur.

### Why

1. **Toddler cognitive load**: Children aged 3 to 4 years are still learning to recognise and remember faces. Adding too many characters overwhelms them.

2. **Consistency**: With only three characters, AI-generated episodes remain visually consistent. Each character is easy to recognise and reproduce.

3. **Emotional attachment**: Toddlers form bonds with characters. Three characters allow deep attachment. Six characters create shallow attachment.

4. **Story simplicity**: Three-character stories are easier to write, easier to follow, and easier to learn from.

### When to Add Characters

- **Season 2**: Add 1-2 occasional background characters (a bird, a butterfly, a squirrel)
- **Season 3**: Add 1 more friend character (another child or animal)
- **Never**: More than 5-6 characters total

### Background Characters

- Appear briefly (5-10 seconds)
- No speaking lines
- No distinct personality
- Just "village life" ambience
- Should not distract from main trio

### How to Avoid Overwhelming Toddlers

- **Visual clarity**: Each character has one main colour
- **Consistent appearance**: Characters never change clothes or look
- **Limited screen time**: Only 1-2 characters per scene (usually all three together)
- **No crowd scenes**: Never more than 3-4 characters visible
- **Simple backgrounds**: Backgrounds are soft and uncluttered
