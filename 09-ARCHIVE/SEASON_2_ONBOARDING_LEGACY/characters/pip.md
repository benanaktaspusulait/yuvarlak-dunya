## Character Description

Pip is a small, curious squirrel who loves music.

He sits on branches and hums soft melodies.

He does not speak yet — he communicates through humming, tilting his head, and small gestures.

He is shy but warm. He watches from a distance before approaching.

## Intro Video Concept

### Title: "Meet Pip!"

**Duration:** 30–45 seconds

**Script:**

| Time | Visual | Audio |
|------|--------|-------|
| 0–5 sec | Central Square, morning light | Soft birdsong |
| 5–10 sec | A small branch sways | Gentle humming begins |
| 10–15 sec | Pip sits on the branch, head tilted | Humming continues |
| 15–20 sec | Pip closes eyes, sways to his own melody | Soft, warm humming |
| 20–25 sec | Kiko looks up, curious | Humming fades slightly |
| 25–30 sec | Pip opens eyes, sees Kiko, tilts head | Quiet pause |
| 30–35 sec | Pip hums one last note, smiles | Final note |
| 35–40 sec | Title card: "Pip" | Soft jingle |

**Key Moment:** Pip's head tilt — this becomes his signature gesture.

## First Story Use

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Episode | Season 2, Episode 1 |
| Title | "The Shy Squirrel" |
| Characters | Kiko, Aiko |
| World | Little Forest |
| Pip's Role | Visual cameo — sits on branch, hums |
| Speaking | None |

**Story beat:** Kiko and Aiko find a shy squirrel hiding behind a tree. They wait patiently. The squirrel tilts its head, hums a note, and stays.

**Purpose:** Build recognition. Children see Pip, hear his hum, learn he is shy but friendly.

---

## Regular Episode Use (After Onboarding)

After Episode 1, Pip can appear in:
- Little Forest episodes (background or supporting)
- Music Hill episodes (main or supporting)
- Any episode needing a "quiet observer" character

Do NOT place Pip in complex multi-character episodes until Season 3.

---

## Voice Notes (Future — Season 3)

When Pip speaks:
- Soft, quiet voice
- Short sentences
- Musical rhythm in speech
- "Hmm..." before speaking
- Never loud, never fast
