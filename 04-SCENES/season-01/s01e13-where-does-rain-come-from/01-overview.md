# S01E13 — Where Does Rain Come From

---

## Duration

1:30 (6 shots × 15 seconds)

---

## Theme

Discovering rain through shared curiosity, quiet observation, and the living world of Cloud Hill.

---

## Learning

Curiosity • Nature • Shared discovery

Children should naturally understand that rain comes from clouds.

The lesson should emerge from what Luca and Mimi see, feel, and notice together.

---

## Characters

| Character | Role |
|---|---|
| Luca | Curious explorer |
| Mimi | Observant friend |

Neither character is a teacher.

Neither character gives long explanations.

---

## World

Cloud Hill

Cloud Hill should feel like a participant in the story: wind moves the grass, clouds drift and darken, CH-STONE-01 offers a quiet place to watch, the waiting silence builds attention, and the first raindrop gives the answer.

---

## Story Summary

Luca and Mimi climb Cloud Hill together on a cloudy day. They notice the wind, the moving grass, and the huge clouds above them. Luca wonders if rain comes from the clouds. Mimi watches quietly and notices one cloud becoming darker and fuller. The children wait without forcing an answer. Luca feels the first raindrop on his hand. They both look at it, smile, and look back up as gentle rain begins.

The final feeling is: "We discovered something together."

---

## Emotional Flow

Curiosity → Wonder → Thinking → Waiting → Discovery → Joy

---

## Production Intent

This episode should feel like a real preschool animation moment, not an educational demonstration.

Use short dialogue, small reactions, silence, and environmental action.

Every 3-5 seconds, something small should happen: looking up, blinking, pointing, touching grass, sitting, holding out a hand, sharing a small laugh, watching the sky, feeling the first drop, or smiling into the rain.
