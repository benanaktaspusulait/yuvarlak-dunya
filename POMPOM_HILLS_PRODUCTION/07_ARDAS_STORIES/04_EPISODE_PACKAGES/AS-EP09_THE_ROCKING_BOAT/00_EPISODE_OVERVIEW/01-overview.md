## Logline

Arda finds his soft toy boat and places it in a shallow stream in the garden. The boat rocks
gently — he pushes it too hard and it tips over. Arda slows down, tries a gentler push, and the
boat floats smoothly. He blows on it and it moves beautifully. Luca and Noah arrive with more
boats, and together they race their three boats gently across the water.

---

## Story Promise

This episode extends the central promise of *Arda's Stories*: Arda begins with joyful water
play using a soft toy boat, meets a small problem — pushing too hard tips the boat — tries again
with a calmer method, discovers that gentle force works better, and carries that discovery into
shared boat play with Luca and Noah.

---

## Learning Outcome

Children see that gentle force moves objects more effectively than hard force. When Arda pushes
the boat too hard, it tips over. An unsuccessful first try is okay. Observing and trying again
with a gentler approach reveals a simple, wonderful truth: gentle pushes make the boat float
smoothly. The final boat parade models cooperation, shared discovery, and joyful group play.
