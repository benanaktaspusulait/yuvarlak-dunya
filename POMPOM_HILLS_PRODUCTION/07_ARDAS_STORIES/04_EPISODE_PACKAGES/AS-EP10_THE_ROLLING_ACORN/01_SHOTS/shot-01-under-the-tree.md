## Start Frame

This is the first story shot. Arda is crouching near the base of the rounded tree, already
exploring. The first second should establish the garden environment and Arda's presence
naturally.

Preferred structure:
- 0-1 sec: Establish garden and tree; Arda is already crouching.
- 1-5 sec: Arda peers under the low branches.
- 5-10 sec: Arda reaches carefully into the soft soil.
- 10-15 sec: Arda lifts out the acorn; delighted expression.

This still defines the Episode Colour Master (exposure, brightness, colour temperature,
saturation, contrast). All later shots must match it.

Maintain:
- Character appearance
- Garden environment
- Warm morning daylight
- Camera composition
- Character proportions

Do not redesign the environment.

## Background Object Lock

The first frame defines the physical garden set. All visible elements — tree, grass, daisies,
path, fence, house in background — remain stable throughout the shot.

Do not:
- remove the tree
- replace grass with pavement
- add modern objects
- repaint the environment mid-shot
- change the garden layout

Allowed: Arda moves, grass sways gently, leaves move gently, camera moves slowly if planned.

## Camera Direction

Begin with a soft 35mm wide establishing shot that shows the garden and the rounded tree.
Arda is already crouching near the tree base.

Within the first 3-5 seconds he peers under the low branches.

Perform a gentle push-in as Arda reaches into the soil and discovers the acorn.

Focus settles softly on the character and the acorn while preserving the established camera
distance.

Camera movement must remain slow, stable and natural. Warm morning daylight throughout.

Avoid sudden zooms, shakes, whip pans or dramatic cinematic movements.
