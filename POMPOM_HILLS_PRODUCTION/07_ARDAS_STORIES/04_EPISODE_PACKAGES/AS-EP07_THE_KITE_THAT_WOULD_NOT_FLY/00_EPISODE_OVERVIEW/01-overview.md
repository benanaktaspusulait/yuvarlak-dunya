## Logline

Arda has a soft colourful kite. He tries to fly it in his garden, but there is no wind — the kite
flops to the ground every time. He runs with it, tries from a hill, still nothing. Arda slows down,
watches the leaves, feels a tiny breeze, and waits. The wind comes! The kite lifts into the sky.
Luca and Noah arrive with their own kites, and together they fly three kites in the bright sky.

---

## Story Promise

This episode extends the central promise of *Arda's Stories*: Arda begins with joyful physical play —
trying to fly a kite — meets a small, safe problem — no wind — tries again with energy but it does not
work. He slows down, observes the world around him, discovers that wind comes if you wait for it, and
carries that patience into shared kite play with Luca and Noah.

## Learning Outcome

Children see that sometimes things do not work on the first try — and that is okay. Arda tries hard
to fly his kite, but the wind is not ready. Instead of giving up, he slows down, watches the leaves,
feels the air, and waits patiently. When the wind arrives, the kite flies beautifully. The episode
models patience, observation, and the joy of shared play — flying kites together is more fun than
flying alone.
