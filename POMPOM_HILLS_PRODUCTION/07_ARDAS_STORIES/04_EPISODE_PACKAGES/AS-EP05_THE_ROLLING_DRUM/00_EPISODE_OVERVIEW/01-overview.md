## Logline

When Arda finds a soft toy drum in his play area, he taps it and discovers it makes a gentle
'poof' sound. He rolls it — it rolls away, making rhythmic sounds. Arda chases it, learns that
rolling gently makes a steady beat, rolling fast makes a fast beat, and finds the perfect middle
speed. Friends bring more drums, and a joyful drum circle begins.

---

## Story Promise

This episode extends the central promise of *Arda's Stories*: Arda begins with joyful play,
meets a physical puzzle — a drum that rolls away and changes tempo — tries again with a calmer
method, discovers the simple truth that speed controls rhythm, and carries that discovery into
shared play with Luca and Noah.

## Repeated Rhyme

```text
Slow roll? Poof… poof…
Fast roll? Poof-poof-poof!
Together? Poof poof poof!
```

---

## Learning Outcome

Children see that rhythm is connected to movement speed — slow rolling creates a gentle, steady
beat, fast rolling creates an energetic beat, and the right speed creates the best rhythm. An
unsuccessful first try (the drum rolling away) is okay. Observing before trying again reveals
a simple, wonderful truth: you control the beat with your hands. The final drum circle models
cooperation, shared rhythm, and joyful group play.
