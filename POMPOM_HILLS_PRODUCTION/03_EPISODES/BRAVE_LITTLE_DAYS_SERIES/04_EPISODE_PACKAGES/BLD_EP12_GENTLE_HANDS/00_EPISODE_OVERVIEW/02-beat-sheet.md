# Gentle Hands — Beat Sheet v1.0

---

## Story Rhythm

```
Beat 1 (Shot 01): Kiko and Arda are in the Living Room. A soft rounded toy car sits on the play rug. Both notice it and reach for it at the same time — not aggressively, but with eager curiosity. The problem is established.

Beat 2 (Shot 02): Kiko's hands tense up — the hitting impulse is visible. Her face shows big feeling: brows furrow, jaw tightens. Arda watches with mild concern. NO contact is made. The impulse is held.

Beat 3 (Shot 03): Kiko pauses. She remembers the breathing technique from EP11: "Breathe in, breathe out." This is an independent self-calming moment — Opa is not present.

Beat 4 (Shot 04): Kiko's hands visibly soften — tension drops, fingers open, hands settle on her knees. She looks at Arda and speaks calmly: "I want a turn." Words instead of hands.

Beat 5 (Shot 05): Arda listens and gently pushes the toy car to the centre: "Here you go." Both children begin to guide the car together on the rug. Speaking instead of hitting solved the problem.

Beat 6 (Shot 06): Kiko traces a soft path on the rug. Arda drives the car along it. Hands are used for helping — the physical proof of the lesson.

Beat 7 (Shot 07): They park the car together. Kiko looks at her hands, then at Arda: "Hands are for helping, not hurting." A shared discovery, not a lecture.

Beat 8 (Shot 08): Both sit beside the car, face the camera: "Hands are for helping!" They give a small gentle wave with soft hands. Warm closing.
```
