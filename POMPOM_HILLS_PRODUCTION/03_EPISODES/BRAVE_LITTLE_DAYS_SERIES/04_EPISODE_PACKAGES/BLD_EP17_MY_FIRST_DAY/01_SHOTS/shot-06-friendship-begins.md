## Purpose

The friendship payoff: Kiko and Amara draw together on the same piece of paper — Kiko draws a
circle, Amara draws a square. The shared creative activity bonds them. Separation anxiety has
fully dissolved into playful connection.

---

## Opening Hook

First 2–3 seconds: A sheet of soft paper lies on the round table. Kiko's hand holds a large
coral crayon and begins drawing a circle. Amara watches, then reaches for her own crayon.

---

## Continuity

Direct continuation from Shot 05. The crayons are on the table; Kiko and Amara are engaged.
Now they begin drawing together — the natural next step from exploring crayons to using them.
Background remains locked.

## Emotion

Friendship through shared creation. Drawing together on the same paper is a cooperative act —
neither child dominates, both contribute. The circle and square are simple, but the act of
making them together creates a bond. Kiko's earlier hesitation is gone; she's present and
engaged.
