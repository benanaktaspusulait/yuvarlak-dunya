---

## Purpose

Teach the "look" step: Kiko and Opa slowly look right and left together. The world is calm
and clear — this is the visual confirmation that it's safe to cross.

---

## Opening Hook

First 2–3 seconds: Kiko and Opa, still holding hands, begin to turn their heads slowly to
the right. The movement is deliberate and calm — a shared ritual.

---

## Continuity

Direct continuation from Shot 04. The green disc is now lit; Kiko and Opa have acknowledged
"Green!" Now they look both ways before stepping onto the path. Background remains locked.

---

## Environment Anchors

- Same Pompom Crossing practice path; stepping-stone band and green disc visible.
- Kiko and Opa holding hands, standing at the path start.
- The world around them is calm and clear — soft grass, open space.
- No road, no asphalt, no cars, no traffic.
- No new props, no new characters, no background movement.

---

## Background Object Lock

Identical to Shots 01–04. Background locked from first frame. Only Kiko and Opa move
(looking right, then left). The stepping-stone band, colour discs, and soft grass remain
stable.

## Camera Direction

Medium two-shot at child eye level. Camera follows their gaze as they look right, then
left — a gentle pan movement that mirrors their head turns. Then settles back to medium
as Kiko repeats the action.

---

## Dialogue

```text
Opa: Look right... and left.
Kiko: Right... left!
```

## Sound

A quiet moment — silence for the looking action. Kiko's "Right... left!" repetition is
playful and rhythmic, like a learning game. Soft playground ambience continues.

---

## Emotion

The "look" ritual becomes a game. Kiko enjoys the rhythm of looking right and left —
"Right... left!" is almost like a dance. The world is calm, the path is clear, and the
ritual feels safe and fun.

---

## Safe-Exposure Notes

No cars passing, no traffic, no "it's clear" from a distance, no rushing, no "let's run."
The looking is slow, deliberate, and thorough — modeling careful behavior.
