## CRITICAL Production Rule — No Surprise Elements + Staged Full-Cast

The video builds from single-character shots up to a full-cast finale. Per-shot character counts
are fixed and locked from the first frame: Shot 01 (1), Shot 02 (1), Shot 03 (2), Shot 04 (2),
Shot 05 (3), Shot 06 (6). Each shot document carries a **No Surprise Elements Rule** and an
**Approved Characters and Props** list.

Hard rules for every shot:
- The character list is locked from the first frame — no extra, background, duplicate, partial,
  shadow, reflection, or later-entering characters.
- The object list is locked from the first frame — no new props, toys, signs, furniture, stones,
  or background details appear later.
- The camera must not pan/zoom/pull-back/tilt/orbit/reframe to reveal new characters or objects.

**Shot 06 (full cast) is the highest-risk shot.** All six characters must be present in the first
frame (no one enters later). Generate a cheap still/preview first and reject-and-regenerate if the
first frame is wrong — see the Production Warning in `shot-06-full-cast-central-square.md`.

Each shot is an independent generation (world ref + the approved character ref(s)). Shots are
joined in edit with soft crossfades; keep colour/lighting unified warm so the cut feels like one film.
