## Purpose

Story Circle is a peaceful outdoor storytelling space beneath a large ancient tree, surrounded by round sitting stones. It is where Opa reads stories every evening, where children gather to listen, and where imagination and wisdom flow between generations.

---

## Immutable Identity

The following elements define the canonical identity of Story Circle and must never change:

- Large ancient storytelling tree with wide canopy
- Round sitting stones arranged in a circle
- Warm amber light filtering through leaves
- Peaceful, quiet atmosphere
- Natural materials throughout
- No noise, no speed, no sharp edges
- Intergenerational warmth and wisdom

---

## Visual Identity

A large ancient tree with a wide canopy dominates the space. Round sitting stones are arranged in a circle beneath its branches. Warm amber sunlight filters through the leaves, creating dappled light patterns on the stones and ground. The atmosphere is deeply peaceful and wise. Everything reads as natural, rounded, and timeless. The overall feeling is a "wise gathering place" — where stories are born and memories are made.

---

## World Layout

The world reads as one continuous gathering space. A path leads from the surrounding forest to the tree, which anchors the circle of stones. There are no separate structures — just one flowing natural space from path to tree to stones.

---

## Spatial Relationships

- The path connects naturally to the tree base.
- The stones are always arranged around the tree.
- The tree canopy always covers the stones.
- Light always filters through the leaves.
- The surrounding forest frames the scene.
- The sky is visible through gaps in the canopy.

---

## Playable Areas

**Path Approach** — Purpose: the journey to the circle. Visual identity: natural stone path through green grass, tree visible ahead. Important props: path, grass. Typical use: approaching, anticipation.

**Tree Base** — Purpose: first contact with the ancient tree. Visual identity: thick trunk, roots, moss. Important props: trunk, roots. Typical use: touching, looking up, respect.

**Stone Circle** — Purpose: the heart of the space — where sitting and listening happens. Visual identity: round stones in a circle, dappled light, canopy overhead. Important props: stones, light patterns. Typical use: sitting, listening, gathering.

**Canopy View** — Purpose: looking up through the leaves. Visual identity: branches, leaves, sky visible through gaps. Important props: branches, leaves. Typical use: wonder, contemplation, sky observation.

---

## Materials

Tree: ancient wood, thick bark, natural texture. Stones: smooth, round, natural grey-brown. Ground: soft green grass, fallen leaves. Light: warm amber, dappled through leaves. All surfaces read as natural, ancient, and sacred. No artificial materials, no sharp edges, no modern elements.

---

## Lighting

Primary light is warm amber sunlight filtering through the tree canopy, creating beautiful dappled patterns on the stones and ground. The light shifts gently with the breeze moving the leaves. Shadows are soft and green-tinted. The overall feeling is warm, sacred, and peaceful.

## Scale

Everything is sized for small preschool children sitting on the ground. The stones are low and round, easy to sit on. The tree is massive, creating a sense of awe and protection. The circle is intimate — large enough for a small group, small enough to feel enclosed and safe.

---

## Atmosphere

Peaceful. Wise. Warm. Sacred. Timeless. Every surface reads as ancient and natural. The mood is consistently serene and reverent — a place where children feel "this is a special place for special stories."

---

## Consistency Rules

- The tree is always large and ancient — never small, never young.
- The stones are always round and smooth — never sharp, never irregular.
- The light is always warm amber — never cold, never artificial.
- The atmosphere is always peaceful — never noisy, never rushed.
- No modern elements, no artificial structures, no sharp edges.
- The circle arrangement is always present — never scattered.

---

## Generation Considerations

The tree must be generated as large and ancient — not as a small sapling or generic tree. Keep all shapes rounded. Keep the light warm and amber. The space's identity — its wisdom, its peace, its timelessness — must be preserved exactly across every generation.

## Framing

Tree always visible in every wide shot — it is the primary visual anchor. Stones visible beneath.

| Shot | Max Character % | Tree Visible |
|------|:---------------:|:------------:|
| Wide establishing | 15–20% | Always |
| Medium single | 30–35% | Yes |
| Medium group | 40–45% | Yes |
| Close-up | 65–75% | Partial |

Emotional peak = wide shot showing full tree with circle of stones beneath.

---

## Navigation

Default flow: Path (approach) → Tree Base (contact) → Stone Circle (gathering) → Canopy (wonder).
The journey from path to tree — the growing sense of reverence — should feel natural and unhurried.
**Alternative openings are permitted when story continuity requires them.**

---

## Generation Failures — Quick Reference

| Failure | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Tree too small | Make tree large and ancient |
| Stones not round | Smooth all stones |
| Cold or artificial light | Warm to amber (#FFB74D) |
| Noisy or busy atmosphere | Create peaceful silence |
| Sharp edges anywhere | Smooth all forms |
| Modern elements present | Remove — natural only |
| No dappled light effect | Add leaf-filtered light |
