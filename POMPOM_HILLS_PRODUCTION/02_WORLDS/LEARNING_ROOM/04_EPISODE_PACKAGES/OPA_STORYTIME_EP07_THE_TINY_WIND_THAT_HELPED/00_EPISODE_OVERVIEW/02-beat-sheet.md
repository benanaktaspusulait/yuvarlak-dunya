# The Tiny Wind That Helped — Beat Sheet v2.0 (5-Minute Version)

---

## Story Rhythm

```
Beat 1 (Shot 01): Kiko and Noah arrive at the Reading Corner on a breezy sunny day, sit near Opa. Series opening beat: "Come close, little friends."
Beat 2 (Shot 02): Opa opens the book. Children lean in with curiosity.
Beat 3 (Shot 03): "Once there was a tiny wind." Noah asks if it was very tiny.
Beat 4 (Shot 04): The tiny wind wanted to help everyone. Kiko asks how.
Beat 5 (Shot 05): The tiny wind thinks it is too small to help. Noah notices.
Beat 6 (Shot 06): "Can something so tiny help?" Kiko wonders aloud.
Beat 7 (Shot 07): The tiny wind gives a soft puff and gently lifts a kite. Noah cheers.
Beat 8 (Shot 08): "What else can it do?" Kiko is curious.
Beat 9 (Shot 09): The tiny wind helps a fluffy seed float down to soft soil. Noah asks if a flower will grow.
Beat 10 (Shot 10): The tiny wind cools a warm friend on a sunny day. Kiko compares it to a breeze.
Beat 11 (Shot 11): "Even a little help makes a big difference." Opa smiles. Noah checks: even a tiny bit?
Beat 12 (Shot 12): "The little wind helped everyone!" Kiko's warm reaction.
Beat 13 (Shot 13): "I can help a little too." Noah's micro-moment.
Beat 14 (Shot 14): Book closes gently. Warm closing begins. No new dialogue.
Beat 15 (Shot 15): Children smile softly. Quiet warmth.
Beat 16 (Shot 16): Breezy sunny window light, gentle air movement, calm.
Beat 17 (Shot 17): Gentle pull-back, the Reading Corner widens softly.
Beat 18 (Shot 18): Quiet warm moment shared together.
Beat 19 (Shot 19): Final warm hold.
Beat 20 (Shot 20): Quiet warm closing hold. No new dialogue; leads into closing bumper.
```
