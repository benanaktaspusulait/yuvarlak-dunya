# The Tiny Wind That Helped — Beat Sheet v1.0 (5-Minute Version)

---

## Story Rhythm

```
Beat 1 (Shot 01): Kiko and Noah arrive at the breezy Reading Corner, sit near Opa. Series opening beat: "Come close, little friends."
Beat 2 (Shot 02): Opa opens the book. Children lean in with curiosity as gentle air stirs the pages.
Beat 3 (Shot 03): "Once there was a tiny wind." Kiko asks if it was really that small.
Beat 4 (Shot 04): The big winds did big things — sailboats and tall trees. Noah is impressed; the tiny wind feels small.
Beat 5 (Shot 05): "Can a tiny wind help too?" Kiko wonders out loud.
Beat 6 (Shot 06): The tiny wind takes a brave breath and tries. Noah asks what it did.
Beat 7 (Shot 07): It nudges a stuck kite up into the sky. Kiko cheers softly.
Beat 8 (Shot 08): "What else can it help?" Noah asks with bright curiosity.
Beat 9 (Shot 09): It carries a little seed to soft, warm soil. Kiko asks if it will grow.
Beat 10 (Shot 10): It pushes a tiny leaf-boat gently across a puddle. Noah watches it glide.
Beat 11 (Shot 11): "Even a little help makes a big difference." Kiko repeats the idea back.
Beat 12 (Shot 12): "The little wind did a lot!" Noah's warm reaction.
Beat 13 (Shot 13): "I can help a little too." Micro-moment. Opa: "That is a kind thing, Kiko."
Beat 14 (Shot 14): Book closes gently. Closing beat begins. No new dialogue.
Beat 15 (Shot 15): Children smile softly in the warm breezy light.
Beat 16 (Shot 16): Breezy window light drifts across the Reading Corner.
Beat 17 (Shot 17): Gentle pull-back; the cozy room widens.
Beat 18 (Shot 18): Quiet warmth; shared stillness.
Beat 19 (Shot 19): Final warm hold; characters settle.
Beat 20 (Shot 20): Quiet warm hold. Transition to closing bumper. No new dialogue.
```
