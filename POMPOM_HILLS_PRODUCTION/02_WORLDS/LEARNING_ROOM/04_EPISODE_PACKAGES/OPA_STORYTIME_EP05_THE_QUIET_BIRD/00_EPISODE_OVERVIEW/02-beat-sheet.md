# The Quiet Bird — Beat Sheet v2.0 (5-Minute Version)

---

## Story Rhythm

```
Beat 1 (Shot 01): Luca and Mimi sit with Opa in the Reading Corner as rain falls softly outside. Series opening beat: "Come close, little friends."
Beat 2 (Shot 02): Opa opens the book. Children lean in with curiosity.
Beat 3 (Shot 03): "Once, in a forest full of chatting birds, there was one quiet bird." Luca asks why she didn't sing loudly.
Beat 4 (Shot 04): All the other birds talked and sang all day, but the quiet bird just listened. Mimi reacts, recognising herself a little.
Beat 5 (Shot 05): Luca and Mimi both ask why the bird stayed so quiet. Opa explains gently.
Beat 6 (Shot 06): She liked to listen more than she liked to talk — the wind, the leaves, everything. Luca is fascinated.
Beat 7 (Shot 07): Because she was quiet, she noticed a soft rustle no one else heard. Luca leans in, fascinated.
Beat 8 (Shot 08): Mimi asks what the rustle sounded like. Opa describes it as a tiny whisper in the leaves.
Beat 9 (Shot 09): The quiet bird finds a tiny hidden nest that no one else had seen. Mimi gasps softly with delight.
Beat 10 (Shot 10): Luca connects that being quiet helped the bird see what others could not. Opa confirms.
Beat 11 (Shot 11): Opa gives the lesson. Mimi connects it to herself: "Being quiet helps me notice more."
Beat 12 (Shot 12): Mimi says the rain is quiet too and she can hear it. Luca agrees. Opa says they both know how to listen.
Beat 13 (Shot 13): Luca and Mimi say they liked the story. Opa tells them they are brave too.
Beat 14 (Shot 14): Opa gently closes the book. Quiet warm hold begins.
Beat 15 (Shot 15): Children smile; group warmth.
Beat 16 (Shot 16): Soft rain runs down the arched window; quiet warmth.
Beat 17 (Shot 17): Very slow pull-back reveals the full Reading Corner.
Beat 18 (Shot 18): Wide warm hold; no movement.
Beat 19 (Shot 19): Soft rain drops trace gentle paths down the window.
Beat 20 (Shot 20): Final warm hold; closing bumper follows.
```
