> Source of truth: `31-learning-room-bible.md` (canonical, creative). This document

## Purpose

Learning Room is a cozy wooden preschool learning cottage interior where curiosity, creativity and friendship come alive. It is the permanent canonical learning space for all PomPom Hills educational scenes — story time, drawing, arts & crafts, colour/number/shape learning, nature lessons, music time, quiet reading, and friendship moments.

---

## Immutable Identity

The following elements define the canonical identity of the Learning Room and must never change:

- Cozy wooden cottage interior
- Large arched windows with warm morning sunlight
- Rounded wooden ceiling beams and floorboards
- Cream walls with indoor plants everywhere
- Large round wooden activity table in center
- Small rounded preschool chairs (4–6 capacity)
- Large rounded chalkboard on back wall
- Hand-drawn chalk illustrations (sun, mountain, tree, butterfly, rainbow, stars)
- Low wooden bookshelves with books facing outward
- Reading corner with soft rug, cushions, lantern, stuffed animals
- Creative corner with paper, crayons, blocks, clay, nature collection
- Nature shelf with globe, wooden bird, magnifying glass, toy microscope
- Children's artwork, paper stars, leaf garlands, cloud mobiles, butterflies
- Warm morning sunlight streaming through windows
- No characters, no text, no logos, no blackboard writing

---

## Visual Identity

A warm wooden learning cottage interior. Rounded shapes only — no sharp edges anywhere. Soft clay and plush materials give every surface a handcrafted, toy-like quality. The palette is warm and pastel, centred on natural wood tones with cream walls and soft accent colours. Warm golden morning sunlight streams through large arched windows. The overall feeling is a "cozy learning cottage" — inviting, soft, and visually simple enough for a preschool audience to read instantly.

---

## World Layout

The room reads as one continuous connected learning space rather than separate areas. The activity table sits at the center. The chalkboard anchors the back wall. Side walls hold bookshelves and storage. A reading corner occupies one side, a creative corner the other. A nature shelf adds discovery elements. All zones flow naturally into each other without walls or dividers.

---

## Spatial Relationships

- The activity table is always the central focus of the room.
- The chalkboard is always visible from the table and entrance.
- The reading corner remains visually connected to the main space.
- The creative corner shares light with the windows.
- The nature shelf is always accessible from the main area.
- Windows maintain a visual relationship with the outdoor world.
- Plants are distributed throughout, connecting all zones.

## Playable Areas

**Activity Area (Center)** — Purpose: the heart of the room, all group activities happen here. Visual identity: large round wooden table, 4–6 small rounded chairs, warm wood tones. Important props: table, chairs. Typical use: drawing, puzzles, colour learning, group work.

**Chalkboard Area** — Purpose: visual learning and presentation. Visual identity: large rounded chalkboard with simple hand-drawn illustrations. Important props: chalkboard, chalk drawings. Typical use: shape recognition, storytelling, visual learning.

**Reading Corner** — Purpose: quiet, peaceful reading zone. Visual identity: soft rug, floor cushions, tiny bookshelf, lantern, stuffed animals. Important props: rug, cushions, bookshelf, lantern, stuffed animals. Typical use: calm, quiet moments, book exploration.

**Creative Corner** — Purpose: art and creativity zone. Visual identity: paper, crayons, colour pencils, wooden blocks, clay, nature collection. Important props: crayons, pencils, paper, blocks, clay, leaves, pinecones, flowers, magnifying glass. Typical use: drawing, sculpting, building, nature exploration.

**Nature Shelf** — Purpose: nature discovery and observation. Visual identity: small globe, wooden bird, acorns, feathers, flowers, toy microscope. Important props: globe, bird, magnifying glass, microscope. Typical use: nature study, observation, curiosity.

## Materials

Walls: soft matte cream. Floor: warm wooden planks. Ceiling beams: rounded natural wood. Table and chairs: smooth matte natural wood, rounded forms. Chalkboard: soft matte, rounded frame. Bookshelves: low natural wood. Rug: soft plush. Cushions: soft plush. Lantern: soft matte. All surfaces read as touchable and gentle. No glass sharpness, no metal, no plastic, no realistic textures anywhere.

---

## Lighting

Primary light is warm morning sunlight streaming through large arched windows. The light falls gently across the room, creating soft golden pools on the floor and table. Indoor lighting is always warm, mixing window daylight with a cozy interior glow. Shadows are always soft and short — never hard, never dark. Bounce lighting fills the room evenly.

---

## Colour Palette

Walls: warm cream. Floor: warm wood brown. Ceiling beams: natural wood. Furniture: natural wood tones. Chalkboard frame: natural wood. Rug: warm pastel. Cushions: pastel green, pink, yellow. Plants: soft green. Accent colours: pastel red, yellow, blue, purple (in props and decorations). The palette stays within this small, consistent pastel-warm set.

---

## Scale

Everything is sized for a small preschool child. The table is low, child-proportioned. Chairs are small and rounded. Bookshelves are low and accessible. The chalkboard is at child eye level. The nature shelf is reachable. The room itself feels cozy and enclosing, not grand or institutional. Every prop reads as toy-sized and approachable.

---

## Atmosphere

Safe. Warm. Creative. Curious. Joyful. Educational. Cozy. Peaceful. Magical. Every surface reads as touchable and gentle. The mood is consistently warm and inviting — a place where children immediately feel "I want to sit here and learn."

## Consistency Rules

- The room is always a cozy wooden cottage interior — never institutional, never modern.
- The table is always round, wooden, and at the center.
- The chalkboard is always rounded with simple hand-drawn illustrations.
- All furniture is always rounded with no sharp edges.
- Windows are always arched with warm sunlight streaming through.
- The colour palette stays warm, pastel, and consistent.
- Plants are always present and alive.
- No modern technology, no vehicles, no realistic architecture.
- No visual clutter — each zone stays simple and readable.
- No dark or high-contrast lighting.
- No characters, no text, no logos in the canonical reference.

---

## Generation Considerations

The room must be generated as a single connected learning space with soft activity zones, not as separate walled areas — this is the defining trait and the most common failure point. Keep all shapes rounded with no sharp corners anywhere. Keep the colour palette limited to warm pastels and natural wood. Favour a soft, matte, handcrafted material language over any glossy, metallic, or photorealistic rendering. The room's identity — its warmth, its roundness, its handcrafted feel — must be preserved exactly across every generation.

computers, screens), sharp corners anywhere, cold or fluorescent lighting,
cluttered or disorganised shelves, and licensed characters or logos
anywhere.

2. **Colour Test** — recognisable from the warm cream walls, natural wood tones, and morning sunlight alone?
3. **Charm Test** — does it make a child want to sit at the round table and make something?

If any test fails, reject.
