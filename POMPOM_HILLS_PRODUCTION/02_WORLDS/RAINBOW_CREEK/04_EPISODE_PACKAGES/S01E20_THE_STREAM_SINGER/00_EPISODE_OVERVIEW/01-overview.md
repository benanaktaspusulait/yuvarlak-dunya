## Story Summary

Mimo sits by Rainbow Creek, humming a little tune. He hears something — a soft, gurgling sound coming from the water itself. He stops humming and listens. The creek sings in its own way: bubbling over stones, rushing through narrow gaps, and whispering as it flows into a calm pool. Kiko arrives and wonders why Mimo is so still. He holds up a finger — listen. Kiko hears it too: the water's gentle song. Mimo dips his hand in and makes soft splashes that match the creek's rhythm. Kiko finds a flat stone and taps it gently — a bright, clear note rings out. They play together: Mimo with the water, Kiko with the stone. The creek flows on, and their music blends with it perfectly — a natural harmony made of water, stone, and joy.

---

## Micro-Moment (Key Discovery)

Mimo and Kiko creating music together with the creek — Mimo splashing the water in rhythm and Kiko tapping the stone — the moment they realize they can join nature's song instead of just listening.
