## Overview

Music Hill, Pompom Hills'in müziğin yaşadığı tepesi. Rüzgarın ağaçlardan geçerken çıkardığı seslerin bir orkestraya dönüştüğü, her yaprağın bir enstrüman gibi titreştiği yumuşak bir tepe. Bu dünya, doğanın kendi seslerini müziğe dönüştüren bir orman alanıdır — ağaçlar yalnızca yeşil yapraklar değil, tınılar, ritimler ve melodiler üretir. Rüzgar estiğinde yapraklar çan gibi çınlar, dallar birbirine değer ve yumuşak bir melodi doğar. Kuşlar bu ağaçlarda oturur ve onların tınılarına yanıt verir. Su, taş, rüzgar — her şey bir enstrümandır.

Music Hill, Pompom Hills'in doğu kenarında, Little Forest ile Rainbow Creek arasında konumlanmıştır. Tepenin kendisi yumuşak yuvarlak bir yapıya sahiptir — sivri köşe veya keskin eğim yoktur. Üzerinde 3 ila 5 arasında "müzik ağacı" bulunur. Bu ağaçlar, normal ağaçlardan farklı olarak, yuvarlak yaprak kümeleri ve özel bir görünüme sahiptir — yaprakları tını veren çanlar gibi titreşir, dalları rüzgarı yakalar ve melodik sesler üretir. Her ağaç kendine özgü bir tınıya sahiptir: bazıları tiz ve parlak, bazıları kalın ve yumuşak, bazıları ritmik ve dans eden bir karakter taşır.

Bu dünyanın atmosferi sıcaktır, davetkardır ve ritmiktir. Hava canlı ama sakin hissettirir — sanki her an bir melodi başlayabilecekmiş gibi. Gökyüzü açık mavi, çimenler sıcak yeşil ve çiçekler melodi pembesi ile süslenmiştir. Stepping stones hafifçe parlar, sanki müzik çalıyormuş gibi — çocukların bu taşlara bastığında hafif bir tını duyduğu hayal edilir.

Music Hill'in temel amacı, çocuklara "dinleme" sanatını öğretmektir. Burada müzik bir enstrümandan gelmez — rüzgardan, yapraklardan, kuşlardan, taşlardan gelir. Çocuklar bu mekanda kulaklarını açar, farklı sesleri tanımlar, ritim hisseder ve birlikte şarkı söyler. Müziğin her yerde olduğunu keşfederler — sessizlik bile bir sestir.

## Purpose

Müzik keşfi ve yaratıcılık mekanı. Çocuklar burada doğanın seslerini dinler, ritim hisseder, birlikte şarkı söyler ve müziğin her yerde olduğunu keşfeder. Music Hill, çocukların duyusal dünyasını genişleten bir alan yaratır — sadece görmeye değil, duymaya ve hissetmeye odaklanır. Bu mekan, preschool döneminin en önemli gelişim alanlarından biri olan "duyusal farkındalık" eğitimi için tasarlanmıştır.

Müzik keşfi, children's content'te nadir işlenen bir konudur. Çoğu çocuk programı müziği arka plan olarak kullanır — ama Music Hill müziği ÖN PLANA çıkarır. Burada müzik bir dekor değil, karakterdir. Her ağaç bir ses üretir, her rüzgar bir ritim oluşturur, her kuş bir melodi söyler. Çocuklar bu ortamda doğal olarak müziksel algılarını geliştirir — fark etmeden, oynayarak, keşfederek.

Mekan aynı zamanda yaratıcılık için bir alan sunar. Çocuklar ağaçların tınılarını keşfeder, birlikte ritim tutar, yaprakları sallayarak kendi müziklerini yapar. Burada "yanlış" bir ses yoktur — her ses değerli, her ritim kabul edilebilir. Bu felsefe, çocukların yaratıcı özgüvenini destekler ve müziğin bir yarışma değil, bir paylaşım olduğunu öğretir.

## Why This World Exists ⭐

Music Hill, çocuklar için "dinleme" anının somut karşılığıdır. Rüzgar, yapraklar, kuşlar — hepsi birer sestir. Bu mekan çocukların kulaklarını açar ve "sessizlik bile bir sestir" düşüncesini güvenle keşfetmelerini sağlar.

Modern dünya children's content'inde müzik genellikle arka planda, duygusal bir araç olarak kullanılır — mutlu sahnelerde neşeli müzik, üzgün sahnelerde hüzünlü müzik. Ama Music Hill bu paradigmayı değiştirir: burada müzik bir araç değil, konudur. Çocuklar burada müziğin nereden geldiğini keşfeder — ve cevap doğadan gelir.

Her ağaç bir ses üretir, her rüzgar bir ritim yaratır, her kuş bir melodi söyler. Çocuklar bu gerçeği deneyimleyerek öğrenir. Bu deneyim, onların duyusal dünyasını genişletir ve "dışarıda ne var?" sorusunu "içeride ne duyuyorum?" sorusuna dönüştürür. Music Hill, çocukların iç seslerini keşfetmeleri için güvenli bir alan yaratır.

Ayrıca bu dünya, çocukların birlikte müzik yapma deneyimini yaşaması için tasarlanmıştır. Müzik tek başına güzel olabilir, ama birlikte yapıldığında bir bağ yaratır. Music Hill, çocukların birlikte ritim tutmasını, birlikte şarkı söylemesini ve birlikte dinlemesini destekler. Bu deneyim, paylaşma ve işbirliği becerilerini doğal olarak geliştirir.

## Emotional Purpose

### Why does this place exist emotionally?

Music Hill, neşe ve yaratıcılığı temsil eder. Sesler dünyası çocuklara rhythm ve melody hissini öğretir; birlikte müzik yapmak bağlılık duygusunu güçlendirir. Bu mekan, çocukların duygusal dünyasını zenginleştiren bir alan yaratır — sadece duyma değil, hissetme ve paylaşma deneyimi sunar.

Müzik, çocukların duygularını ifade etmesinin en doğal yollarından biridir. Music Hill bu ifade özgürlüğünü destekler — burada her çocuk kendi ritmini bulabilir, kendi melodisini keşfedebilir ve bu keşfi arkadaşlarıyla paylaşabilir. Bu deneyim, çocukların duygusal zekasını ve sosyal bağlarını güçlendirir.

Ayrıca bu mekan, çocukların "sessizlik" ile olan ilişkisini de şekillendirir. Sessizlik burada korkutucu değil, huzurludur — ve sessizliğin içinde bile sesler vardır. Çocuklar bu felsefeyi keşfederek, dünyaya farklı bir perspektiften bakmayı öğrenir.

### How do children feel when they arrive?

Meraklı, neşeli, ritmik. Seslerin büyüsüne kapılmış, kulaklarını dikip dinleyen çocuklar. İlk adımlarını attıklarında, hafif bir rüzgar eser ve ağaçlardan gelen tınılar onları karşılar — bu karşılama anı, children's content'te eşsiz bir duyusal deneyimdir.

Çocuklar genellikle gözlerini kısarak dinler — bu doğal bir tepkidir ve Music Hill bunu destekler. Tınılar yükseldiğinde vücutları ritme uyum sağlar, başları hafifçe sallanır, elleri ritmi takip eder. Bu tepkiler otomatiktir, öğretim gerektirmez — children's content'te en güçlü anlar, zorlamadan doğal olarak oluşan anlardır.

Yeni gelen çocuklar önce sessizce dinler, sonra merakla yaklaşır, en sonunda katılırlar. Bu doğal ilerleme, Music Hill'in davetkar atmosferinin bir yansımasıdır — mekan çocuklar üzerinde baskı yaratmaz, onları davet eder.

### What stories naturally belong here?

Rüzgarla şarkı söyleme, ağaç tınılarını keşfetme, birlikte ritim tutma, kuş seslerini taklit etme, yaprakları çalıp müzik yapma. Bu hikayeler, children's content'te nadir işlenen konularıdır — ve bu yüzden özgün ve değerlidirler.

Her hikaye, çocukların farklı sesleri keşfetmesiyle başlar. Bir ağaç tınısı, bir rüzgar hışırtısı, bir kuş cıvıltısı — her ses bir hikayenin tohumudur. Çocuklar bu sesleri tanımlar, isimlendirir ve birlikte bir melodi oluşturur. Bu süreç, children's content'te "birlikte yaratma" deneyimini sunar.

Ayrıca, "sessiz anlar" da bu dünyanın hikayelerine aittir. Bazen en güçlü müzik, sessizliğin içinde duyulan bir fısıltıdır. Music Hill bu felsefeyi destekler — sessizlik bir hikaye unsuru olarak kullanılır ve çocuklar "sessiz bile olsa, müzik vardır" gerçeğini keşfeder.

### What emotional experiences does this world support?

Neşe · Yaratıcılık · Keşif · Birlikte müzik · Ritim hissi

Bu beş duygusal deneyim, Music Hill'in temel direkleridir. Neşe, seslerin büyüsünden gelir — children's content'te neşe, zorlamadan doğal olarak oluşan bir duygudur ve Music Hill bunu başarır. Yaratıcılık, çocukların kendi ritimlerini ve melodilerini keşfetmelerinden gelir — bu keşif süreci, children's content'te en değerli anlardan biridir.

Keşif, farklı sesleri tanımlama ve isimlendirme sürecinden gelir — children's content'te keşif, çocukların dünyayı anlamlandırma sürecidir. Birlikte müzik, çocukların birlikte ritim tutmasından ve şarkı söylemesinden gelir — bu, paylaşma ve işbirliği duygusunu güçlendirir. Ritim hissi, children's content'te nadir işlenen bir konudur — ama Music Hill bunu başarır.

## Play Philosophy

### Play style

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Open-ended | Sesleri dinle, ritim bul, şarkı kur |
| Imagination | Ağaçlardan enstrüman yapma, rüzgarla beste |
| Cooperation | Birlikte ritim tutma, birlikte şarkı söyleme |
| Observation | Farklı sesleri tanıma ve adlandırma |
| Gentle movement | Ritimle sallanma, yumuşak dans |

Music Hill'in oyun felsefesi, children's content'te nadir işlenen "duyusal keşif" üzerine kuruludur. Açık uçlu oyun, çocukların kendi ritimlerini ve melodilerini keşfetmelerini sağlar — burada "doğru" veya "yanlış" bir müzik yoktur. Her ses değerlidir, her ritim kabul edilebilir.

Hayal gücü, ağaçlardan enstrüman yapma sürecinde ortaya çıkar — children's content'te hayal gücü, çocukların dünyayı yeniden şekillendirmesinin en güçlü yoludur. Bir yaprak bir çana dönüşebilir, bir dal bir flüte, bir taş bir davula —孩子们 bu dönüşümü doğal olarak yapar.

İşbirliği, birlikte ritim tutma ve şarkı söyleme süreçlerinde ortaya çıkar — children's content'te işbirliği, çocukların birlikte bir şey yaratmasıdır. Music Hill bu deneyimi destekler ve çocukların paylaşma becerilerini doğal olarak geliştirir.

### No rule-based games

- Yarış yok — müzik bir yarışma değildir, bir paylaşım alanı yaratır
- Puan yok — her çocuğun katkısı eşit değer taşır
- Rekabet yok — birlikte müzik yapmak rekabeti gereksiz kılar

Music Hill'de oyun, çocukların kendi hızlarında ve kendi yollarında keşfetmesine izin verir. Hiçbir zaman "daha hızlı" veya "daha doğru" bir şekilde yapmaları istenmez. Bu felsefe, children's content'te çocukların duygusal güvenliğini destekler.

### Natural interactions

- Rüzgar estiğinde ağaçları dinleme —孩子们 doğal olarak kulaklarını dikerek dinler
- Kuş seslerini taklit etme —孩子们 kendi seslerini keşfeder
- Yaprakları sallayarak ritim oluşturma —孩子们 kendi ritimlerini yaratır
- Birlikte basit şarkılar söyleme —孩子们 birlikte müzik yapar
- Sessiz anlarda bile sesleri fark etme —孩子们 duyusal farkındalık geliştirir

Bu etkileşimler, children's content'te nadir işlenen konulardır — ve bu yüzden özgün ve değerlidirler. Her etkileşim, çocukların duyusal dünyasını genişletir ve "dışarıda ne var?" sorusunu "içeride ne duyuyorum?" sorusuna dönüştürür.

## Visual Identity

### General

Music Hill, yumuşak yuvarlak bir tepedir — sivri köşe veya keskin eğim yoktur. Tepenin üzerinde 3 ila 5 arasında müzik ağacı bulunur. Bu ağaçlar, normal ağaçlardan farklı olarak, yuvarlak yaprak kümeleri ve özel bir görünüme sahiptir. ## Camera Identity

> The permanent camera standard for Music Hill. All shots — regardless of story — must follow this identity.

### Default Lens

35mm–50mm equivalent. Wide enough to show the trees and hill together. Narrow enough to keep the world intimate and warm. Never wider than 28mm (distorts the trees). Never tighter than 50mm (loses the hill context).

### Child Eye Level

All shots default to 0.70–1.10 m camera height — the natural eye level of a standing preschool child. This makes the trees feel tall and musical, and the hill feel safe rather than intimidating.

| Height | When Used |
|--------|-----------|
| 1.10 m | Standing character, wide and establishing shots |
| 0.85 m | Walking character, medium shots among trees |
| 0.70 m | Sitting character, intimate listening shots |
| 0.60 m | Close-up object or stepping stone detail |
| POV | Leaf chime listening — looking up at branches |

### Movement Philosophy

The camera on Music Hill moves like a gentle rhythm — slow, steady, breathing. Nothing is sudden. Nothing cuts hard. The world is rhythmic and the camera respects that rhythm.

### Allowed Movements

| Movement | Speed | When |
|----------|-------|------|
| Slow pan | Very slow — 3–5 seconds across | Establishing, environment reveal |
| Gentle tilt up | 2–3 seconds | Tree canopy reveal, leaf chime focus |
| Slow push in | Very slow — 4+ seconds | Emotional close moment, listening |
| Static hold | — | Dialogue, observation, silence |

### Forbidden Movements

```
✗ Fast pan or whip pan
✗ Handheld shake or bounce
✗ Zoom in or zoom out
✗ Crane drop or fast vertical movement
✗ 360° rotation
✗ Drone-style fly-over
```

### Framing Consistency

- Subject is never placed in the centre of frame — slightly left or right of centre
- Trees always visible in background — never cropped out of frame
- Path always visible in wide shots — provides visual anchor
- Characters are always framed with trees behind them — trees dominate

### Transition Rules

- Scene openings: always begin with environment wide shot before introducing character
- Scene closings: always end wider than the previous shot — pull back to hill
- Between shots: cut or slow dissolve only — no wipe, no flash

## Lighting Identity

> The canonical lighting system for Music Hill. Must be consistent across every scene in every episode that uses this world.

### Light Direction

Primary light comes from the upper left in all standard daytime scenes. This is the canonical sun position for Music Hill and must not change between shots in the same scene.

| Time | Direction | Quality |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Morning | Upper left, warm | Softest — golden, gentle |
| Midday | Top centre, neutral | Brightest — even, clean |
| Evening | Upper left, golden-orange | Warmest — long soft shadows |
| Night | Top, cool moonlight | Softest — pale blue-white |

### Softness

All light on Music Hill is soft. There are no hard shadows, no sharp shadow edges, no spotlight effects. The light feels like it is being filtered through a thin layer of leaf canopy at all times — bright but never harsh. This softness creates a warm, inviting atmosphere that supports the world's musical identity.

### Ambient Fill

A gentle ambient fill always lifts the shadow side of every object and character. No side of any subject falls into true darkness. The darkest shadow value on Music Hill is approximately 70% of the highlight value — never lower. This ensures that every tree, every leaf cluster, every flower is always visible and readable.

### Shadow Density

| Shadow Type | Density | Notes |
|-------------|---------|-------|
| Character shadow on grass | Very soft, 20–30% opacity | Short — never longer than character height |
| Tree shadow on grass | Soft, 30–40% opacity | Natural tree shadow, not dramatic |
| Leaf shadow detail | Barely visible | Ground stays bright and readable |
| Cloud shadow on hill | None — clouds do not cast shadows on the hill | This keeps the ground clean and safe |

### Exposure Behaviour

The world is always slightly bright — not overexposed, but lifted. Midtones are open and readable. Nothing is crushed in the darks. Highlights are soft — never blown. This exposure behaviour ensures that the musical trees' leaf clusters are always readable and their colours are always vibrant.

### Continuity Rules

- Light direction must not change between shots in the same scene
- If a scene begins in morning light, every shot in that scene stays in morning light
- Cloud shadows are not cast on the ground — they exist only in the sky
- Character lighting must match environment lighting — no isolated character illumination
- Tree leaf clusters must always be lit consistently — same colour, same brightness

## Colour Identity

> The permanent colour profile for Music Hill. Every future shot must preserve this profile unless the story explicitly requires a shift (e.g. a deliberate sunset or night scene).

### White Balance

Warm daylight — approximately 5500K. Slightly warm, never cool or blue-shifted in daytime scenes. This warmth supports the musical, inviting atmosphere of the world.

### Exposure

+0.3 to +0.5 stops above neutral. The world is bright, open, and lifted. This exposure ensures that the musical green of the leaves and the melody pink of the flowers are always vibrant and readable.

### Contrast

Low contrast. The distance between the brightest and darkest values is narrow. Everything reads clearly. Nothing disappears into shadow. This low contrast ensures that the world feels safe, warm, and inviting.

### Saturation

Medium-low. Colours are present and readable but never vivid or punchy. The palette feels hand-painted, not digital. This saturation level ensures that the world feels natural and organic, not synthetic.

## Environmental Sound Identity

### Wind

| Tone | Low, smooth, slightly breathy — not whistling |

### Tree Chimes

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Presence | Always present when wind blows — the defining sound of Music Hill |
| Tone | Soft, warm, melodic — like distant bells or gentle xylophone |
| Frequency | Continuous during wind — varies with wind intensity |
| Volume | Medium — the primary melodic element of the soundscape |

### Bird Frequency

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Presence | Always present but distant — birds are in the trees, not on the ground |
| Call type | Single melodic calls, not flocks or chattering |
| Frequency | One call every 8–12 seconds on average |
| Volume | Low — never louder than the wind layer |

### Ambience Level

The total ambient sound of Music Hill is moderate. It should feel like a living, breathing musical space. The wind, chimes, and birds together sit at approximately 30–40% of the maximum audio level. Dialogue always sits clearly above ambience. The soundscape should never be silent — there is always a gentle hum of nature.

### Silence Ratio

Silence — or near-silence with only the faintest wind — is a valid and intentional sound state for Music Hill. It should be used at emotional peaks: the moment a character first hears a tree chime, the moment a character stops to listen, the moment a question is asked and not yet answered. Silence is not the absence of music — it is the space between notes.

Music Hill is a canonical reusable world. Every time it appears — regardless of episode, regardless of characters, regardless of story — it must feel like exactly the same place.

The world has one defining quality: it belongs to sound. More rhythm than silence. More melody than noise. More warmth than distance. Characters come here to listen, discover, create, and share. The hill itself is the simplest possible form — a smooth dome of soft green grass. Everything else is music.

For production, Music Hill is low-complexity and high-consistency. It has few props, a defined colour palette, a locked silhouette, and a clear camera language. This makes it one of the fastest worlds to generate reliably — but only if the identity rules are respected on every frame.

The single most common failure point is the tree appearance becoming normal (non-musical) trees. Always verify tree identity first. The second most common failure is modern objects appearing in frame. Music Hill has no infrastructure — any modern element immediately breaks the world's identity.

Every future episode using Music Hill should load the Hero View as its master reference, verify the Colour Identity before accepting any frame, and run the Production QA checklist before signing off on any shot. The world does not change. Only the story does.

## World Navigation

> The canonical movement flow through Music Hill. Every episode using this world should follow this navigation unless the story explicitly requires a variation.

### Entry Point

Characters always enter Music Hill from the lower hillside — approaching from the direction of Little Forest or Rainbow Creek. They are never already at the summit when a scene begins. Arrival is part of the world experience.

### Canonical Transition Sequence

```
1. Entry — characters appear at the base of the visible slope
       ↓
2. Path Discovery — characters notice the winding path and stepping stones
       ↓
3. Climb Path — characters walk upward along the path
       ↓
4. Tree Discovery — characters hear the first tree chimes
       ↓
5. Summit Arrival — characters reach the top, trees surround them
       ↓
6. Listening Area — characters settle among the trees
       ↓
7. Natural Exit — characters descend back down the same path
```

### Path Discovery

The path discovery is a canonical emotional beat. As characters approach the hill, they notice the winding path and the stepping stones. The camera typically follows from behind or from the side. This moment sets up the journey — children see the path and want to follow it.

### Tree Discovery

Tree discovery is the first musical moment. As characters climb, they begin to hear the tree chimes — soft, melodic, inviting. The camera reveals the trees gradually — first one, then two, then the cluster at the summit. This reveal should never be cut short or skipped.

### Summit Arrival

Summit arrival is a canonical emotional beat. As characters reach the top, the trees surround them and the music intensifies — the chimes become louder, the wind harp plays, the birds join in. This is the visual and auditory payoff. Camera widens to reveal the full tree cluster. This moment should never be cut short or skipped.

### Listening Area

The listening area is the summit plateau among the trees. Characters sit near the trees, stand at the edge looking outward, or walk among the stepping stones. This is where the episode's main story content happens — listening, discovering, creating.

### Natural Exit

Characters exit the same way they entered — descending the hillside path. The exit is always calm and unhurried. A closing wide shot of the hill after characters have left (or are small silhouettes descending) is a canonical closing image.

### Canonical vs. Optional Transitions

| Transition | Status | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Entry → Path → Climb → Summit | Canonical | Use in every episode that visits Music Hill |
| Summit → Listening → Exit | Canonical | Standard story structure |
| Starting at summit with no climb | Optional | Only when the climb is shown in a previous episode of a multi-part story |
| Teleporting between worlds without a transition shot | Forbidden | Always show the approach |

## View Transition Rules

> How camera views should progress during a standard Music Hill episode. This is the canonical shot progression.

### Standard Shot Sequence

Default episode flow — alternative openings are permitted when story continuity requires them.

```
Environment wide (establish — no character)
    ↓
Approach shot (character on hillside, moving upward)
    ↓
Tree discovery (first chimes heard)
    ↓
Summit shot (arrival + main story)
    ↓
Close shot (emotional peak — face, leaf, stepping stone)
    ↓
Closing wide (exit or tree hold)
```

### When to Use Each Shot Type

| Shot Type | When | Notes |
|-----------|------|-------|
| Environment wide | Scene opening — before any character appears | Always first |
| Approach shot | Character on hillside, moving upward | Can be skipped when continuity permits |
| Tree discovery | First chimes heard — trees revealed | Key emotional beat |
| Summit shot | Character at the top — main story content | Always present |
| Close shot | Emotional peak | Optional but recommended |
| Closing wide | Scene end — exit or silence | Always end wider than the previous shot |

### Transition Guidance

- **Environment wide → Approach shot:** Cut or slow dissolve. Never jump straight to the summit without the climb.
- **Approach → Tree discovery:** The camera reveals trees gradually — this is a key emotional transition — give it time.
- **Tree discovery → Summit:** The tilt-up or widen as the summit is crested. The music intensifies. This is the payoff.
- **Summit → Close:** Slow push in or direct cut to close-up. The detail should feel like a natural zoom of attention, not a hard jump.
- **Close → Closing wide:** Pull back slowly or cut to wide. Never end on a close shot — always return to the world.
- **Closing wide:** Let the world breathe before cutting.

### Transitions to Avoid

```
✗ Opening directly on a character close-up — always establish the world first
✗ Cutting from environment wide to close shot without a summit shot in between
✗ Ending on a medium or close shot — always close wider
✗ Two consecutive close shots without a wider shot between them
✗ Sudden cut from the summit to a completely different world with no transition
✗ Skipping the tree discovery moment — children need to hear the first chimes
```

## Character Occupancy

> Recommended character-to-frame ratios for Music Hill. The world must remain the primary visual element — characters inhabit it, not dominate it.

### Wide Shots

Characters occupy no more than 10–15% of the total frame area. They appear small against the hill and trees. Two characters side by side should feel welcomed by the musical landscape around them. This is intentional — the musical trees are the stars of this world.

| Shot Type | Character Frame % | Trees Visible | Notes |
|-----------|:-----------------:|:-------------:|-------|
| Hero View | 0% | 3–5 | No character — pure environment |
| Wide establishing | 8–12% | 3–5 | Character small on hill |
| Wide with two characters | 12–15% | 3–5 | Both characters visible full-body |

### Medium Shots

Characters occupy 30–40% of the frame. Trees remain visible above and/or behind. The path, stepping stones, or flowers are visible in the lower frame. Characters are never the only thing in the frame.

| Shot Type | Character Frame % | Trees Visible | Notes |
|-----------|:-----------------:|:-------------:|-------|
| Single character medium | 30–35% | 1–2 | Trees behind character always |
| Two character medium | 35–40% | 1–2 | Trees visible between characters |

### Close Shots

Characters occupy 60–75% of the frame. Even in close shots, a leaf cluster or tree trunk is always visible. A close-up of a face listening should still show the musical leaves as background — never a blurred or neutral background.

| Shot Type | Character Frame % | Tree Visible | Notes |
|-----------|:-----------------:|:------------:|-------|
| Face close-up | 65–75% | 1 | Leaves as background, not bokeh |
| Hand or stepping stone detail | 50–60% | — | Grass visible, tree glimpsed |

### Emotional Moments

At peak emotional moments — first chime, discovery, rhythm creation — the camera pulls back rather than pushing in. The character becomes smaller, the trees become larger. This is Music Hill's signature emotional move: intimacy through scale contrast, not through closeness.

## Typical Episode Usage

### 30-Second Story

**Structure:** Arrive → Single sound → Exit

```

5–12s   Character arrives at hill, hears first chime
12–24s  One simple sound discovery or listening moment
24–30s  Closing wide — character looks out or begins descent
```

**Fits this format:**
- First visit to Music Hill (character hears tree chimes for the first time)
- A simple question asked and wondered about ("Where does that sound come from?")
- A quiet moment between two characters listening together

### 60-Second Story

**Structure:** Arrive → Discover → Create → Exit

```
0–5s    Environment wide — establish
5–12s   Climb and arrival
12–40s  Main story content — discover sounds, create rhythm (2–3 shots + close shot)
40–52s  Quiet reflection or dialogue
52–60s  Closing wide — exit or tree hold
```

**Fits this format:**
- Discovering tree chimes and creating a rhythm together (S01E24)

- Two characters sharing a musical moment

### 90-Second Story

**Structure:** Arrive → Discover → Create → Collaborate → Emotional peak → Exit

```
0–5s    Environment wide
5–15s   Climb (longer, more exploratory)
15–50s  Main story — multiple beats, tree shots and close shots alternating
50–70s  Emotional peak — close shot, then pull back to wide
70–85s  Resolution — characters settled, listening together
85–90s  Closing wide — world returns to stillness
```

**Fits this format:**
- A full musical journey — discovering multiple sounds and creating together

### Story Types That Naturally Fit Music Hill

| Story Type | Why It Fits |
|------------|------------|

| Rhythm creation | Children naturally clap and tap — the world provides the beat |
| Listening together | Two characters sharing a moment of attention |
| First-time arrival | The tree discovery is inherently a story beat |
| Creative expression | Children create music from natural sounds |

### Story Types That Do Not Fit Music Hill

```
✗ High-energy action or chase scenes — the world is rhythmic, not fast
✗ Large group scenes (more than 3 characters) — the space is intimate
✗ Stories that require props, buildings, or tools
✗ Stories that need enclosed spaces
```

## Common Generation Failures

> The most frequent AI generation problems when producing Music Hill. Each entry explains how to recognise it, why it matters, and how to fix it.

### Failure 01 — Trees Look Normal (Non-Musical)

**How to recognise:** The trees look like regular deciduous trees — pointed leaves, normal branches, no round leaf clusters. They do not look like they could produce sound.

**Why it breaks identity:** Music Hill's defining quality is the musical trees. When trees look normal, the world loses its musical identity. It becomes just a hill with trees, not a place where nature makes music.

**How to correct:** Regenerate with explicit instruction: music trees must have round, plump leaf clusters in musical green #66BB6A, trunks in soft brown #A1887F. The leaf clusters should look like chimes or bells. Reject any frame where trees look like normal deciduous trees.

### Failure 02 — Modern Objects in Frame

**How to recognise:** A fence, path (paved), road, building, utility pole, speaker, microphone, or any man-made structure appears anywhere in the frame — including the background.

**Why it breaks identity:** Music Hill has no infrastructure. Any modern object immediately pulls the viewer out of the Pompom Hills world and signals that the world has not been generated correctly.

**How to correct:** Regenerate with explicit instruction: no fences, no paved paths, no buildings, no modern objects anywhere in frame. Check the full frame including background horizon before accepting.

### Failure 03 — Wrong Tree Count

**How to recognise:** The sky is completely empty of trees (zero trees) or packed with many small trees. Either extreme breaks the canonical identity.

**Why it breaks identity:** Zero trees makes Music Hill meaningless — the defining feature of the world is absent. Too many trees makes the world busy and visually noisy, breaking the calm musical atmosphere.

**How to correct:** Regenerate with explicit instruction: exactly 3–5 music trees, different heights, with round leaf clusters. Reject any frame with zero, one, or six or more trees.

### Failure 04 — Oversaturated or Wrong Grass Colour

**How to recognise:** The grass appears vivid emerald green, yellow-green, olive, or brown. It does not match the warm green (#81C784) of the canonical palette.

**Why it breaks identity:** Grass colour is a primary continuity marker. If it shifts between frames or episodes, the world no longer reads as the same place. Oversaturation also breaks the pastel feel of the entire Pompom Hills visual language.

**How to correct:** Reduce saturation, warm the green slightly, and lighten toward #81C784. Regenerate with explicit colour instruction if needed. Always verify grass colour against the canonical hex value before accepting.

### Failure 05 — Hill Sharp or Angular

**How to recognise:** The hill outline has visible bumps, ridges, or a pointed peak. The smooth dome silhouette becomes irregular.

**Why it breaks identity:** The smooth dome is the hill's visual identity. Irregularity makes the hill look like a mountain or a rocky terrain — both wrong for Pompom Hills. It also breaks visual continuity with other Music Hill frames.

**How to correct:** Regenerate with explicit instruction: hill must be a single smooth rounded dome, no peaks, no ridges, no bumps in the silhouette. Reject any frame where the dome curve is interrupted.

### Failure 06 — Flowers in Rows

**How to recognise:** The melody pink flowers are arranged in straight lines, geometric patterns, or uniform clusters. They look planted rather than naturally scattered.

**Why it breaks identity:** Music Hill's flowers are natural and wild — they grow where seeds fall. Ordered flowers suggest a garden or park, not a wild musical hill. This breaks the natural, organic feel of the world.

**How to correct:** Regenerate with explicit instruction: flowers must be scattered naturally, never in rows or patterns. Flowers should appear at tree bases and along the path in organic clusters of 2–5.

### Failure 07 — Path Too Straight

**How to recognise:** The path runs in a straight line from bottom to top without curves or natural variation.

**Why it breaks identity:** A straight path suggests construction and human design. Music Hill's path is natural and organic — it winds gently up the hillside, following the natural contours of the land.

**How to correct:** Regenerate with explicit instruction: path must wind gently, with natural curves. Never straight, never geometric. The path should feel like it was created by many small feet over time.

### Failure 08 — Hero View Silhouette Lost

**How to recognise:** The generated hill has a different shape from the Canon Hero View — narrower base, more pointed top, asymmetrical slope, or a plateau instead of a dome. On first glance the world is not immediately recognisable as Music Hill.

**Why it breaks identity:** Music Hill's visual identity is its silhouette. A viewer who has seen the Canon Hero View must recognise every subsequent frame as the same world. If the silhouette changes, world continuity breaks across episodes — even if all other rules are followed.

**How to correct:** Reject without evaluating other elements. Load `01-hero-view.png` as the primary reference and regenerate. The hill silhouette check is the first and fastest rejection test.

## Video Generation Rules

> The reusable standard for animated scenes on Music Hill. These rules ensure the world feels identical in both still images and animated video.

### Leaf Movement

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Type | Gentle sway, low amplitude — leaf clusters move, trunks stay still |
| Speed | One full sway cycle every 3–4 seconds |
| Direction | Follows wind direction — consistent across the frame |
| Pattern | Gentle ripple across the tree canopy, not chaotic or random |
| Amplitude | Leaf clusters move no more than 15% of their size — subtle, not wild |

### Tree Movement

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Type | Barely perceptible sway — the tree itself breathes |
| Speed | One full cycle every 5–7 seconds |
| Amplitude | Trunk moves less than 5% — almost invisible |
| Consistency | All trees sway in the same direction at the same time |

### Wind Intensity

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Visual intensity | Low — hair moves gently, clothing barely stirs |
| Hair movement | Slow, smooth sweep — one direction, not flapping |
| Consistency | Wind direction and intensity do not change within a scene |
| Gusting | No gusts — wind is continuous and even |

### Camera Movement Speed

| Movement Type | Speed Rule |
|---------------|-----------|
| Pan | No faster than 5% of frame width per second |
| Tilt | No faster than 4% of frame height per second |
| Push in | No faster than 2% of subject size increase per second |
| Pull back | Same speed as push in — symmetrical |
| Static hold | Minimum 3 seconds before any movement begins |

All camera movement must feel as if the camera is breathing rather than moving. If the movement is perceptible as "the camera is moving," it is too fast.

### Dialogue Pacing

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Cut timing | Never cut during a line — always cut between lines |
| Pause after line | Minimum 1 second of silence before the next line begins |
| Silence as dialogue | Silence after a sound discovery is intentional — hold for 2–3 seconds |
| Reaction shots | Allow 1.5–2 seconds for a character to react before cutting away |

The pace of dialogue on Music Hill is slower than other worlds. The environment demands it. Lines are shorter. Pauses are longer. The music fills the silence.

### Environmental Rhythm

Music Hill has a single ambient rhythm: gentle, steady, and musical. Every moving element follows this rhythm:

| Element | Rhythm |
|---------|--------|
| Leaf sway | 3–4 second cycle across visible motion |
| Tree breath | 5–7 second cycle — almost invisible |
| Wind audio | Rises slightly every 8–10 seconds, falls back gently |
| Bird call | Once every 8–12 seconds — irregular but not surprising |
| Camera | Moves on the same rhythm as the leaves — never fights it |

When all elements are in rhythm, Music Hill feels alive but still. When any element breaks rhythm — a sudden camera move, a fast leaf, a sharp sound — the world loses its identity. Rhythm is the final check before approving any animated scene.

### Still Image vs. Video Consistency

Every frame from an animated scene must pass the same Production QA as a still image. Animation does not exempt a frame from identity checks. The canonical colour palette, tree identity, hill silhouette, and grass colour apply equally to every individual frame of any animated sequence.
