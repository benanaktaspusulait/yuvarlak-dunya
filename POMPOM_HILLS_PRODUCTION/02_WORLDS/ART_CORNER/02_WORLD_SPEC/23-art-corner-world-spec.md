---

## Purpose

Art Corner is a small, round, open-air art studio that functions as the
creative heart of Pompom Hills. It is where art, expression, and colour
come alive through the simple joy of painting and discovery.

---

## Immutable Identity

The following elements define the canonical identity of Art Corner and must never change:

- Open-air art studio with round canvases
- Round brushes with thick handles
- Colourful paint palettes in all colours
- Round wooden tables
- Display wall with artworks
- Warm natural sunlight
- Soft, handcrafted, toy-like materials
- No sharp corners anywhere

---

## Visual Identity

A small, fully round open-air studio with no rectangles, no straight edges,
no sharp corners anywhere. Soft matte surfaces give every element a
handcrafted, toy-like quality. The palette is bright and colourful, centred
on rainbow paints with warm wood tones. The overall feeling is a "living toy
studio" — approachable, creative, and visually simple enough for a preschool
audience to read instantly.

---

## World Layout

The world reads as one continuous creative space rather than a set of separate
areas. The entrance leads into the studio, which flows naturally from painting
zone to colour mixing area to display wall to supply station to inspiration
corner. All zones are open and connected without barriers between them.

---

## Spatial Relationships

- The studio entrance is visible from the path.
- The painting zone can be seen from the entrance.
- The colour mixing area is visible from the painting zone.
- The display wall is visible from the mixing area.
- The supply station connects to all areas.
- The inspiration corner is visible from the supply station but feels separate.

---

## Playable Areas

**Studio Entrance** — Purpose: welcoming approach to the studio. Visual identity: colourful signs, canvases, welcoming palette. Important props: signs, canvas. Typical use: arrival, first brush stroke.

**Painting Zone** — Purpose: painting and creating zone. Visual identity: white canvases on round easels, warm sunlight. Important props: canvases, easels. Typical use: painting, expression.

**Colour Mixing Area** — Purpose: mixing and discovering colours. Visual identity: paint cups, mixing palettes, rainbow colours. Important props: paint cups, palettes. Typical use: colour discovery, experimentation.

**Display Wall** — Purpose: showcasing artworks. Visual identity: large round frame, colourful artworks. Important props: wall, frames. Typical use: pride, sharing.

**Supply Station** — Purpose: storing and sharing materials. Visual identity: organized brushes, paints, canvases. Important props: brushes, paints. Typical use: preparation, sharing.

**Inspiration Corner** — Purpose: rest and inspiration. Visual identity: soft cushions, nature view. Important props: cushions, view. Typical use: resting, imagining.

---

## Materials

Brushes: soft, round-tipped, thick handles. Paints: soft, colourful, non-toxic. Canvases: smooth, white, round frames. Tables: smooth matte wood, rounded. Palettes: soft, round, colourful. No sharp surfaces, no glass, no metal anywhere. Everything reads as handcrafted and safe.

---

## Lighting

Primary light is warm natural sunlight. Morning brings soft golden light;
midday is bright and even; evening turns golden-orange with longer soft
shadows; night the studio rests. Studio lighting is always natural daylight.
Shadows are always soft — never hard, never dark.

---

## Colour Palette

Paints: all rainbow colours (red, yellow, blue, green, purple, orange, pink).
Wood: warm brown. Canvas: white. Tables: warm wood. The palette stays within
this bright, creative set across every zone.

---

## Scale

Everything is sized for a small preschool child. Canvases are child-sized,
low on easels. Tables are child-height. Brushes are thick and easy to grip.
The studio feels spacious enough to create but intimate enough to feel safe.

---

## Atmosphere

Bright. Soft. Safe. Creative. Every surface reads as handcrafted and gentle.
The mood is always inspiring and inviting — full of colour and gentle
movement. The studio never feels empty or quiet during the day.

---

## Consistency Rules

- The studio is always open-air and round — never enclosed, never sharp-edged.
- Paints stay rainbow-coloured across every generation.
- Brushes stay round-tipped and soft.
- Canvases stay white and round.
- No sharp corners, no dirty paint, no dull colours.
- No dark or high-contrast lighting.
- The studio's core shape, colours, and proportions are never redesigned or reinterpreted.

---

## Generation Considerations

The studio must be generated as a single bright creative space with
colourful materials, not as a dark or messy art room — this is the defining
trait of this world and the most common failure point to watch for. Keep all
shapes rounded with no sharp corners anywhere. Keep the colour palette
limited to the bright creative set defined above. Favor a soft, matte,
handcrafted material language over any glossy, metallic, or realistic
rendering. Treat the studio's shape, colours, and proportions as a fixed
identity to preserve exactly, not to reinterpret, across every generation.

## Visual Richness Layer

> See `POMPOM_HILLS_PRODUCTION/00_GLOBAL_RULES/WORLD_STANDARDS/GLOBAL_ENVIRONMENT_STANDARD.md` § Visual Richness Principle.
> Art Corner must never be reduced to "some easels outside."

This layer protects Art Corner from becoming too plain. Keep the round
open-air studio silhouette readable and the bright, colourful creative
identity clear — add only the canon-approved charm details below. Do not add
random objects. Do not remove the joyful, creative-studio emotional
identity.

**Allowed charm details:** AC-EASEL-01, the round wooden easel holding a
canvas mid-painting, is the primary landmark and must be visible as the
centrepiece of every establishing shot. The round wooden display wall
showing finished, colourful artwork adds landmark charm nearby. AC-PAINT-01,
the vividly colourful paint palette with many hues visible, and soft
paint-splash patterns on the round wooden tables add surface charm. A
cluster of round-handled paintbrushes resting in a water cup and small
stacks of blank canvases waiting nearby are the central reusable small
props. Gentle motion — a soft drip or swirl of wet paint on the palette and
the sway of nearby leaves providing dappled natural light — keeps the world
feeling alive without adding clutter.

**Forbidden clutter:** empty or blank canvases with no visible art, a dull
or muted or monochrome palette, messy or dirty-looking paint spills,
sharp-edged easels or furniture, modern art tools such as spray cans or
digital tablets, cluttered disorganised supply piles, and dark or gloomy
lighting.
