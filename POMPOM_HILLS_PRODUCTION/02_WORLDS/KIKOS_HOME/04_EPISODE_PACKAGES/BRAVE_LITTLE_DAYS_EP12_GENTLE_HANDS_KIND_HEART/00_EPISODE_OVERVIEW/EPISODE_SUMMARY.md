# EP12 — Episode Summary

## "Gentle Hands, Kind Heart"

**Series:** Brave Little Days
**Sub-series:** Pompom Hills Preschool Animation
**Duration:** 120 seconds (8 shots × 15 seconds)
**Characters:** Kiko, Mimi
**Location:** Kiko's Home — Garden

---

## Logline

Kiko discovers a butterfly in the garden and learns that reaching out gently — not grabbing eagerly — is the way to connect with living things.

---

## Story Summary

In a warm garden filled with soft light and pastel flowers, Kiko notices a beautiful butterfly resting on a round pink flower. Her first instinct is excitement — she reaches out eagerly, wanting to touch it right away. But her friend Mimi, a soft-blue rabbit, steps in with calm wisdom: "Soft and gentle."

Kiko listens. She slows down. She extends one careful finger toward the butterfly. And then — magic. The butterfly lifts from the flower and lands gently on Kiko's fingertip. Pure amazement.

"It likes you!" Mimi says, beaming with joy. Kiko, transfixed, whispers: "So beautiful."

The butterfly rests peacefully on Kiko's finger. Kiko whispers the lesson back: "Soft and gentle." The phrase she learned from Mimi is now hers.

Finally, the butterfly lifts off and flies gracefully into the soft sky. Kiko watches it go with a peaceful smile and says: "Gentle hands, kind heart."

---

## Theme

Gentle touch with living things. Learning that kindness opens doors that force cannot.

---

## Safety Notes

- No hurting the butterfly. No rough touch shown.
- Kiko's "rough" is eagerness — never actual grabbing or harm.
- Butterfly is unharmed throughout. It flies away freely at the end.
- Gentle from the very start. The "rough" moment is purely about speed/eagerness.
