# Repeated Phrase Map — EP13

## Primary Repeated Phrase
**"It's okay to feel."**

## Occurrences

### Occurrence 1 — Shot 03
**Full line:** "It's okay to feel sad, Kiko."
**Speaker:** Mimi
**Context:** Validation moment after Kiko names her sadness
**Tone:** Gentle, reassuring
**Visual:** Mimi close to Kiko, empathetic expression
**Purpose:** Validate the specific emotion (sadness)

### Occurrence 2 — Shot 08
**Full line:** "It's okay to feel!"
**Speaker:** Both (Kiko and Mimi together)
**Context:** Final celebration, addressing the audience
**Tone:** Joyful, confident
**Visual:** Both facing camera, waving
**Purpose:** Generalize the lesson — all feelings are okay

## Phrase Evolution

```
Shot 03: "It's okay to feel SAD" (specific)
    ↓
Shot 08: "It's okay to feel" (universal)
```

The phrase evolves from specific (sadness) to universal (all emotions), teaching children that the validation applies to any feeling they might have.

## Usage Pattern

| Shot | Phrase Form | Who Says It | Direction |
|------|-------------|-------------|-----------|
| 03 | "It's okay to feel sad." | Mimi | To Kiko |
| 08 | "It's okay to feel." | Both | To audience |

## Thematic Function
1. **Comfort** (Shot 03): Immediate relief for Kiko's current emotion
2. **Empowerment** (Shot 08): Children internalize the message for future use

## Production Notes
- Occurrence 1 is intimate, personal
- Occurrence 2 is celebratory, universal
- Voice delivery should differ: gentle vs. joyful
- The simplified phrase in Shot 08 is easier for children to remember and repeat
