## Story Summary

Kiko wants to go outside but struggles to put on her coat. She tries and fails, expressing mild frustration. Mimi offers patient support, guiding Kiko step by step — one arm, then the other, then click-click the snap. Kiko stands cozy and proud, declaring "I can do it myself!" The episode teaches children that asking for help is okay, and with patience, they can learn to do things independently.

## Learning Point

**Asking for help is brave, and with patience, you can learn to do things yourself.**

The episode demonstrates that:
- It's okay to feel frustrated when something is hard
- Asking for help is not weakness — it's wisdom
- Step-by-step guidance leads to success
- Independence is earned through practice and patience
