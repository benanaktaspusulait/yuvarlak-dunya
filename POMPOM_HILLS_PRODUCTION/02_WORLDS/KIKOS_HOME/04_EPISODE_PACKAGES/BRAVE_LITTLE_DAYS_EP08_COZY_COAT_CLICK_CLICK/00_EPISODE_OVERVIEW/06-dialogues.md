## Dialogue Rules

### Timing Rules
1. **No overlap**: Lines don't overlap between characters
2. **Breathing room**: 0.2-0.4s gap between lines
3. **First line timing**: Start after 0.4s (allow visual establishment)
4. **Last line timing**: End before 14s (leave room for visual ending)

### Voice Rules
1. **Kiko**: Bright, childlike, higher pitch. Frustration without whining. Excitement without screaming.
2. **Mimi**: Soft, warm, reassuring. Never rushed. Gentle teacher tone.
3. **Volume**: Moderate — not whispering, not shouting
4. **Pace**: Slow to moderate — preschool comprehension speed

### Language Rules
1. **Vocabulary**: Simple, everyday words
2. **Sentence length**: Short (2-5 words max)
3. **Clarity**: Every word must be clearly audible
4. **Repetition**: The repeated phrase anchors the lesson

### Emotional Rules
1. **Frustration**: Mild, not distressing. Kiko is trying, not giving up.
2. **Encouragement**: Warm, not patronizing. Mimi believes in Kiko.
3. **Success**: Genuine, not exaggerated. Real accomplishment.
4. **Pride**: Confident, not arrogant. Healthy self-esteem.

Shot 01: Mimi sets up the situation

## Voice Recording Notes

### Kiko Voice
- **Actor direction**: Play a 3-4 year old who is frustrated but not upset
- **Key emotion**: Determination mixed with mild helplessness
- **Avoid**: Whining, crying, yelling, baby talk
- **Aim**: Authentic child voice, not performed cuteness

### Mimi Voice
- **Actor direction**: Patient teacher, warm friend, gentle guide
- **Key emotion**: Unconditional support and belief in Kiko
- **Avoid**: Patronizing, lecturing, impatient, distant
- **Aim**: The voice of a loving, patient caregiver
