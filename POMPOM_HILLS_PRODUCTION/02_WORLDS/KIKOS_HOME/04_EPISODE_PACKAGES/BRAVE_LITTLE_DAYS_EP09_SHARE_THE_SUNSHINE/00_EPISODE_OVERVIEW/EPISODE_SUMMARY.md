### Story Summary

Kiko is playing happily alone with her favourite soft ball in the living area. Her friend Arda arrives and asks to play. Kiko hesitates — "It's mine." She doesn't want to share. Arda waits patiently without pushing. Mimi the butterfly appears briefly and gently suggests, "What if you play together?" Kiko considers, then tries rolling the ball to Arda. He catches it and rolls it back. They discover the joy of playing together — rolling the ball back and forth, laughing. Arda says, "This is more fun together!" Kiko agrees and declares, "Together is more fun!" Both wave goodbye.

### Emotion Arc

```
Happiness → Hesitation → Reflection → Courage → Joy → Connection → Shared Joy → Celebration
```

### Learning Point

Sharing turns a solo activity into a shared joy. When you include others, you don't lose — you gain a friend and more fun.

### Repeated Phrase

"Together is more fun!" — appears 3-4 times across the episode, building from variation to full declaration.
