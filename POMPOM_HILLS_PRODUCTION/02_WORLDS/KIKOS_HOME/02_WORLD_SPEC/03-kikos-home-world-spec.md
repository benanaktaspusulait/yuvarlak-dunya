---

## Purpose

Kiko's Home is a small rounded pink cottage that functions as the safe home
base of Pompom Hills. It is the world's most familiar location — where most
adventures begin and end.

---

## Immutable Identity

The following elements define the canonical identity of Kiko's Home and must never change:

- Round coral-pink cottage
- Rounded dark-pink dome roof
- White arched front door
- Two round windows
- One continuous open interior
- Rounded furniture throughout
- Pastel, handcrafted materials
- Warm morning atmosphere

---

## Visual Identity

A single-story, fully rounded dome cottage. No rectangles, no straight edges,
no sharp corners anywhere. Soft matte clay-like surfaces give every part of
the house a handcrafted, toy-like quality. The palette is warm and pastel,
centred on coral pink with white trim. The overall feeling is a "living toy
house" — approachable, soft, and visually simple enough for a preschool
audience to read instantly.

---

## World Layout

The world reads as one continuous journey rather than a set of separate
buildings or rooms. A front garden leads to a porch, which leads to the
entrance, which opens directly into one continuous interior living space.
That interior space contains soft activity zones rather than walled rooms.
A back door connects the interior to a smaller, quieter back garden. Exterior
and interior are treated as a single flowing environment, not two separate
sets.

---

## Spatial Relationships

- The front garden connects naturally to the porch, and the porch to the entrance.
- The entrance opens directly into the main living space — no intermediate hallway room.
- The reading corner remains visually connected to the rest of the main living space at all times.
- The window maintains a visual relationship with both the reading area and the table.
- The garden is visible through the window from inside.
- The sleeping corner is visible from the main living space but reads as slightly more private.
- The back garden is glimpsed through the back door rather than fully separated.
- The kitchen nook shares its light source with the window corner.

---

## Playable Areas

**Front Garden** — Purpose: welcoming approach to the house. Visual identity: three large round flowers (red, yellow, pink), soft green grass, low coral pink fence, round stepping-stone path. Important props: flowers, fence, path. Typical use: arrival, waiting, garden activities.

**Front Porch** — Purpose: threshold between outside and inside. Visual identity: round white arched door, stone doorstep. Important props: door. Typical use: greetings, entering/exiting.

**Entrance** — Purpose: first step inside. Visual identity: warm interior light meeting daylight. Important props: simple shoe area. Typical use: arrival transition.

**Main Living Space** — Purpose: the heart of the home, one continuous open room. Visual identity: warm white walls, round window, round table and chairs, round patterned rug. Important props: table, chairs, rug, toy box, shelf. Typical use: shared activity, gathering.

**Reading Corner** — Purpose: quiet zone within the main space. Visual identity: round shelf with books, soft cushion, natural window light. Important props: shelf, books, cushion. Typical use: calm, quiet moments.

**Kitchen Nook** — Purpose: simple food-related zone. Visual identity: small round table, fruit basket. Important props: fruit basket, cup. Typical use: light snack moments.

**Window Corner** — Purpose: connection point between inside and the outside world. Visual identity: one large round window with a coral pink curtain. Important props: window, curtain. Typical use: looking outside, noticing the world.

**Sleeping Corner** — Purpose: rest and closure. Visual identity: round coral pink bed with a round pillow. Important props: bed. Typical use: sleep, calm night moments.

**Back Garden** — Purpose: quieter, more hidden outdoor zone. Visual identity: flowers, one small tree, smooth stones, dappled shade. Important props: small tree, stones. Typical use: quiet discovery.

## Materials

Exterior walls and roof: soft matte clay, smooth and toy-like. Door and
window frames: smooth matte white. Curtains: soft draping fabric. Fence:
soft matte, rounded tops. Path: smooth rounded stone. Grass: soft plush
texture. Interior walls and floor: soft matte, warm and clean. Furniture
(table, chairs, shelf): smooth matte, rounded forms. Rug: soft plush.
Bed: soft plush, rounded. Toy box: smooth matte. No glass sharpness, no
metal, no realistic textures anywhere.

---

## Lighting

Primary light is warm daylight. Morning brings soft golden light through
the windows; midday is bright and even; evening turns golden-orange with
longer soft shadows; night is soft moonlight with warm light still glowing
from inside. Indoor lighting is always warm, mixing window daylight with a
cozy interior glow. Shadows are always soft and short — never hard, never
dark.

---

## Colour Palette

Walls: coral pink. Roof: darker pink. Door and window frames: white.
Curtain: coral pink. Interior light: warm pale yellow. Grass: soft green.
Path: light grey. Toy box: warm yellow. The palette stays within this
small, consistent pastel set across every zone.

---

## Scale

Everything is sized for a small preschool child. The house itself is a
single, modestly sized cottage — large enough to feel like a real home, small
enough to feel cozy rather than grand. The door is child-proportioned, low
and round. Windows are round and modestly sized. Furniture (table, chairs,
bed) reads as rounded and slightly oversized-soft rather than adult-scaled.
The garden is intimate, not sprawling — a few flowers and a short path, not a
large yard.

---

## Atmosphere

Warm. Soft. Safe. Inviting. Every surface reads as touchable and gentle.
The mood shifts gently with time of day — hopeful in the morning, calm in
the evening — but never loses its warmth or softness.

---

## Consistency Rules

- The house is always a small, fully rounded cottage — never rectangular, never sharp-edged.
- The interior is always one continuous connected space — never separate walled rooms.
- Wall colour, roof colour, and door/window colour stay fixed across every generation.
- The three front-garden flowers stay red, yellow, and pink.
- The fence stays low and coral pink.
- No modern technology, no vehicles, no realistic architecture.
- No visual clutter — each zone stays simple and readable.
- No dark or high-contrast lighting.
- The house's core shape, roof, door, and window design are never redesigned or reinterpreted.

---

## Generation Considerations

The interior must be generated as a single open room with soft activity
zones, not as separate walled rooms — this is the defining trait of this
world and the most common failure point to watch for. Keep all shapes
rounded with no sharp corners anywhere, inside or outside. Keep the colour
palette limited to the set defined above. Favor a soft, matte, handcrafted
material language over any glossy, metallic, or photorealistic rendering.
Treat the house's exterior shape, colours, and proportions as a fixed
identity to preserve exactly, not to reinterpret, across every generation.

## Visual Richness Layer

> See `POMPOM_HILLS_PRODUCTION/02_WORLDS/GLOBAL_ENVIRONMENT_STANDARD.md` § Visual Richness Principle.
> Kiko's Home must never be reduced to "a pink house."

This layer protects Kiko's Home from becoming too plain. Keep the rounded
cottage silhouette clear and readable — add only the canon-approved charm
details below. Do not add random objects. Do not remove the warm, safe,
toy-house emotional identity.

**Allowed charm details:** KH-DOOR-01, the round white arched front door with
its round white handle, is the primary landmark and should be visible or
implied in every exterior shot. The rounded dark-pink dome roof with its small
decorative round chimney reinforces the toy-cottage silhouette. The round
stepping-stone path (#E0E0E0) curves from the low coral-pink fence to the
door. The three large round front-garden flowers (red, yellow, pink) and the
yellow round toy box (KH-TOYBOX-01) inside add small reusable prop charm.
Gentle motion — the door's soft slow "squeak" as it opens, and the coral-pink
curtain swaying at the Window Corner — keeps the world feeling alive without
adding clutter.

**Forbidden clutter:** rectangular or sharp-edged house shapes, a generic
unremarkable cottage with no visible door detail, vehicles or modern
technology anywhere on the property, broken or messy objects, large crowds
or clutter in the garden, harsh dark shadows, sharp corners on any furniture,
and sudden or fast motion anywhere in or around the house.

### Hero View Quality Tests

Before accepting a Hero View, it must pass:

1. **Silhouette Test** — recognisable as Kiko's Home from the rounded dome-roof cottage shape alone?
2. **Colour Test** — recognisable from the coral-pink walls, white door, and white trim alone?
3. **Charm Test** — does it make a child want to knock on the round white door and go inside?

If any test fails, reject.
