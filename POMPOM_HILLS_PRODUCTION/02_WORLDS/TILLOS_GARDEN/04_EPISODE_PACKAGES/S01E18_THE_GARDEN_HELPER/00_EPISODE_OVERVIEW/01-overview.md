## Story Summary

Tillo and Kiko are working in Tillo's Garden when they notice something wrong — a little flower bed is looking droopy and dry. Tillo shows Kiko the problem: the soil is cracked and thirsty. Kiko feels worried for the flowers. Tillo teaches Kiko how to protect the garden — they gently loosen the soil, add fresh earth, and water carefully. Kiko learns to pat the soil around the roots so the flowers feel safe. They step back together and watch. The flowers slowly perk up, their petals lifting toward the sun. Tillo and Kiko smile proudly — they protected the garden together, and the flowers are happy again.

---

## Micro-Moment (Key Discovery)

Kiko learning to pat the soil around the flower's roots — the gentle, caring touch that shows protecting nature isn't just about water, but about making plants feel safe and loved.

## Learning Point

Caring for nature isn't just about planting — it's about protecting what's already growing. When you notice something needs help and work together to fix it, you make the whole garden stronger.
