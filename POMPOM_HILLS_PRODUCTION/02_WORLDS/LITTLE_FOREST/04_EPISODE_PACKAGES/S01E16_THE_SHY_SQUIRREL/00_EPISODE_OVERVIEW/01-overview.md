## Story Summary

Aiko is sitting quietly in Little Forest when she spots a small squirrel hiding behind an oak tree. She watches it peek out — its nose twitches — then it hides again. Aiko doesn't move. She waits. Kiko walks up and starts to say hello, but Aiko gently puts a finger to her lips. They both sit quietly. The squirrel — Hazelnut — peeks out again, nose twitching. It takes one small step forward. Then another. It looks at them. They smile softly. Hazelnut slowly walks closer, carrying a tiny acorn in its paws. It places the acorn near them — a gift — then sits close, nose still twitching. Aiko and Kiko share a warm look. They learned that patience and gentleness help shy friends feel safe.

## Micro-Moment (Key Discovery)

Hazelnut the squirrel placing a tiny acorn near Aiko and Kiko — its small trust offering — then sitting close, nose twitching, while they smile quietly.

## Learning Point

Shyness isn't something to fix — it's something to respect. When you give space and wait with kindness, trust grows on its own.

---

## Character Note: Hazelnut

Hazelnut is not a generic squirrel. It has a specific personality:
- Always carries acorns (one at a time, in both paws)
- Peeks from behind the tree trunk, never fully visible at first
- Nose twitches 2-3 times before each movement
- Moves in small, hesitant steps
- The acorn gift is its way of saying "I trust you"
