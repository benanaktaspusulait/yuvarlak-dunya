## Overview

Cloud Hill, Pompom Hills'in en yüksek tepesi. Gökyüzüne uzanan yumuşak yeşil bir tepe — bulutların en yakın göründüğü, rüzgarın en hafif estiği yer.

---

## Purpose

Gökyüzünü izleme ve hayal kurma mekanı. Çocuklar buraya çıkarak dünyayı yukarıdan görür, bulutları yakından izler, yağmurun nereden geldiğini keşfeder.

---

## Why This World Exists ⭐

Cloud Hill, çocuklar için "yukarı bakma" anının somut karşılığıdır. Yüksek bir tepeden gökyüzüne bakmak sonsuzluk hissi verir. Yağmur, gökkuşağı, bulutlar — hepsi buradan başlar. Bu mekan merakı besler ve büyük soruların güvenle sorulabileceği bir alan yaratır.

---

## Emotional Purpose

### Why does this place exist emotionally?

Cloud Hill, huzur ve hayal gücünü temsil eder. Yüksekte olmak özgürlük hissi verir; açık gökyüzü sonsuz keşif alanı sunar.

### How do children feel when they arrive?

Sakin, meraklı, özgür. Zirvede durmanın getirdiği sessiz bir tatmin ve "daha neler var?" merakı.

### What stories naturally belong here?

Yağmur ve bulut keşfi, gökkuşağı izleme, gün batımı seyretme, yıldız sayma, rüzgarla oynama.

### What emotional experiences does this world support?

Huzur · Hayal gücü · Merak · Keşif · Sessiz hayranlık

## Visual Identity

### General

- Yuvarlak, yumuşak eğimli tek tepe — sivri ya da sarp hiçbir şey yok
- Tepe düz bir plato ile biter — oturulabilir, geniş, sade
- Açık gökyüzü her zaman görünür — yüzde altmış alan gökyüzü, yüzde kırk tepe
- Büyük yuvarlak bulutlar tepede süzülür
- Yumuşak rüzgar çimenleri ve saçları sallandırır

### Tepe

- Kısa, yumuşak, açık yeşil çimen (#A5D6A7)
- Eğimli yamacın tepesinde düz geniş alan
- Çimler yüzeyden hafif yükselir — tüylü his
- Hiç taş, çıkıntı ya da sivri öğe yok

### Gökyüzü

- Açık, geniş, pale mavi (#E3F2FD → #BBDEFB zirvede)
- 2–3 büyük yuvarlak pamuklu bulut — hiçbiri karanlık değil
- Bulutlar yavaşça sürüklenir — soldan sağa, çok yavaş
- Güneş daima sağ üst köşede, yumuşak ve parlak değil

### Horizon

- Uzakta diğer tepeler pastel siluet olarak görünür
- Hiçbir bina, yol ya da modern öğe ufukta yok
- Ufuk çizgisi yumuşak ve alçak — gökyüzü ezici çoğunlukta

### Rüzgar

- Görünmez ama hissedilir — çimen dalgalanması, saç hareketi, hafif ses
- Sürekli, yumuşak, değişmez hızda
- Hiçbir zaman sert ya da ani değil

## Visual Richness & World Charm

> See `POMPOM_HILLS_PRODUCTION/00_GLOBAL_RULES/WORLD_STANDARDS/GLOBAL_ENVIRONMENT_STANDARD.md` § Visual Richness Principle.
> Cloud Hill must NOT be only a hill and clouds. If Cloud Hill looks like any
> normal hill, reject it.

Cloud Hill is the soft cloud-watching hill of Pompom Hills. It should feel
like: **"I want to climb up there and look at the sky."**

### Iconic Landmark

CH-STONE-01 — the sitting/viewing stone at the summit, right of centre. This
is the primary visual anchor: the object that turns "a hill" into "the hill
you climb to sit and watch the clouds."

### Allowed Charm Details

**Landmark Charm**
- CH-STONE-01 — smooth summit sitting/viewing stone (already a Locked Element, see World Identity Lock below)
- gentle curved path or climbable slope leading to the summit

**Surface Charm**
- soft cloud-shadow patches drifting across the grass
- tiny rounded pebbles along the climb path

**Small Prop Charm**
- small soft grass tufts near the summit
- small white/yellow wildflowers scattered on the hillside (CH-DAISY-01)
- one small cloud-shaped decorative stone near the summit, if approved

**Motion Charm**
- gentle wind ribbons or subtle grass waves
- soft horizon hills visible in the background, gently hazy

### Forbidden Clutter

```
✗ empty plain hill with no landmark
✗ generic meadow
✗ harsh mountain
✗ cliffs
✗ too many flowers like Flower Hill
✗ houses on the summit
✗ busy village elements
✗ dark storm clouds
✗ realistic landscape
```

### Thumbnail Hook

A soft green dome hill with an inviting summit stone under large friendly
clouds.

### Child Recognition Test

**"The hill where clouds feel close."**

### Hero View Must Show

- dome silhouette
- summit
- sitting/viewing stone (CH-STONE-01)
- curved path or climbable slope

## World Identity Lock

> These elements define the canonical visual identity of Cloud Hill. Every future generation must preserve them exactly. **The Canon Hero View (`00-hero-view.png`) is the primary reference — it overrides descriptive ambiguity.**

### Canonical Hero View Rule

Before generating any new Cloud Hill scene, match the Canon Hero View on these six points:

1. Hill silhouette — single smooth dome, same curve as Hero View
2. Summit shape — broad and rounded, CH-STONE-01 visible right-of-centre
3. Sky ratio — approximately 62% sky
4. Cloud layout — three clouds: large upper-left, small centre, medium upper-right
5. Colour palette — grass, sky, and clouds within the defined pastel range
6. Stone position — CH-STONE-01 at summit, slightly right of centre

If the generated hill does not immediately resemble the Hero View silhouette on first glance, reject without further evaluation.

### Locked Elements

| Element | Canonical Definition | Locked |
|---------|---------------------|:------:|
| Hill silhouette | Single smooth dome — wide base, gently rounded top, no peak. **Matches Canon Hero View.** | 🔒 |
| Skyline shape | Unbroken soft curve — the hill meets the sky in one continuous arc, never jagged | 🔒 |
| Horizon height | Horizon sits at 35–40% from the bottom of the frame in all wide shots | 🔒 |
| Sky-to-ground ratio | Sky 62% / Horizon 8% / Ground 30% — as established in Canon Hero View | 🔒 |
| Cloud count | **3 clouds per frame** — large upper-left, small centre, medium upper-right | 🔒 |
| Cloud spacing | Clouds spread across upper sky — never clustered, never touching | 🔒 |
| Cloud style | Large, rounded, fully white, soft-edged cumulus — never wispy, never storm-shaped | 🔒 |
| Sun position | Upper right quadrant, always — soft glow, never a hard disc | 🔒 |
| Grass appearance | Short, even, soft plush texture, open green (#A5D6A7) — Hero View reads slightly brighter; acceptable variation | 🔒 |
| CH-STONE-01 position | Summit, slightly right of centre — **primary visual anchor of the world** | 🔒 |
| Overall composition | Sky-dominant — as Canon Hero View. The world breathes upward, not inward | 🔒 |

### Acceptable Natural Variations

These elements may shift slightly between generations without breaking identity:

- Exact cloud shape (clouds may vary organically as long as they remain white and rounded)
- Exact grass blade detail (texture variation is fine, colour must stay within range)
- Horizon silhouette of distant hills (may shift slightly in detail, must remain pastel and low)
- Time of day lighting (morning vs. midday vs. evening — as long as mood rules are respected)

### What Must Never Change

- Sky-to-ground ratio falling below 60/40
- More than 4 clouds or zero clouds in frame
- Hill silhouette becoming pointed, jagged, or asymmetrical
- Horizon rising above 45% of frame height
- Any modern object, structure, or road appearing anywhere
- Grass turning yellow, brown, or oversaturated green

## Camera Identity

> The permanent camera standard for Cloud Hill. All shots — regardless of story — must follow this identity.

### Default Lens

35mm equivalent. Wide enough to show sky dominance. Narrow enough to keep the world intimate and warm. Never wider than 28mm (distorts the hill). Never tighter than 50mm (loses the sky).

### Child Eye Level

All shots default to 0.75–0.80 m camera height — the natural eye level of a standing preschool child. This makes the sky feel expansive and the hill feel safe rather than towering.

| Height | When Used |
|--------|-----------|
| 0.80 m | Standing character, wide and establishing shots |
| 0.75 m | Sitting character, intimate conversation shots |
| 0.60 m | Close-up object or grass detail |
| POV | Cloud-watching only — free height, looking upward |

### Movement Philosophy

The camera on Cloud Hill moves like a slow breath. Nothing is sudden. Nothing cuts hard. The world is still and the camera respects that stillness.

### Allowed Movements

| Movement | Speed | When |
|----------|-------|------|
| Slow pan | Very slow — 3–5 seconds across | Establishing, environment reveal |
| Gentle tilt up | 2–3 seconds | Face-to-sky transition |
| Slow push in | Very slow — 4+ seconds | Emotional close moment |
| Static hold | — | Dialogue, observation, silence |

### Forbidden Movements

```
✗ Fast pan or whip pan
✗ Handheld shake or bounce
✗ Zoom in or zoom out
✗ Crane drop or fast vertical movement
✗ 360° rotation
✗ Drone-style fly-over
```

### Framing Consistency

- Subject is never placed in the centre of frame — slightly left or right of centre
- Sky always occupies at least 50% of frame even in medium shots
- Horizon is always visible in wide shots — never cut off by framing
- Characters are always framed with the sky behind them — never the ground

### Transition Rules

- Scene openings: always begin with environment wide shot before introducing character
- Scene closings: always end wider than the previous shot — pull back to sky
- Between shots: cut or slow dissolve only — no wipe, no flash

## Lighting Identity

> The canonical lighting system for Cloud Hill. Must be consistent across every scene in every episode that uses this world.

### Light Direction

Primary light comes from the upper left in all standard daytime scenes. This is the canonical sun position for Cloud Hill and must not change between shots in the same scene.

| Time | Direction | Quality |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Morning | Upper left, warm | Softest — golden, gentle |
| Midday | Top centre, neutral | Brightest — even, clean |
| Evening | Upper left, golden-orange | Warmest — long soft shadows |
| Night | Top, cool moonlight | Softest — pale blue-white |

### Softness

All light on Cloud Hill is soft. There are no hard shadows, no sharp shadow edges, no spotlight effects. The light feels like it is being filtered through a thin layer of high cloud at all times — bright but never harsh.

### Ambient Fill

A gentle ambient fill always lifts the shadow side of every object and character. No side of any subject falls into true darkness. The darkest shadow value on Cloud Hill is approximately 70% of the highlight value — never lower.

### Shadow Density

| Shadow Type | Density | Notes |
|-------------|---------|-------|
| Character shadow on grass | Very soft, 20–30% opacity | Short — never longer than character height |
| Grass shadow detail | Barely visible | Ground stays bright and readable |
| Cloud shadow on hill | None — clouds do not cast shadows on the hill | This keeps the ground clean and safe |

### Exposure Behaviour

The world is always slightly bright — not overexposed, but lifted. Midtones are open and readable. Nothing is crushed in the darks. Highlights are soft — never blown.

### Continuity Rules

- Light direction must not change between shots in the same scene
- If a scene begins in morning light, every shot in that scene stays in morning light
- Cloud shadows are not cast on the ground — they exist only in the sky
- Character lighting must match environment lighting — no isolated character illumination

## Colour Identity

> The permanent colour profile for Cloud Hill. Every future shot must preserve this profile unless the story explicitly requires a shift (e.g. a deliberate sunset or night scene).

### White Balance

Warm daylight — approximately 5500K. Slightly warm, never cool or blue-shifted in daytime scenes.

### Exposure

+0.3 to +0.5 stops above neutral. The world is bright, open, and lifted.

### Contrast

Low contrast. The distance between the brightest and darkest values is narrow. Everything reads clearly. Nothing disappears into shadow.

### Saturation

Medium-low. Colours are present and readable but never vivid or punchy. The palette feels hand-painted, not digital.

### Pastel Palette

| Element | Colour | Hex |
|---------|--------|-----|
| Grass | Soft open green | #A5D6A7 |
| Sky upper | Pale blue | #BBDEFB |
| Sky lower | Very pale blue-white | #E3F2FD |
| Clouds | Pure soft white | #F5F5F5 |
| Distant hills | Pale grey-green pastel | #C8E6C9 |
| Daisy white | Clean white | #FFFFFF |
| Daisy yellow centre | Soft warm yellow | #FFF59D |
| Sitting stone | Smooth pale grey | #ECEFF1 |

### Highlight Behaviour

Highlights are soft and rolled — they never clip to pure white. The brightest point in any frame is a cloud highlight, which should feel like soft cotton, not a white wall.

### Shadow Behaviour

Shadows are lifted and warm — never cold, never black. The darkest value in any standard daytime frame is a warm mid-grey, approximately #9E9E9E or lighter.

### Forbidden Colour Shifts

```
✗ Cool blue or teal shift in daytime scenes
✗ Desaturation to grey or monochrome
✗ High-contrast or HDR look
✗ Neon or oversaturated colours anywhere
✗ Pure black in any element
✗ Pure white except soft cloud highlights
✗ Grass shifting to yellow-green or olive
✗ Sky shifting to deep blue or dramatic purple
```

## Environmental Sound Identity

> The reusable ambient sound standard for Cloud Hill. These rules apply to every scene, every episode.

### Wind

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Intensity | Soft and constant — never gusting, never silent |
| Tone | Low, smooth, slightly breathy — not whistling |
| Variation | Very slight — rises gently with emotional peaks, drops in quiet moments |
| Forbidden | Howling, sharp gusts, wind that sounds threatening |

### Bird Frequency

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Presence | Always present but distant — birds are below on the hillside, not at the summit |
| Call type | Single melodic calls, not flocks or chattering |
| Frequency | One call every 8–12 seconds on average |
| Volume | Low — never louder than the wind layer |

### Ambience Level

The total ambient sound of Cloud Hill is quiet. It should feel like the quietest outdoor space in Pompom Hills. The wind and birds together sit at approximately 20–25% of the maximum audio level. Dialogue always sits clearly above ambience.

### Silence Ratio

Silence — or near-silence with only the faintest wind — is a valid and intentional sound state for Cloud Hill. It should be used at emotional peaks: the moment a character first sees the clouds, the moment rain begins, the moment a question is asked and not yet answered.

### Forbidden Sounds

```
✗ Traffic, machinery, or any modern sound
✗ Crowd noise or group laughter from the summit
✗ Thunder or storm sounds
✗ Insect buzz (bees, flies)
✗ Sudden loud sounds of any kind
✗ Music that was not authored for the scene
```

```
✓ Scene feels open, calm, and sky-dominant?
✓ No threatening or dramatic visual tone?
✓ Wind implied by grass or hair movement?
✓ No modern objects in any part of the frame?
```

### World Recognition

```
✓ Would a viewer recognise this as Cloud Hill from a previous episode?
✓ Does the hill silhouette match the canonical dome shape?
✓ Does the sky-to-ground ratio feel consistent with other Cloud Hill frames?
✓ Could this frame be placed next to another Cloud Hill frame without jarring inconsistency?
```

## Production Summary

Cloud Hill is a canonical reusable world. Every time it appears — regardless of episode, regardless of characters, regardless of story — it must feel like exactly the same place.

The world has one defining quality: it belongs to the sky. More sky than ground. More open than enclosed. More still than active. Characters come here to look up, wonder, ask questions, and be quietly amazed. The hill itself is the simplest possible form — a smooth dome of soft green grass. Everything else is sky.

For production, Cloud Hill is low-complexity and high-consistency. It has few props, a defined colour palette, a locked silhouette, and a clear camera language. This makes it one of the fastest worlds to generate reliably — but only if the identity rules are respected on every frame.

The single most common failure point is the sky ratio dropping below 60%. Always verify this first. The second most common failure is clouds becoming grey, dark, or dramatically lit. Clouds on Cloud Hill are always white, always soft, always safe.

Every future episode using Cloud Hill should load the Hero View as its master reference, verify the Colour Identity before accepting any frame, and run the Production QA checklist before signing off on any shot. The world does not change. Only the story does.

## World Navigation

> The canonical movement flow through Cloud Hill. Every episode using this world should follow this navigation unless the story explicitly requires a variation.

### Entry Point

Characters always enter Cloud Hill from the lower hillside — approaching from the direction of Central Square or Flower Hill. They are never already at the summit when a scene begins. Arrival is part of the world experience.

### Canonical Transition Sequence

```
1. Entry — characters appear at the base of the visible slope
       ↓
2. Climb Path — characters walk upward along the grassy hillside
       ↓
3. Summit Arrival — characters crest the top, sky opens fully
       ↓
4. Observation Area — characters settle at the summit plateau
       ↓
5. Natural Exit — characters descend back down the same slope
```

### Climb Path

The climb path is the open grassy hillside. There is no defined trail, no steps, no rope — characters walk freely up the gentle slope. The path curves slightly left-to-right as it rises so the summit is revealed gradually, not all at once. The camera typically follows from behind or from the side during the climb.

### Summit Arrival

Summit arrival is a canonical emotional beat. As the character reaches the top, the horizon drops and the sky expands — this is the visual payoff. Camera tilts up slightly or widens to reveal the full sky. This moment should never be cut short or skipped.

### Observation Area

The observation area is the summit plateau. Characters sit on or near the sitting stone, lie on the grass, or stand at the edge looking outward. This is where the episode's main story content happens — conversation, cloud-watching, discovery.

### Natural Exit

Characters exit the same way they entered — descending the hillside. The exit is always calm and unhurried. A closing wide shot of the hill after characters have left (or are small silhouettes descending) is a canonical closing image.

### Canonical vs. Optional Transitions

| Transition | Status | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Entry → Climb → Summit | Canonical | Use in every episode that visits Cloud Hill |
| Summit → Observation → Exit | Canonical | Standard story structure |
| Starting at summit with no climb | Optional | Only when the climb is shown in a previous episode of a multi-part story |
| Teleporting between worlds without a transition shot | Forbidden | Always show the approach |

## View Transition Rules

> How camera views should progress during a standard Cloud Hill episode. This is the canonical shot progression.

### Standard Shot Sequence

Default episode flow — alternative openings are permitted when story continuity requires them (e.g. a character already established at the summit in a prior scene does not need to climb again).

```
Environment wide (establish — no character)
    ↓
Approach shot (character on hillside, moving upward)
    ↓
Summit shot (arrival + main story)
    ↓
Close shot (emotional peak — face, cloud, grass, or hand)
    ↓
Closing wide (exit or sky hold)
```

### When to Use Each Shot Type

| Shot Type | When | Notes |
|-----------|------|-------|
| Environment wide | Scene opening — before any character appears | Always first |
| Approach shot | Character on hillside, moving upward | Can be skipped when continuity permits |
| Summit shot | Character at the top — main story content | Always present |
| Close shot | Emotional peak | Optional but recommended |
| Closing wide | Scene end — exit or silence | Always end wider than the previous shot |

### Transition Guidance

- **Environment wide → Approach shot:** Cut or slow dissolve. Never jump straight to the summit without the climb.
- **Approach → Summit:** The tilt-up or widen as the summit is crested. This is a key emotional transition — give it time.
- **Summit → Close:** Slow push in or direct cut to close-up. The detail should feel like a natural zoom of attention, not a hard jump.
- **Close → Closing wide:** Pull back slowly or cut to wide. Never end on a close shot — always return to the world.
- **Closing wide:** Let the world breathe before cutting.

### Transitions to Avoid

```
✗ Opening directly on a character close-up — always establish the world first
✗ Cutting from environment wide to close shot without a summit shot in between
✗ Ending on a medium or close shot — always close wider
✗ Two consecutive close shots without a wider shot between them
✗ Sudden cut from the summit to a completely different world with no transition
```

## Character Occupancy

> Recommended character-to-frame ratios for Cloud Hill. The world must remain the primary visual element — characters inhabit it, not dominate it.

### Wide Shots

Characters occupy no more than 15–20% of the total frame area. They appear small against the hill and sky. Two characters side by side should feel dwarfed by the open space around them. This is intentional — the vastness of the sky is part of the story.

| Shot Type | Character Frame % | Sky Visible | Notes |
|-----------|:-----------------:|:-----------:|-------|
| Hero View | 0% | 62% | No character — pure environment |
| Wide establishing | 10–15% | 60%+ | Character small on hill |
| Wide with two characters | 15–20% | 55%+ | Both characters visible full-body |

### Medium Shots

Characters occupy 30–45% of the frame. Sky remains visible above and/or behind. The sitting stone, daisies, or grass are visible in the lower frame. Characters are never the only thing in the frame.

| Shot Type | Character Frame % | Sky Visible | Notes |
|-----------|:-----------------:|:-----------:|-------|
| Single character medium | 30–35% | 40%+ | Sky behind character always |
| Two character medium | 40–45% | 35%+ | Horizon visible between characters |

### Close Shots

Characters occupy 60–75% of the frame. Even in close shots, a sliver of sky or horizon is always visible. A close-up of a face looking up at clouds should still show the pale sky as background — never a blurred or neutral background.

| Shot Type | Character Frame % | Sky Visible | Notes |
|-----------|:-----------------:|:-----------:|-------|
| Face close-up | 65–75% | 20%+ | Sky as background, not bokeh |
| Hand or prop detail | 50–60% | 15%+ | Grass visible, sky glimpsed |

### Emotional Moments

At peak emotional moments — wonder, discovery, quiet realisation — the camera pulls back rather than pushing in. The character becomes smaller, the world becomes larger. This is Cloud Hill's signature emotional move: intimacy through scale contrast, not through closeness.

## Typical Episode Usage

> Recommended story structures for Cloud Hill. Each format is self-contained and production-ready.

### 30-Second Story

**Structure:** Arrive → Single moment → Exit

```
0–5s    Environment wide — establish Cloud Hill, no character
5–12s   Character arrives at summit
12–24s  One simple discovery or observation at the summit
24–30s  Closing wide — character looks out or begins descent
```

**Fits this format:**
- First visit to Cloud Hill (character sees clouds for the first time)
- A simple question asked and wondered about ("Why do clouds float?")
- A quiet moment between two characters looking at the sky together

### 60-Second Story

**Structure:** Arrive → Discover → Reflect → Exit

```
0–5s    Environment wide — establish
5–12s   Climb and arrival
12–40s  Main story content at summit (2–3 summit shots + close shot)
40–52s  Quiet reflection or dialogue
52–60s  Closing wide — exit or sky hold
```

**Fits this format:**
- Cloud-watching and wondering where rain comes from (S01E13)
- Discovering a rainbow begins here
- Two characters sharing a question and an answer
- First raindrop — noticing and wondering

### 90-Second Story

**Structure:** Arrive → Discover → Explore → Emotional peak → Exit

```
0–5s    Environment wide
5–15s   Climb (longer, more exploratory)
15–50s  Main story — multiple beats, summit shots and close shots alternating
50–70s  Emotional peak — close shot, then pull back to wide
70–85s  Resolution — characters settled, looking out
85–90s  Closing wide — world returns to stillness
```

**Fits this format:**
- Watching rain begin — from first cloud to first drops
- A longer cloud-imagination session (finding shapes together)
- A story involving another character arriving at the summit partway through
- Opa tells a story about the sky

### Story Types That Naturally Fit Cloud Hill

| Story Type | Why It Fits |
|------------|------------|
| Nature discovery | "Where does rain come from?" — the clouds are here |
| Wondering together | Open sky encourages open questions |
| Quiet companionship | Two characters sitting in comfortable silence |
| First-time arrival | The summit reveal is inherently a story beat |
| Emotional reset | Characters come here to feel better after something hard |
| Evening or night scene | Sunset and stars — natural story endings |
| Rainbow start | Cloud Hill is the canonical beginning of any rainbow story |

### Story Types That Do Not Fit Cloud Hill

```
✗ High-energy action or chase scenes — the world is calm
✗ Large group scenes (more than 3 characters) — the space is intimate
✗ Stories that require props, buildings, or tools
✗ Stories that need enclosed spaces
✗ Conflict or confrontation scenes — the world is safe
```

## Common Generation Failures

> The most frequent AI generation problems when producing Cloud Hill. Each entry explains how to recognise it, why it matters, and how to fix it.

### Failure 01 — Sky Ratio Too Low

**How to recognise:** The hill takes up more than 45% of the frame. The sky feels compressed or crowded. The world looks like a hill with some sky rather than a sky with a hill.

**Why it breaks identity:** Cloud Hill's defining quality is sky dominance. When the ground takes over, the world loses its sense of openness and wonder. It becomes just a hill, not a place that belongs to the sky.

**How to correct:** Lower the camera angle slightly or reframe upward. Alternatively, regenerate with an explicit instruction: sky must occupy at least 60% of frame. Verify before accepting.

---

### Failure 02 — Dark or Grey Clouds

**How to recognise:** Clouds have grey undersides, dark centres, or appear threatening. The sky mood shifts from calm to ominous.

**Why it breaks identity:** Cloud Hill is a safe world. Dark clouds carry emotional weight — threat, storm, danger. These feelings are completely wrong for this world and for the preschool audience.

**How to correct:** Regenerate with explicit instruction: all clouds must be fully white, soft matte texture, no grey or dark undersides. If the sky overall is too grey, warm the white balance and reduce contrast.

---

### Failure 03 — Jagged or Pointed Hill Silhouette

**How to recognise:** The hill outline has visible bumps, ridges, or a pointed peak. The smooth dome silhouette becomes irregular.

**Why it breaks identity:** The smooth dome is the hill's visual identity. Irregularity makes the hill look like a mountain or a rocky terrain — both wrong for Pompom Hills. It also breaks visual continuity with other Cloud Hill frames.

**How to correct:** Regenerate with explicit instruction: hill must be a single smooth rounded dome, no peaks, no ridges, no bumps in the silhouette. Reject any frame where the dome curve is interrupted.

---

### Failure 04 — Modern Objects in Frame

**How to recognise:** A fence, path, road, building, utility pole, or any man-made structure appears anywhere in the frame — including the background.

**Why it breaks identity:** Cloud Hill has no infrastructure. Any modern object immediately pulls the viewer out of the Pompom Hills world and signals that the world has not been generated correctly.

**How to correct:** Regenerate with explicit instruction: no fences, no paths, no buildings, no modern objects anywhere in frame. Check the full frame including background horizon before accepting.

---

### Failure 05 — Oversaturated or Wrong Grass Colour

**How to recognise:** The grass appears vivid emerald green, yellow-green, olive, or brown. It does not match the soft open green (#A5D6A7) of the canonical palette.

**Why it breaks identity:** Grass colour is a primary continuity marker. If it shifts between frames or episodes, the world no longer reads as the same place. Oversaturation also breaks the pastel feel of the entire Pompom Hills visual language.

**How to correct:** Reduce saturation, warm the green slightly, and lighten toward #A5D6A7. Regenerate with explicit colour instruction if needed. Always verify grass colour against the canonical hex value before accepting.

---

### Failure 06 — Wrong Cloud Count

**How to recognise:** The sky is completely clear (zero clouds) or packed with many small clouds. Either extreme breaks the canonical identity.

**Why it breaks identity:** Zero clouds makes Cloud Hill meaningless — the defining feature of the world is absent. Too many clouds makes the sky busy and visually noisy, breaking the calm open atmosphere.

**How to correct:** Regenerate with explicit instruction: exactly 2–3 large rounded white clouds, well-spaced, in the upper half of the sky. Reject any frame with zero, one, or four or more clouds.

---

### Failure 07 — Colour Temperature Too Cool

**How to recognise:** The overall image feels blue, cold, or desaturated. The grass reads as grey-green, the sky as steel blue, and the whole world feels damp or overcast even in a technically sunny scene.

**Why it breaks identity:** Cloud Hill is a warm, safe, inviting world. Cool colour temperature signals distance, sadness, or threat — none of which belong here.

**How to correct:** Warm the white balance toward 5500K. Add a subtle warm tone to the highlights. Ensure the sky reads pale blue-white (#E3F2FD) at the horizon rather than saturated blue.

---

### Failure 08 — Hero View Silhouette Lost

**How to recognise:** The generated hill has a different shape from the Canon Hero View — narrower base, more pointed top, asymmetrical slope, or a plateau instead of a dome. On first glance the world is not immediately recognisable as Cloud Hill.

**Why it breaks identity:** Cloud Hill's visual identity is its silhouette. A viewer who has seen the Canon Hero View must recognise every subsequent frame as the same world. If the silhouette changes, world continuity breaks across episodes — even if all other rules are followed.

**How to correct:** Reject without evaluating other elements. Load `00-hero-view.png` as the primary reference and regenerate. The hill silhouette check is the first and fastest rejection test.

## Video Generation Rules

> The reusable standard for animated scenes on Cloud Hill. These rules ensure the world feels identical in both still images and animated video.

### Cloud Movement

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Speed | Very slow — clouds travel approximately 5% of frame width per second |
| Direction | Always left to right — never right to left, never vertical |
| Behaviour | Smooth, continuous drift — no acceleration, no stopping |
| Shape change | Clouds do not visibly change shape within a single scene |
| Interaction | Clouds never overlap or merge during a scene |

### Grass Movement

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Type | Gentle wave, low amplitude — tips move, roots stay still |
| Speed | One full wave cycle every 3–4 seconds |
| Direction | Follows wind direction — consistent across the frame |
| Pattern | Gentle ripple across the field, not chaotic or random |
| Amplitude | Grass tips move no more than 20% of grass height — subtle, not wild |

### Wind Intensity

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Visual intensity | Low — hair moves gently, clothing barely stirs |
| Hair movement | Slow, smooth sweep — one direction, not flapping |
| Consistency | Wind direction and intensity do not change within a scene |
| Gusting | No gusts — wind is continuous and even |

### Camera Movement Speed

| Movement Type | Speed Rule |
|---------------|-----------|
| Pan | No faster than 5% of frame width per second |
| Tilt | No faster than 4% of frame height per second |
| Push in | No faster than 2% of subject size increase per second |
| Pull back | Same speed as push in — symmetrical |
| Static hold | Minimum 3 seconds before any movement begins |

All camera movement must feel as if the camera is breathing rather than moving. If the movement is perceptible as "the camera is moving," it is too fast.

### Dialogue Pacing

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Cut timing | Never cut during a line — always cut between lines |
| Pause after line | Minimum 1 second of silence before the next line begins |
| Silence as dialogue | Silence after a big question is intentional — hold for 2–3 seconds |
| Reaction shots | Allow 1.5–2 seconds for a character to react before cutting away |

The pace of dialogue on Cloud Hill is slower than other worlds. The environment demands it. Lines are shorter. Pauses are longer. The sky fills the silence.

### Environmental Rhythm

Cloud Hill has a single ambient rhythm: slow, steady, and breathing. Every moving element follows this rhythm:

| Element | Rhythm |
|---------|--------|
| Clouds | Slow drift — 3–5 second cycle across visible motion |
| Grass | 3–4 second wave cycle |
| Wind audio | Rises slightly every 8–10 seconds, falls back gently |
| Bird call | Once every 8–12 seconds — irregular but not surprising |
| Camera | Moves on the same rhythm as the grass — never fights it |

When all elements are in rhythm, Cloud Hill feels alive but still. When any element breaks rhythm — a sudden camera move, a fast cloud, a sharp sound — the world loses its identity. Rhythm is the final check before approving any animated scene.

### Still Image vs. Video Consistency

Every frame from an animated scene must pass the same Production QA as a still image. Animation does not exempt a frame from identity checks. The canonical colour palette, sky ratio, cloud style, and grass colour apply equally to every individual frame of any animated sequence.
