## Story Summary

Kiko arrives at Opa's Tree, a little rushed and noisy. She wants to show Opa something exciting. But Opa holds up a finger — shhh. He invites her to sit. They sit together quietly. Kiko fidgets at first — it's hard to be still. Then she notices something: a tiny caterpillar crawling on the bark, right next to her paw. She gasps softly. Opa smiles. They watch it together in silence. Then Kiko notices the moss — soft and green, like a tiny forest. Opa tells her: "When we are quiet, we see more. The forest has secrets for those who wait." They sit together, watching clouds drift through the branches above. Kiko leans against Opa. The quiet place is the best place.

---

## Micro-Moment (Key Discovery)

Kiko noticing the tiny caterpillar on the bark — the moment she stops fidgeting and sees something she would have missed if she stayed noisy. The second wow moment is when she leans against Opa and watches clouds through the branches — the quiet becoming a shared experience of peace.
