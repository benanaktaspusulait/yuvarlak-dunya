## Overview

Painting Garden, Pompom Hills'in renklerin yaşadığı bahçesi. Her çiçeğin farklı bir renk olduğu, her yaprağın bir fırça darbesi gibi göründüğü, sanat ve doğanın birleştiği yumuşak bir bahçe. Dört renk çiçek bölgesi — kırmızı, mavi, sarı, mor — bahçenin dört yanına yayılmış durumda. Ortada duran tuval ve boya malzemeleri, bu bahçenin sadece doğal değil aynı zamanda yaratıcı bir alan olduğunu gösterir. Beyaz çit, bahçeyi sarmalayan yumuşak bir sınır oluşturur — güvenli, davetkar, ama asla kapalı hissettirmeyen. Bu bahçe, renklerin dilini konuşan, çocukların ellerindeki fırçayla dünyanın güzelliğini keşfettiği bir yerdir.

---

## Purpose

Sanat keşfi ve renk tanıma mekanı. Çocuklar burada renkleri keşfeder, çiçekleri boyar, birlikte resim yapar ve güzelliği paylaşma deneyimini yaşar. Painting Garden'ın temel amacı, renklerin sadece görme duyusuyla değil, dokunma ve yaratma yoluyla da deneyimlenmesini sağlamaktır. Burada bir çiçeğe bakmak yetmez — onu boyamak, rengini karıştırmak, başkasıyla paylaşmak gerekir. Her etkinlik, çocuğun renkler的世界üne farklı bir pencere açar.

---

## Why This World Exists ⭐

Painting Garden, çocuklar için "güzellik paylaşıldıkça çoğalır" düşüncesinin somut karşılığıdır. Bir çiçeği boyamak, bir arkadaşıyla paylaşmak, bahçeyi birlikte güzelleştirmek — hepsi burada başlar. Bu dünya, çocukların yaratıcılığının bir sınırı olmadığını gösterir. Bir yaprak bile bir fırça olabilir, bir taş bile bir palet olabilir. Painting Garden, çocukların "ben de yapabilirim" hissini yaşadığı, güvenli bir yaratıcılık alanıdır. Burada yanlış boya yoktur — her renk güzeldir, her karışım bir keşiftir. Bu bahçe, çocukların içlerindeki sanatçıyı besler ve onlara güzellik yaratmanın ne demek olduğunu öğretir.

## Emotional Purpose

### Why does this place exist emotionally?

Painting Garden, yaratıcılık ve paylaşmayı temsil eder. Renkler dünyası çocuklara güzellik hissini öğretir; birlikte sanat yapmak arkadaşlık duygusunu güçlendirir. Her çiçek bölgesi farklı bir duygu taşır — kırmızı tutkuyu, mavi huzuru, sarı neşeyi, mor hayal gücünü. Birlikte boyama eylemi, çocukların birbirlerine güvendiğini, fikirlerini paylaştığını ve birlikte güzel bir şey yarattıklarını gösterir. Bu bahçe, "birlikte yaparsak daha güzel olur" hissinin yeşerdiği yerdir.

### How do children feel when they arrive?

Meraklı, yaratıcı, neşeli. Renklerin büyüsüne kapılmış, ellerinde fırçalarla bahçeyi keşfeden çocuklar. İlk adımlarını attıklarında, gözlerinin önünde açılan renk cümbüşü onları büyüler. Her çiçek bölgesi onlara farklı bir hediye sunar — kırmızı bölge enerji verir, mavi bölge sakinleştirir, sarı bölge neşelendirir, mor bölge hayal gücünü tetikler. Children feel safe here — beyaz çit onları korur ama kapatmaz, tuval onları davet eder ama zorlamaz.

### What stories naturally belong here?

Çiçek boyama, renk keşfi, birlikte resim yapma, bahçeyi güzelleştirme, renkleri paylaşma. Painting Garden'da doğan hikayeler, çocukların günlük hayatındaki yaratıcılık anlarını yansıtır. Bir çiçeğin rengine hayran kalmak, bir fırçayı eline almak, birlikte büyük bir resim yapmak — bunlar çocukların her gün yaşadığı ama burada büyüyen deneyimlerdir. Hikayeler asla gergin veya rekabetçi olmaz; her zaman paylaşımcı, keşif dolu ve neşeli olur.

### What emotional experiences does this world support?

Yaratıcılık · Güzellik · Paylaşma · Keşif · Birlikte sanat

## Play Philosophy

### Play style

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Open-ended | Renkleri keşfet, çiçekleri boyama, resim yap — sonuç her zaman farklı olur |
| Imagination | Doğadan sanat üretme, renkleri karıştırma, hayal gücünü renklere dönüştürme |
| Cooperation | Birlikte boyama, birlikte resim yapma, birbirinin fikrine saygı duyma |
| Observation | Farklı renkleri tanıma ve adlandırma, doğadaki renkleri fark etme |
| Gentle movement | Yavaşça boyama, yumuşak fırça darbeleri, sakin ve odaklanmış hareket |

### No rule-based games

- Yarış yok — herkes kendi hızında boyar
- Puan yok — her resim aynı derecede değerlidir
- Rekabet yok — birlikte güzelleştirme var

### Natural interactions

- Çiçekleri renklerine göre ayırt etme — kırmızı, mavi, sarı, mor bölgeleri keşfetme
- Birlikte büyük bir resim yapma — herkes kendi parçasını boyar
- Doğadan malzeme toplama — yaprak, taş, çiçek, dal
- Renkleri karıştırarak yeni renk keşfetme — mavi + sarı = yeşil
- Birlikte bahçeyi güzelleştirme — çitin yanına çiçek boyama

### Play depth

Painting Garden, çocukların farklı gelişim aşamalarına uygun etkinlikler sunar. Küçük çocuklar sadece renklere dokunur ve keşfederken,更大的孩子们 boyama ve karıştırma yapabilir. Birlikte oynayan çocuklar iletişim kurar, fikir paylaşır ve birlikte karar verir. Bu bahçede her孩子 kendi hızında ilerler — kimisi sadece izler, kimisi hemen başlar, kimisi yavaş yavaş yaklaşır. Tüm bu davranışlar doğal ve beklenen davranışlardır.

### Landmark Details

**Colour Zones** — Her renk bölgesi, bahçenin dört bir yanına stratejik olarak yerleştirilmiştir. Kırmızı bölge sol üstte, sarı bölge sağ üstte, mor bölge sol altta, mavi bölge sağ altta konumlanır. Bu düzenleme, çocukların bahçeyi dolaşırken her renkle doğal olarak karşılaşmasını sağlar. Her bölge yaklaşık 1.5–2 metre çapındadır — küçük ama belirgin. Çiçekler yuvarlak, dolgun formdadır ve fırça darbesi gibi görünür — realistik çiçek fotoğrafları değil, sanatsal yorumlar. Her bölgede 5–7 çiçek bulunur, birbirine yakın ama sıkışık değil.

**Canvas on Easel** — Tuval, bahçenin tam merkezinde, hafif sağa kaydırılmış olarak durur. Bu, bahçenin yaratıcılık odak noktası olduğunu gösterir. Tuval boyanmamış durumda bırakılır —孩子的能量 onu doldurur. Ahşap çerçeve yumuşak kahverengidir, metal parça yoktur. Tuvalin yüzeyi pürüzsüz ve beyazdır, fırça darbeleri için hazır bir alan sunar.

**Paint Pots** — Boya kapları, tuvalin solunda, çocukların kolayca ulaşabileceği bir yükseklikte durur. Kaplar yuvarlak, pastel renklerde ve dolgun formdadır. Her kap farklı bir renk içerir — kırmızı, mavi, sarı, mor, yeşil, turuncu. Kaplar taşınabilir ama bahçede sabit bir yerleri vardır. Boya kalitesi çocuk dostudur — temizlenmesi kolay, zararsız, parlak.

**White Fence** — Beyaz çit, bahçeyi tamamen çevreler. Çubuklar yuvarlak, beyaz ve yumuşaktır. Çitin yüksekliği çocukların göğüs hizasındadır — yeterince yüksek ki bahçe hissedilsin, yeterince alçak ki çocuklar dışarıyı görebilsin. Çit, güvenli bir sınır oluşturur ama kapatmaz. Her çubuk arasındaki boşluk, çocukların dışarıyı görmesini ve bahçenin geniş hissedilmesini sağlar.

**Trees** — İki ağaç, bahçenin sol ve sağ kenarlarında durur. Ağaçlar taze yeşil yapraklı, yumuşak gövdeli ve geniş taçlıdır. Gölgeleri yumuşak ve yuvarlaktır — sert veya karanlık gölge oluşturmazlar. Ağaçlar, bahçenin doğal çerçevesini tamamlar. Yaprakları hafifçe sallanır — rüzgarın varlığını hissettirir ama asla sert veya tehditkar bir şekilde değil.

### Spatial Depth

Painting Garden, üç katmanlı bir derinlik yapısına sahiptir:

- **Ön plan** — Çimenler, küçük çiçekler, boya lekeleri — detay ve doku
- **Orta plan** — Çiçek bölgeleri, tuval, boya kapları, çit — hikaye alanı
- **Arka plan** — Ağaçlar, gökyüzü, uzak tepeler — atmosfer ve genişlik

Bu katmanlama, bahçenin derin hissedilmesini sağlar. Her karede en az iki katman görünür — bu, bahçenin "yaşayan" bir mekan olduğunu gösterir.

## Forbidden

### Visual

- Sharp edges, thorns, thistles — sivri kenarlar, dikenler, tehlikeli öğeler
- Realistic art supplies (professional brushes, toxic paints) — profesyonel boya malzemeleri
- Modern objects (easels with metal legs, plastic containers) — metal ayaklar, plastik kaplar
- Neon colours, harsh contrasts — neon renkler, sert kontrastlar
- Dark shadows, scary lighting — karanlık gölgeler, korkutucu ışık
- Crowded scenes — kalabalık sahneler
- Fast-moving objects — hızlı hareket eden nesneler
- Text, watermarks, signs — yazı, filigran, tabelalar
- Unfinished or messy paintings — bitmemiş veya dağınık resimler

### Behavioural

- Competition, racing, scoring — yarış, rekabet, puanlama
- Aggressive movement — agresif hareket
- Loud shouting — yüksek sesle bağırma
- Throwing paint — boya fırlatma
- Messy or destructive behaviour — dağınık veya yıkıcı davranış

## Emotional Tone

> The emotional register for Painting Garden. Every scene must feel within this range.

### Emotion Map

| Emotion | Intensity | When |
|---------|-----------|------|
| Curiosity | Medium | İlk kez geldiğinde, renkleri keşfederken |
| Joy | High | Renkleri keşfettiğinde, yeni bir renk karıştırdığında |
| Wonder | High | Güzel bir resim yaptığında, doğadaki renkleri fark ettiğinde |
| Calm | Medium | Boyama yaparken, fırçayı yavaşça hareket ettirirken |
| Connection | High | Birlikte sanat yaparken, birbirinin resmini beğendiğinde |

### Emotional Arc

A typical Painting Garden episode follows this emotional arc:

```
Curiosity (entry) → Joy (discovery) → Calm (painting) → Wonder (creation) → Connection (sharing)
```

This arc is natural and expected. The world guides children from initial curiosity through joyful discovery, calm creation, and finally the wonder of having made something beautiful — and the connection of sharing it with a friend.

### Forbidden Emotional States

```
✗ Frustration — the world is always successful and rewarding
✗ Fear — the world is always safe
✗ Anger — the world is always peaceful
✗ Sadness — the world is always cheerful
✗ Competition — the world is always cooperative
✗ Impatience — the world encourages slow, careful work
```

### Emotional Continuity

The emotional tone must remain consistent within a scene. If a scene begins with curiosity, it should progress through joy and calm to connection — not jump to frustration or fear. Emotional continuity ensures that the world feels safe and predictable for the preschool audience.

## Production Notes

> Practical production guidance for Painting Garden. These notes ensure consistency and quality across all episodes.

### Best Characters

- **Sofia** (sanatçı) — natural-born artist, çiçekleri boyar, renkleri keşfeder, yaratıcılığı paylaşır. Sofia Painting Garden'ın doğal karakteridir — burada doğar, burada büyür. Onun fırça tutuşu, renk seçimi ve paylaşma içgüdüsü bu dünyanın ruhunu yansıtır.
- **Kiko** (arkadaş) — meraklı, birlikte boyar, renkleri keşfeder, Sophia'ya yardım eder. Kiko, Sofia'nın yaratıcılığını tamamlar — birlikte daha güzel resim yaparlar. Kiko'nun merakı, renkleri keşfetme sürecini doğal ve eğlenceli kılar.
- **Mimi** (helper) — boyalara yardım eder, malzemeleri taşır, düzen sağlar. Mimi, bahçenin düzenini korur — boya kaplarını sıralar, fırçaları hazırlar, temizlik yapar. Onun varlığı, bahçenin düzenli ve davetkar kalmasını sağlar.
- **Amara** (observer) — izler, katılır, sonunda birlikte resim yapar. Amara, bahçeye dışarıdan bakarak başlar, sonra yavaşça katılır. Onun hikayesi, cesaret ve keşif üzerine kuruludur — "ben de yapabilirim" hissi.

### Avoid

- Fazla hareket veya kalabalık — bahçe sakin ve huzurlu olmalı
- Gerçekçi boya malzemeleri — pastel, yumuşak, oyuncak gibi boyalar
- Yarış veya rekabet — herkes kendi hızında boyar
- Dağınık veya yıkıcı davranış — bahçe tertemiz ve düzenli
- Korkutucu veya tehditkar unsurlar — bahçe her zaman güvenli
- Profesyonel sanatçı imajı — çocuklar doğal ve samimi yaratır

### Typical Duration

30–60 saniye per sahne. Painting Garden sahneleri kısa ama yoğundur — her saniye bir renk keşfi veya yaratıcılık anı taşır. 90 saniyelik bölümler için 3–4 sahne idealdir.

### Best Emotional Themes

- **Renk keşfi** — her bölgenin rengini adlandırma, renkleri karıştırma
- **Birlikte yaratma** — aynı tuvalde çalışma, birbirinin fikrine saygı
- **Güzelliği paylaşma** — boyadığı çiçeği arkadaşına gösterme
- **Doğadan sanat** — yaprak, taş, çiçekle resim yapma
- **Kendine güven** — "ben de yapabilirim" hissi
- **Sabır** — yavaş yavaş, dikkatlice boyama
- **İletişim** — birlikte karar verme, fikir paylaşma

## Lighting Identity

### Light Direction

Primary light comes from the upper left in all standard daytime scenes. This is the canonical sun position for Painting Garden and must not change between shots in the same scene.

| Time | Direction | Quality |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Morning | Upper left, warm | Softest — golden, gentle |
| Midday | Top centre, neutral | Brightest — even, clean |
| Evening | Upper left, golden-orange | Warmest — long soft shadows |
| Night | Top, cool moonlight | Softest — pale blue-white |

### Softness

All light on Painting Garden is soft. There are no hard shadows, no sharp shadow edges, no spotlight effects. The light feels like it is being filtered through a thin layer of high cloud at all times — bright but never harsh. This softness ensures that the flower colours remain true and the painting activity feels gentle and safe.

### Ambient Fill

A gentle ambient fill always lifts the shadow side of every object and character. No side of any subject falls into true darkness. The darkest shadow value on Painting Garden is approximately 70% of the highlight value — never lower. This ensures that even the red and purple flower zones remain readable and inviting, not dark or heavy.

### Shadow Density

| Shadow Type | Density | Notes |
|-------------|---------|-------|
| Character shadow on grass | Very soft, 20–30% opacity | Short — never longer than character height |
| Flower shadow detail | Barely visible | Ground stays bright and readable |
| Tree shadow on garden | Soft dappled | Gentle, round shapes — not harsh or angular |
| Fence shadow | Minimal | White fence reflects light, minimal shadow |

### Exposure Behaviour

The world is always slightly bright — not overexposed, but lifted. Midtones are open and readable. Nothing is crushed in the darks. Highlights are soft — never blown. The exposure ensures that every colour in the flower zones is visible and true, and that the painting activity is clearly readable.

### Continuity Rules

- Light direction must not change between shots in the same scene
- If a scene begins in morning light, every shot in that scene stays in morning light
- Tree shadows are soft and dappled — never harsh or angular
- Character lighting must match environment lighting — no isolated character illumination
- Flower colours must remain true under all lighting conditions

## Colour Identity

### White Balance

Warm daylight — approximately 5500K. Slightly warm, never cool or blue-shifted in daytime scenes. The warmth ensures that the flower colours feel inviting and the painting activity feels safe and comfortable.

### Exposure

+0.3 to +0.5 stops above neutral. The world is bright, open, and lifted. This ensures that even the darker purple and blue flower zones remain readable and cheerful.

### Contrast

Low contrast. The distance between the brightest and darkest values is narrow. Everything reads clearly. Nothing disappears into shadow. This low contrast ensures that the painting activity is always visible and the colour zones remain distinct.

### Saturation

Medium-high. Colours are present, vivid, and readable — this is a colour-focused world. The palette feels hand-painted, not digital, but the colours are intentionally bright and cheerful. This is one of the most colourful worlds in Pompom Hills.

### Primary palette

| Grass | Warm green | #81C784 |

### Colour Relationships

The four flower colours are chosen for maximum contrast and harmony. Red and green (complementary), blue and orange (complementary), yellow and purple (complementary). This ensures that each zone is visually distinct and that the overall garden feels balanced and vibrant.

### Highlight Behaviour

Highlights are soft and rolled — they never clip to pure white. The brightest point in any frame is the canvas or the fence, which should feel like soft cotton, not a white wall. The white fence and canvas provide natural highlight anchors in the composition.

### Shadow Behaviour

Shadows are lifted and warm — never cold, never black. The darkest value in any standard daytime frame is a warm mid-grey, approximately #9E9E9E or lighter. Even the purple flower zone, the darkest colour, remains cheerful and inviting.

## Production Summary

Painting Garden is a canonical reusable world. Every time it appears — regardless of episode, regardless of characters, regardless of story — it must feel like exactly the same place.

The world has one defining quality: it belongs to colour. More colour than neutral. More creative than passive. More shared than solitary. Characters come here to paint, discover colours, and share beauty with each other. The garden itself is a simple enclosed space — four colour zones, a canvas, and a fence. Everything else is the children's creative energy.

For production, Painting Garden is low-complexity and high-consistency. It has defined colour zones, a locked layout, a clear camera language, and a simple prop set. This makes it one of the fastest worlds to generate reliably — but only if the identity rules are respected on every frame.

The single most common failure point is colour zones mixing. Always verify this first. The second most common failure is realistic art supplies appearing — the world must remain child-friendly and toy-like.

Every future episode using Painting Garden should load the Hero View as its master reference, verify the Colour Identity before accepting any frame, and run the Production QA checklist before signing off on any shot. The world does not change. Only the story does.

## World Navigation

> The canonical movement flow through Painting Garden. Every episode using this world should follow this navigation unless the story explicitly requires a variation.

```
Little Forest ←→ Painting Garden ←→ Art Corner
     (kuzey)        (merkez)          (doğu)
```

### Entry Point

Characters always enter Painting Garden from the surrounding paths — either from Little Forest (north) or Art Corner (east). They are never already inside the garden when a scene begins. Arrival is part of the world experience — the moment they see the colour zones and the canvas.

### Canonical Transition Sequence

```
1. Entry — characters appear at the garden entrance
       ↓
2. Garden Discovery — characters see the colour zones and canvas
       ↓
3. Colour Exploration — characters move between colour zones
       ↓
4. Painting Activity — characters paint at the canvas or with flowers
       ↓
5. Natural Exit — characters leave the garden the same way they entered
```

### Entry Points

| From | To | Path |
|------|----|------|
| Little Forest | Painting Garden | Patika ile kuzeyden giriş — yeşil ağaçlar arasından |
| Art Corner | Painting Garden | Patika ile doğudan giriş — renkli çiçekler selamlar |

### Garden Discovery

Garden discovery is a canonical emotional beat. As the character enters the garden, the colour zones appear one by one — red, blue, yellow, purple. The canvas stands in the centre-right, waiting to be painted. This moment should never be cut short or skipped. It is the "wow" moment of the world.

### Colour Exploration

Characters naturally move between colour zones, discovering each colour. They may touch the flowers, name the colours, or compare them. This exploration is the core activity of the world — it is how children learn about colour through direct experience.

### Painting Activity

The painting activity is the climax of the world visit. Characters pick up brushes, dip them in paint, and begin creating on the canvas. This is the moment where creativity happens — where the child's energy fills the blank canvas with colour and meaning.

### Natural Exit

Characters exit the same way they entered — through the paths. The exit is always calm and unhurried. A closing wide shot of the garden after characters have left (or are small figures walking away) is a canonical closing image.

### Canonical vs. Optional Transitions

| Transition | Status | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Entry → Discovery → Exploration → Painting | Canonical | Use in every episode that visits Painting Garden |
| Painting → Exit | Canonical | Standard story structure |
| Starting inside the garden with no entry | Optional | Only when the entry is shown in a previous episode of a multi-part story |
| Teleporting between worlds without a transition shot | Forbidden | Always show the approach |

## View Transition Rules

> How camera views should progress during a standard Painting Garden episode. This is the canonical shot progression.

### Standard Shot Sequence

Default episode flow — alternative openings are permitted when story continuity requires them (e.g. a character already established inside the garden in a prior scene does not need to enter again).

```
Environment wide (establish — no character)
    ↓
Entry shot (character arrives at garden entrance)
    ↓
Discovery shot (character sees colour zones and canvas)
    ↓
Exploration shot (character moves between colour zones)
    ↓
Activity shot (character paints at canvas)
    ↓
Close shot (emotional peak — face, brush, colour)
    ↓
Closing wide (exit or garden hold)
```

### When to Use Each Shot Type

| Shot Type | When | Notes |
|-----------|------|-------|
| Environment wide | Scene opening — before any character appears | Always first |
| Entry shot | Character arrives at garden entrance | Can be skipped when continuity permits |
| Discovery shot | Character sees colour zones for the first time | Always present on first visit |
| Exploration shot | Character moves between colour zones | Optional — depends on story |
| Activity shot | Character paints at canvas | Always present — core activity |
| Close shot | Emotional peak | Optional but recommended |
| Closing wide | Scene end — exit or garden hold | Always end wider than the previous shot |

### Transition Guidance

- **Environment wide → Entry shot:** Cut or slow dissolve. Never jump straight to painting without the entry.
- **Entry → Discovery:** The garden reveals itself as the character enters. This is a key emotional transition — give it time.
- **Discovery → Exploration:** The character moves between colour zones. Allow natural movement.
- **Exploration → Activity:** The character reaches the canvas and begins painting. This is the climax.
- **Activity → Close:** Slow push in or direct cut to close-up. The detail should feel like a natural zoom of attention, not a hard jump.
- **Close → Closing wide:** Pull back slowly or cut to wide. Never end on a close shot — always return to the world.
- **Closing wide:** Let the world breathe before cutting.

### Transitions to Avoid

```
✗ Opening directly on a character close-up — always establish the world first
✗ Cutting from environment wide to close shot without a discovery shot in between
✗ Ending on a medium or close shot — always close wider
✗ Two consecutive close shots without a wider shot between them
✗ Sudden cut from the garden to a completely different world with no transition
✗ Skipping the painting activity — it is the core of the world
```

| Wide with two characters | 15–20% | All 4 zones | Both characters visible full-body |

### Medium Shots

Characters occupy 30–45% of the frame. Colour zones remain visible in the background. The canvas, paint pots, or flowers are visible in the frame. Characters are never the only thing in the frame.

| Shot Type | Character Frame % | Flowers Visible | Notes |
|-----------|:-----------------:|:---------------:|-------|
| Single character medium | 30–35% | 2+ zones | Flowers behind character always |
| Two character medium | 40–45% | 2+ zones | Both characters visible with flowers |

### Close Shots

Characters occupy 60–75% of the frame. Even in close shots, flowers or colour are always visible. A close-up of a face painting should still show the flower colours as background — never a blurred or neutral background.

| Shot Type | Character Frame % | Colour Visible | Notes |

| Face close-up | 65–75% | 20%+ | Flower colours as background, not bokeh |
| Hand or brush detail | 50–60% | 15%+ | Paint colours visible, flowers glimpsed |

## Common Generation Failures

> The most frequent AI generation problems when producing Painting Garden. Each entry explains how to recognise it, why it matters, and how to fix it.

### Failure 01 — Flower Colours Mixed in Zones

**How to recognise:** A single flower zone contains multiple colours — red and blue flowers in the same cluster, or yellow flowers appearing in the purple zone.

**Why it breaks identity:** Painting Garden's visual identity is built on the four distinct colour zones. When colours mix, the world loses its structure and becomes chaotic. The colour discovery theme — where each zone represents a different colour — breaks down completely.

**How to correct:** Regenerate with explicit instruction: each flower zone must contain only one colour — red, blue, yellow, or purple. Verify that no zone contains flowers from another colour. Reject any frame where colour zones are mixed.

---

### Failure 02 — Realistic Art Supplies

**How to recognise:** The paint pots look like real professional paint tubes, the brush has metal ferrule, the easel has metal legs, or any modern art supply appears.

**Why it breaks identity:** Painting Garden is a child-friendly, safe world. Realistic art supplies introduce professional adult associations — toxic materials, sharp edges, complex tools. The world must feel like a place where any child can pick up a brush and paint.

**How to correct:** Regenerate with explicit instruction: all art supplies must be child-friendly, pastel, rounded, and toy-like. No metal, no professional materials, no modern objects. The paint pots should look like soft rounded containers, not tubes or jars.

---

### Failure 03 — Characters Not Painting

**How to recognise:** Characters are standing idle, walking around, or doing activities unrelated to painting. Their hands are empty or holding non-painting objects.

**Why it breaks identity:** Painting Garden's purpose is painting and colour discovery. When characters are not engaged in painting, the world's purpose is not being served. The scene feels like a garden with characters in it, not a painting activity.

**How to correct:** Regenerate with explicit instruction: characters must be actively painting, holding brushes, mixing colours, or examining flowers. Their hands should be engaged with the painting activity at all times during the scene.

---

### Failure 04 — Too Many Characters

**How to recognise:** More than 3 characters appear in the garden simultaneously. The scene feels crowded and chaotic.

**Why it breaks identity:** Painting Garden is an intimate, calm space. Too many characters break the peaceful atmosphere and make the scene feel like a party or competition rather than a quiet painting activity.

**How to correct:** Reduce to maximum 2–3 characters. The garden should feel spacious and calm, with room for each character to have their own painting space. If more characters are needed, use sequential shots rather than putting everyone in one frame.

---

### Failure 05 — Colours Too Dull or Too Vivid

**How to recognise:** The overall image feels muted and grey (dull) or neon and oversaturated (too vivid). Neither matches the canonical pastel-but-vibrant palette.

**Why it breaks identity:** Painting Garden must feel colourful and cheerful but not overwhelming. Dull colours make the world feel sad and lifeless. Oversaturated colours feel digital and harsh, breaking the hand-painted aesthetic.

**How to correct:** For dull images: increase saturation slightly, warm the white balance, and ensure flower colours are vivid. For oversaturated images: reduce saturation, soften the contrast, and ensure the palette stays within the pastel range. Always verify against the canonical hex values.

---

### Failure 06 — Fence Missing or Wrong Colour

**How to recognise:** The white fence is absent from the scene, or it appears in a different colour (grey, brown, colourful).

**Why it breaks identity:** The white fence is a key continuity marker and a defining element of Painting Garden's visual identity. It provides the safe boundary that makes the garden feel enclosed and protected. Without it, the garden loses its sense of place.

**How to correct:** Regenerate with explicit instruction: the white fence (#FAFAFA) must surround the garden in every shot. The fence should be visible in at least one corner or edge of the frame. If the fence is missing, reject the frame immediately.

---

### Failure 07 — Canvas Missing

**How to recognise:** The canvas and easel are not visible in the scene, or they are in the wrong position.

**Why it breaks identity:** The canvas is the focal point of Painting Garden — it represents the yaratıcılık (creativity) centre of the world. Without it, the garden is just a garden with flowers, not a painting space.

**How to correct:** Regenerate with explicit instruction: the canvas must be visible in the centre-right position. The canvas should be unpainted, white, with a simple wood frame. If the canvas is missing or in the wrong position, reject the frame.

---

### Failure 08 — Sharp Edges or Dangerous Elements

**How to recognise:** Thorns, spikes, sharp corners, angular shapes, or any potentially dangerous element appears in the garden.

**Why it breaks identity:** Painting Garden is a safe, gentle world for preschool children. Any sharp or dangerous element breaks the safety promise and creates anxiety. The world must feel completely safe and inviting.

**How to correct:** Regenerate with explicit instruction: all elements must be rounded, soft, and gentle. No thorns, no spikes, no sharp corners. The fence should have rounded tops, the flowers should be round and soft, and the trees should have smooth trunks.

## Video Generation Rules

> The reusable standard for animated scenes on Painting Garden. These rules ensure the world feels identical in both still images and animated video.

### Flower Movement

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Type | Gentle sway, low amplitude — flowers move slightly in wind |
| Speed | One full sway cycle every 4–5 seconds |
| Direction | Follows wind direction — consistent across the frame |
| Pattern | Gentle ripple across flower zones, not chaotic |
| Amplitude | Flowers move no more than 15% of their height — subtle, not wild |

### Wind Intensity

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Visual intensity | Low — hair moves gently, clothing barely stirs |
| Hair movement | Slow, smooth sweep — one direction, not flapping |
| Consistency | Wind direction and intensity do not change within a scene |
| Gusting | No gusts — wind is continuous and even |

### Camera Movement Speed

| Movement Type | Speed Rule |
|---------------|-----------|
| Pan | No faster than 5% of frame width per second |
| Tilt | No faster than 4% of frame height per second |
| Push in | No faster than 2% of subject size increase per second |
| Pull back | Same speed as push in — symmetrical |
| Static hold | Minimum 3 seconds before any movement begins |

All camera movement must feel as if the camera is breathing rather than moving. If the movement is perceptible as "the camera is moving," it is too fast.

### Dialogue Pacing

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Cut timing | Never cut during a line — always cut between lines |
| Pause after line | Minimum 1 second of silence before the next line begins |
| Silence as dialogue | Silence after a painting moment is intentional — hold for 2–3 seconds |
| Reaction shots | Allow 1.5–2 seconds for a character to react before cutting away |

### Environmental Rhythm

Painting Garden has a single ambient rhythm: slow, steady, and breathing. Every moving element follows this rhythm:

| Element | Rhythm |
|---------|--------|
| Flowers | Slow sway — 4–5 second cycle |
| Wind audio | Rises slightly every 10–12 seconds, falls back gently |
| Bird call | Once every 10–15 seconds — irregular but not surprising |
| Camera | Moves on the same rhythm as the flowers — never fights it |
| Brush strokes | Gentle, rhythmic — follows the character's painting pace |

When all elements are in rhythm, Painting Garden feels alive but still. When any element breaks rhythm — a sudden camera move, a fast wind, a sharp sound — the world loses its identity. Rhythm is the final check before approving any animated scene.

### Still Image vs. Video Consistency

Every frame from an animated scene must pass the same Production QA as a still image. Animation does not exempt a frame from identity checks. The canonical colour palette, flower zones, fence, and camera/lighting identity apply equally to every individual frame of any animated sequence.
