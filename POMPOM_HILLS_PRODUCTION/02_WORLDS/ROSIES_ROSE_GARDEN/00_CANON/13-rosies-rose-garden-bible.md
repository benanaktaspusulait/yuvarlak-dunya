## 1. Overview

Rosie'nin Gül Bahçesi, Pompom Hills'in en romantik ve zarif mekanıdır. Kalp şeklinde çiçek tarhları, renkli güllerle dolu dairesel bir labirenttir. Her adım yeni bir güzellik, her köşe yeni bir keşif sunar. Dikensiz tasarımı, çocukların güvenle ve huzurla doğanın zarafetini deneyimlemesini sağlar. Merkezdeki beyaz çeşme, bahçenin kalbi gibi durur ve sakin bir su sesiyle mekanı doldurur.

Bu mekan, güzellik ve zarafetin somut karşılığıdır. Çocuklar burada çiçeklerin dilini keşfeder, renklerin dansını izler ve doğanın en zarif yüzüyle tanışır. Labirent yapısı keşif duygusunu besler, kalp şeklinde tarhlar ise sevgi ve şefkat hissini taşır. Rosie'nin Gül Bahçesi, Pompom Hills'in "güzellik durağıdır" — her ziyaretçi buradan daha mutlu, daha huzurlu ve daha meraklı ayrılr.

---

## 2. Purpose

Güzel olan şeyleri izleme, keşfetme ve hayal gücüne taşıma anları sunar. Çocuklar burada çiçeklerin güzelliğiyle tanışır, renklerin dilini öğrenir ve doğanın zarafetini hisseder. Labirent yapısı keşif duygusunu besler, merkez çeşme ise sakin bir mola noktası oluşturur.

Bu mekan, çocukların "güzellik" kavramını somut olarak deneyimlediği yerdir. Bir gülün yaprağına dokunmak, bir kelebeğin kanat izini takip etmek, bir çeşmenin suyunu dinlemek — hepsi burada gerçek olur. Rosie'nin Gül Bahçesi, çocukların estetik duygusunu geliştiren, zarafet ve hassasiyet hissini aşılayan bir üretim alanıdır.

---

## 3. Why This World Exists ⭐

Doğanın zarafetini ve renk zenginliğini çocukların hizmetine sunar. Dikensiz güller ve kalp şeklinde çiçek tarhları, çocukların güvenle güzellikle etkileşime girmesini sağlar. Labirent yapısı, keşif duygusunu pekiştirir. Bu mekan, çocukların "güzel olan şeylere dokunulabilir" hissini yaşadığı tek Pompom Hills mekanıdır.

Rosie'nin Gül Bahçesi, çocukların doğayla kurduğu ilişkinin en hassas halini temsil eder. Burada çiçek koparmak yoktur — izlemek, koklamak, hayal etmek vardır. Dikensiz tasarım, çocukların güvenle yaklaşmasını sağlar. Kalp şeklinde tarhlar, sevginin doğada var olduğunu gösterir. Labirent yapısı ise keşfetmenin, bulmanın, ulaşmanın hazzını yaşatır.

## 4. Emotional Purpose

### Why does this place exist emotionally?

Rosie'nin Gül Bahçesi, güzellik ve zarafeti temsil eder. Pastel renkler, yuvarlak formlar ve sakin atmosfer, çocukların iç huzurunu besler. Bir çiçeğe bakmak, bir kelebeği izlemek, bir çeşmenin suyunu dinlemek — hepsi sakinlik ve hayranlık anlarıdır.

### How do children feel when they arrive?

Meraklı, huzurlu, hayran. Bahçenin güzelliği karşısında sessiz bir şaşırma hissederler. Pastel renkler ve çiçek kokusu onları sarar, labirentin gizemi keşfetme isteği uyandırır. Diken olmaması güven hissini pekiştirir — burası tamamen güvenlidir.

### What stories naturally belong here?

Kelebek izleme, gül koklama, renk sayma, labirentte gezme, çeşme suyunu izleme, yaprak toplama, bahçe güzelleştirme, çiçeklerle sohbet etme, dostluk kurma, güzel anılar biriktirme.

### What emotional experiences does this world support?

Güzellik · Zarafet · Huzur · Keşif · Güven · Hayal gücü

## 17. Visual Richness & World Charm

> See `POMPOM_HILLS_PRODUCTION/00_GLOBAL_RULES/WORLD_STANDARDS/GLOBAL_ENVIRONMENT_STANDARD.md` § Visual Richness Principle.
> Rosie's Rose Garden must NOT be reduced to "a flower field." Reject Rosie's Rose Garden if it is only a generic meadow, has no heart-shaped beds, or the roses are thorny.

Rosie's Rose Garden is the heart-shaped rose maze of Pompom Hills. It should feel like: **"I want to walk through and find the giant heart of flowers."**

### Iconic Landmark

RG-BEDS-HEART-01 — the twin heart-shaped flower beds at the Heart Centre (one pink roses, one red roses, with a small yellow flower cluster at the join). This is the primary visual anchor: the object that turns "a flower garden" into "the garden with the heart made of flowers."

### Allowed Charm Details

**Landmark Charm**
- RG-BEDS-HEART-01, the twin heart-shaped rose beds at the Heart Centre — already a Locked Element, see World Identity Lock below
- RG-FOUNTAIN-01, the small white rounded fountain at the centre — water flows gently
- RG-FENCE-WHITE-01, the low white rounded fence with ball-top finials — surrounds the garden

**Surface Charm**
- the soft winding dirt path that curves through the rose beds (Rose Path)
- the heart-shaped bed soil pattern, barely visible beneath the flowers
- tiny rounded pebbles along the path edges

**Small Prop Charm**
- RG-BUTTERFLY-01 butterflies drifting gently through the garden
- RG-STEPPING-STONE-01, the flat resting stone at the Butterfly Resting Spot, surrounded by yellow roses
- small white and yellow wildflowers scattered between the rose beds

**Motion Charm**
- rose petals occasionally drifting from the bushes
- RG-BUTTERFLY-01 butterflies drifting gently through the garden
- fountain water flowing in a gentle arc
- gentle breeze making the roses sway slightly

### Forbidden Clutter

```
✗ thorns anywhere on any plant
✗ square or oval flower beds — the heart shape must always read clearly
✗ dull or faded colour — palette must stay vivid pastel
✗ broken, wilted, or dead flowers
✗ dark or high-contrast lighting
✗ sharp corners anywhere
✗ tall or decorative fences — always low and rounded
✗ oversized scale — the garden stays child-sized
✗ generic meadow with no structure
✗ realistic botanical detail — must stay painterly
```

### Thumbnail Hook

A close view of the twin pink-and-red heart-shaped rose beds glowing in warm light, with a butterfly resting nearby and the white fountain visible in the background.

### Child Recognition Test

**"The garden with the heart made of flowers."**

### Hero View Must Show

- heart-shaped flower beds (twin hearts)
- white rounded fence surrounding the garden
- winding paths between the rose beds
- small white fountain at the centre
- butterflies drifting through
- warm, pastel, painterly atmosphere

If Rosie's Rose Garden looks like any generic flower field, reject it.

## 18. World Identity Lock

> These elements define the canonical visual identity of Rosie's Rose Garden. Every future generation must preserve them exactly. **The Canon Hero View (`01-hero-view.png`) is the primary reference — it overrides descriptive ambiguity.**

### Canonical Hero View Rule

Before generating any new Rosie's Rose Garden scene, match the Canon Hero View on these six points:

1. Heart-shaped beds — twin hearts clearly readable, one pink, one red
2. White fence — low, rounded, surrounding the garden
3. Fountain position — centre of the garden, small and white
4. Path layout — winding through the rose beds
5. Colour palette — pastel pink, red, yellow, green, white
6. Overall atmosphere — warm, painterly, child-sized

If the generated garden does not immediately resemble the Hero View on first glance, reject without further evaluation.

### Locked Elements

| Element | Canonical Definition | Locked |
|---------|---------------------|:------:|
| Heart-shaped beds | Twin hearts at centre — one pink roses, one red roses, yellow join | 🔒 |
| Rose style | Round, plump, thornless, painterly — never realistic or detailed | 🔒 |
| Fence | Low white rounded fence with ball-top finials — surrounds entire garden | 🔒 |
| Fountain | Small white rounded fountain at garden centre — gentle water flow | 🔒 |

## 28. World Navigation

> The canonical movement flow through Rosie's Rose Garden. Every episode using this world should follow this navigation unless the story explicitly requires a variation.

### Entry Point

Characters always enter Rosie's Rose Garden through the main gate — a low white arched opening in the fence. They are never already deep in the garden when a scene begins. Arrival through the gate is part of the world experience.

### Canonical Transition Sequence

```
1. Gate Approach — characters see the white fence and gate
       ↓
2. Gate Entry — characters pass through the low white gate
       ↓
3. Maze Exploration — characters walk through the winding paths
       ↓
4. Heart Discovery — characters find the twin heart-shaped beds
       ↓
5. Fountain Arrival — characters reach the centre, sit by the fountain
       ↓
6. Natural Exit — characters walk back through the maze and out the gate
```

### Gate Entry

The gate is a low white arched opening with ball-top finials. It is small and inviting — a child can walk through without ducking. The camera typically follows from behind as the garden is revealed for the first time.

### Maze Exploration

The maze paths wind gently between rose beds. There are no dead ends — every path leads somewhere beautiful. The camera follows characters as they discover new colour zones, new butterflies, new scents.

### Heart Discovery

Heart discovery is a canonical emotional beat. As the character reaches the Heart Centre, the twin heart-shaped beds are revealed — one pink, one red, joined by yellow. Camera widens to show both hearts. This moment should never be cut short or skipped.

### Fountain Arrival

The fountain is the garden's heart — a small white rounded structure with gentle water flow. Characters sit on the grass nearby, watch the water, listen to its sound. This is where the episode's main story content happens.

### Natural Exit

Characters exit the same way they entered — through the maze paths and out the gate. The exit is always calm and unhurried. A closing wide shot of the garden after characters have left is a canonical closing image.

### Canonical vs. Optional Transitions

| Transition | Status | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Gate → Maze → Heart → Fountain | Canonical | Use in every episode that visits Rosie's Rose Garden |
| Fountain → Maze → Gate Exit | Canonical | Standard story structure |
| Starting at fountain with no exploration | Optional | Only when the exploration is shown in a prior episode |
| Teleporting between worlds without a transition shot | Forbidden | Always show the approach |

## 32. Common Generation Failures

> The most frequent AI generation problems when producing Rosie's Rose Garden. Each entry explains how to recognise it, why it matters, and how to fix it.

### Failure 01 — Thorns on Roses

**How to recognise:** Rose stems have visible thorns, thistles, or sharp projections. The roses appear realistic rather than painterly.

**Why it breaks identity:** Thornless roses are a foundational promise of this world. Thorns signal danger, which is the exact opposite of what this garden represents. Children must feel safe approaching every flower.

**How to correct:** Regenerate with explicit instruction: all roses must be completely thornless, with smooth round stems. Reject any frame where thorns are visible on any plant.

### Failure 02 — Heart Shape Unreadable

**How to recognise:** The flower beds appear as generic ovals, circles, or amorphous blobs. The heart shape is not clearly readable from the camera angle.

**Why it breaks identity:** The heart-shaped beds are the primary visual anchor of the world. If they cannot be read as hearts, the garden loses its defining feature and becomes a generic flower field.

**How to correct:** Regenerate with explicit instruction: twin heart-shaped flower beds, clearly readable from this angle. Adjust camera angle if needed to make the heart shape visible. Reject any frame where the heart shape is ambiguous.

### Failure 03 — Dark or Moody Lighting

**How to recognise:** The garden has dramatic shadows, dark areas, or a moody atmosphere. The lighting feels cinematic rather than warm and safe.

**Why it breaks identity:** Rosie's Rose Garden is a safe, warm, inviting world. Dark or moody lighting carries emotional weight — mystery, danger, unease — none of which belong here.

**How to correct:** Warm the white balance, lift the shadows, reduce contrast. Regenerate with explicit instruction: warm, soft, even lighting throughout the garden.

### Failure 04 — Modern Objects in Frame

**How to recognise:** A fence post, path light, garden tool, watering can, or any man-made structure appears anywhere in the frame — including the background.

**Why it breaks identity:** Rosie's Rose Garden has no infrastructure. Any modern object immediately pulls the viewer out of the Pompom Hills world.

**How to correct:** Regenerate with explicit instruction: no modern objects anywhere in frame. Check the full frame including background before accepting.

### Failure 05 — Oversaturated or Wrong Rose Colour

**How to recognise:** The roses appear vivid neon pink, blood red, or harsh yellow. They do not match the soft pastel palette.

**Why it breaks identity:** Rose colour is a primary continuity marker. Oversaturation breaks the pastel feel of the entire Pompom Hills visual language.

**How to correct:** Reduce saturation, warm the colours, and lighten toward the canonical hex values. Regenerate with explicit colour instruction if needed.

### Failure 06 — Fence Missing or Wrong

**How to recognise:** The white fence is absent, too tall, too decorative, or the wrong colour. The garden feels open and undefined.

**Why it breaks identity:** The fence defines the garden's boundary and creates the enclosed, safe feeling. Without it, the garden loses its sense of place.

**How to correct:** Regenerate with explicit instruction: low white rounded fence with ball-top finials, surrounding the entire garden.

### Failure 07 — Garden Too Large or Too Small

**How to recognise:** The garden appears as a vast botanical garden or a tiny flower box. The scale feels wrong for children.

**Why it breaks identity:** The garden must feel child-sized — a place a small child could walk through comfortably. Too large feels overwhelming; too small feels cramped.

**How to correct:** Regenerate with explicit instruction: child-sized garden, low fence, proportionate fountain, small heart beds. Verify character-to-garden scale before accepting.

### Failure 08 — Generic Meadow Instead of Garden

**How to recognise:** The frame shows a flat flower field with no structure, no fence, no paths, no heart beds. Just flowers on grass.

**Why it breaks identity:** Without the structural elements (fence, paths, heart beds, fountain), the garden has no identity. It could be any flower field anywhere.

**How to correct:** Regenerate with explicit instruction: enclosed garden with white fence, winding paths, twin heart-shaped beds, and central fountain. Load Hero View as reference.

| Speed | Slow, drifting — never fast or erratic |
| Pattern | Gentle arcs and loops — not straight lines |
| Wing animation | Slow open-close cycle — one flap every 1–2 seconds |
| Landing | Butterflies may land on flowers or characters — gentle touchdown |
| Colour | Pink-purple gradient — consistent across all butterflies |

### Fountain Movement

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Water flow | Gentle arc — constant, never splashing |
| Sound | Soft trickling — never loud or rushing |
| Splash | Minimal — water lands softly |
| Ripple | Small concentric ripples in the basin |

### Petal Movement

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Type | Occasional petal drift — one petal every 5–8 seconds |
| Speed | Very slow — gentle float downward |
| Direction | Follows wind — left to right |
| Landing | Petals land softly on grass or path — no bouncing |

### Wind Intensity

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Visual intensity | Low — roses sway gently, hair barely moves |
| Hair movement | Slow, smooth sweep — one direction, not flapping |
| Consistency | Wind direction and intensity do not change within a scene |
| Gusting | No gusts — wind is continuous and even |

### Camera Movement Speed

| Movement Type | Speed Rule |
|---------------|-----------|
| Pan | No faster than 4% of frame width per second |
| Push in | No faster than 1.5% of subject size increase per second |
| Pull back | Same speed as push in — symmetrical |
| Static hold | Minimum 3 seconds before any movement begins |

All camera movement must feel as if the camera is breathing rather than moving. If the movement is perceptible as "the camera is moving," it is too fast.

### Dialogue Pacing

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Cut timing | Never cut during a line — always cut between lines |
| Pause after line | Minimum 1 second of silence before the next line begins |
| Silence as dialogue | Silence after a beauty moment is intentional — hold for 2–3 seconds |
| Reaction shots | Allow 1.5–2 seconds for a character to react before cutting away |

The pace of dialogue on Rosie's Rose Garden is slower than other worlds. The garden demands it. Lines are shorter. Pauses are longer. The beauty fills the silence.

### Environmental Rhythm

Rosie's Rose Garden has a single ambient rhythm: slow, gentle, and breathing. Every moving element follows this rhythm:

| Element | Rhythm |
|---------|--------|
| Roses | Slow sway — 4–5 second cycle |
| Butterflies | Gentle drift — 3–4 second cycle |
| Fountain | Constant gentle flow — no cycle |
| Petals | Occasional drift — 5–8 seconds between |
| Wind audio | Rises slightly every 8–10 seconds, falls back gently |
| Camera | Moves on the same rhythm as the roses — never fights it |

When all elements are in rhythm, Rosie's Rose Garden feels alive but still. When any element breaks rhythm — a sudden camera move, a fast butterfly, a sharp sound — the world loses its identity. Rhythm is the final check before approving any animated scene.

### Still Image vs. Video Consistency

Every frame from an animated scene must pass the same Production QA as a still image. Animation does not exempt a frame from identity checks. The canonical colour palette, heart beds, fence style, and rose colours apply equally to every individual frame of any animated sequence.
