# 30 — YouTube Metadata Standard

> This is a channel strategy and publishing metadata standard.
> It does NOT change production canon, character canon, world canon, or episode scripts.
> It does NOT require rewriting existing videos unless specifically requested.
> Every future YouTube title, description, tags, Shorts metadata and social caption should follow this document.

Channel URL (reference only — do NOT paste into post/caption text, see §14): https://www.youtube.com/@PompomHills

> Video-level style, pacing, hook and ending decisions (what the video itself shows) are defined in
> `00-CORE/18_VIDEO_STYLE_AND_RETENTION_STANDARD.md`. This document only covers title, description,
> tags, hashtags and Shorts metadata (what is written around the video).

---

## 1. Channel Positioning

Pompom Hills is a warm preschool animation universe.

Metadata should communicate:

- gentle preschool animation
- safe, calm, warm stories
- friendship
- sharing
- taking turns
- colours
- counting
- feelings
- kindness
- nature discovery
- small daily lessons
- recurring characters
- recurring worlds

Tone:

- warm
- parent-friendly
- simple
- trustworthy
- not aggressive
- not clickbait
- not over-hyped
- not noisy

Avoid:

- exaggerated claims
- "best cartoon ever"
- "viral"
- "must watch"
- "YouTube Kids" unless explicitly approved
- misleading education claims
- scary, urgent or pressure-based wording

---

## 2. Default Language

Default YouTube metadata language: **English**.

Reason: Pompom Hills episodes currently use English dialogue and target a broad preschool audience.

Turkish versions may be created separately when requested.

- If the video itself is English, use English metadata.
- If the video is Turkish, use Turkish metadata.
- If the user requests bilingual metadata, provide English first, Turkish second.

---

## 3. Title Rules

Every YouTube title must be clear, searchable and parent-friendly.

Title should include:

- episode concept
- preschool relevance
- Pompom Hills brand
- optional character names

Target length: 45–75 characters when possible. Avoid overly long titles.

### A. Character Introduction

```text
Meet [Character]! | Pompom Hills Preschool Animation
```

Examples:
- Meet Kiko! | Pompom Hills Preschool Animation
- Meet Noah! | Pompom Hills Kids Cartoon
- Meet Mimi! | Gentle Preschool Animation

### B. World Introduction

```text
Welcome to [World]! | Pompom Hills Preschool Animation
```

Examples:
- Welcome to Cloud Hill! | Pompom Hills Preschool Animation
- Kiko's Home Tour | Warm Preschool Story for Kids
- Welcome to Flower Hill! | Colours & Nature for Preschool Kids

### C. Educational Story

```text
[Main Lesson] | [Character Names] | Pompom Hills Preschool Animation
```

Examples:
- My Turn, Your Turn! | Sharing & Taking Turns | Pompom Hills
- Where Does Rain Come From? | Luca & Mimi | Preschool Animation
- Let's Learn Colours! | Flower Hill | Pompom Hills

### D. Shorts

Short, hook-based titles.

Examples:
- Your Turn! | Pompom Hills Shorts
- A Ball! | Luca and Noah | Preschool Shorts
- Hello, Rain! | Pompom Hills Shorts
- Can We Share? | Taking Turns for Kids

---

## 4. Description Rules

Every long-form video description should include:

1. Warm opening hook
2. Short story summary
3. Educational / emotional value
4. Episode details
5. Hashtags

No channel link or CTA line is included (see §14).

Structure:

```text
[Opening hook]

[1–2 sentence story summary]

[Learning value paragraph]

Episode: SxxExx — [Episode Title]
Characters: [Character names]
Location: [World / environment]
Theme: [Sharing / colours / rain / friendship / etc.]

[Hashtags]
```

---

## 5. Description Style

Descriptions should sound natural, not corporate.

Use phrases like:

- In this gentle Pompom Hills preschool story...
- Luca and Noah discover...
- Kiko learns...
- Mimi notices...
- Children can explore...
- A warm story about...
- A calm preschool animation about...

Avoid:

- "This video teaches your child everything about..."
- "Guaranteed learning"
- "Best educational cartoon"
- "Must subscribe"
- "Don't miss out!!!"
- excessive emojis

Emoji use: sparingly only. Allowed: 🌈 💛 ✨ 🎬. Do not overuse.

## 10. Character Intro Metadata

Title formula:

```text
Meet [Character]! | Pompom Hills Preschool Animation
```

Description must include:
- character name
- simple personality trait
- what children can expect from this character

No channel link or CTA line (see §14).

Example structure:

```text
[Character] is [personality trait]. In this gentle preschool animation, children can get to know [him/her/them] through a warm first moment.
```

Tags should include:


## 11. World Intro Metadata

Title formula:

```text
Welcome to [World]! | Pompom Hills Preschool Animation
```

Description must include:

## 13. Long Video to Shorts Metadata

Every long video should generate 3–5 Shorts.

Each Short must have:
- its own title
- its own mini-description
- its own tags
- its own thumbnail frame suggestion
- its own source timestamp
- one clear hook

Shorts are not random cuts. Each Short should be a mini-story:

```text
Hook → Action → Emotion or payoff
```

Example Shorts from one episode:
- A Ball!
- Can I Try?
- Your Turn!
- The Ball Rolled Away!
- Together!

---

## 14. CTA and Link Rules

Pompom Hills post metadata uses **no call-to-action asks and no external links**. This applies
to every Short, long video, and social post (YouTube, Facebook, Instagram).

Do NOT include in any title, description, or caption:

- YouTube links or channel URLs (no `https://www.youtube.com/@PompomHills`, no video URLs)
- "link in bio" or any external link / redirect URL
- subscribe requests of any kind ("Subscribe", "Subscribe for more", "SUBSCRIBE NOW")
- like / share / comment / follow requests ("smash like", "don't forget to like", "follow us")
- "Must subscribe", "You must watch", "Viral", "Best cartoon" and similar hype

Post text ends naturally with the warm caption and hashtags only.

**Allowed (optional):** a gentle brand-voice closing line with **no link**, e.g. "Sweet dreams
from Pompom Hills." A soft, linkless content pointer such as "watch the full episode on our
channel" is permitted but not required — just never attach a link and never add a
subscribe/like/follow ask to it.

> Note: The channel URL at the top of this document is reference information only. It must not
> be pasted into post/caption text.
>
> Video-level ending rules are separate: inside-video subscribe/like language is already
> forbidden by `00-CORE/18_VIDEO_STYLE_AND_RETENTION_STANDARD.md` §9a.

## 15. Facebook / Instagram Captions

Facebook and Instagram captions are self-contained. They do **not** link out to YouTube and do
**not** ask for subscribe / like / follow / share (see §14).

```text
A gentle preschool moment from Pompom Hills. 🌟

Kiko, Noah, Mimi and friends share warm little stories about friendship, colours, nature and small everyday discoveries.

#PompomHills #PreschoolAnimation #KidsAnimation
```

- Do not include any YouTube link (no channel URL, no video URL, no "link in bio", no "watch more" redirect).
- Do not include subscribe, like, follow or share requests.
- Keep the caption warm and complete on its own, ending with hashtags.

## 16. Series Metadata — Opa's Storytime

For any episode belonging to the **Opa's Storytime** series, follow `00-CORE/31_OPA_INTERACTIVE_STORYTELLING_FORMAT.md` for series identity, location rules, character combinations and episode naming. This section only defines the metadata fields required in addition to the standard template above.

**Title formula:**

```text
Opa's Storytime: [Story Title] | Pompom Hills Preschool Story
```

Shorter alternative when needed: `Opa's Storytime: [Story Title]`

**Description template:**

```text
In this gentle Opa's Storytime episode from Pompom Hills, Opa tells a warm preschool
story while [child characters] listen, ask questions, and discover [theme].

This story supports early learning through calm themes like [theme 1], [theme 2], and [theme 3].

Series: Opa's Storytime
Playlist: Opa's Storytime | Gentle Preschool Stories
Characters: Opa, [child characters]
Location: [reading / story location]
Theme: [theme]
```

No channel link, playlist link, or CTA line is included (see §14).

**Shorts naming:** Every Short title must include either "Opa's Storytime" or "Opa" (see `00-CORE/31_OPA_INTERACTIVE_STORYTELLING_FORMAT.md` §15). Every Short description must NOT include a YouTube/playlist link or a subscribe/like/follow CTA (§14).

**Tags:** add `Opa's Storytime`, `Opa`, plus the two required child character tags and the location tag from the Core Tag Bank (§8).

---

## 18. Quality Checklist

Before finalizing metadata, check:

- [ ] Title includes clear story or learning value.
- [ ] Title includes Pompom Hills when useful.
- [ ] Description is warm and parent-friendly.
- [ ] Tags are relevant.
- [ ] No misleading claims.
- [ ] No YouTube/channel/video links or "link in bio" (§14).
- [ ] No subscribe / like / follow / share requests (§14).
- [ ] No excessive emojis.
- [ ] Metadata matches the actual video content.
- [ ] If video is a Short, title is short and hook-based.
- [ ] If video is long-form, description includes episode, characters, location and theme.

---

## 19. Final Principle

Pompom Hills metadata should help parents immediately understand:

- What is this video?
- Who is in it?
- What will my child feel or learn?
- Is this warm, safe and age-appropriate?

Metadata should support discovery without becoming clickbait.

The brand voice is gentle, clear and trustworthy.

---

## How to Use This Standard

To request metadata for a specific episode, use this pattern:

```text
Use 00-CORE/30_YOUTUBE_METADATA_STANDARD.md.

Create YouTube title, description, tags, hashtags and Shorts metadata for this episode:

Episode: S01E10 — My Turn, Your Turn
Characters: Luca, Noah
Location: Central Square
Theme: Sharing, taking turns, teamwork
Length: 90 seconds
```

The output must follow the Required Output Format (§17) and pass the Quality Checklist (§18).
