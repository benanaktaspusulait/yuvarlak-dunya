# 31 — Opa's Storytime: Interactive Storytelling Series Format

> This is a recurring series and production format standard.
> It does NOT redesign Opa, Luca, Mimi, Kiko, Noah or any other character.
> It does NOT create a new world. Opa's Tree remains available but is never mandatory.
> It does NOT replace `00-CORE/17_VIDEO_GENERATION_STANDARD.md`, `00-CORE/18_VIDEO_STYLE_AND_RETENTION_STANDARD.md`,
> or `00-CORE/30_YOUTUBE_METADATA_STANDARD.md` — it builds on top of them for this specific series.
>
> Cross-referenced from `00-CORE/30_YOUTUBE_METADATA_STANDARD.md`, `11-DOCS/02_SEASON_STRATEGY.md`,
> and `11-DOCS/09_YOUTUBE_STRATEGY.md`.

## 1. Series Decision

Opa's storytelling episodes are a recurring Pompom Hills sub-series, not a one-off episode and not a single-location format.

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Series name | Opa's Storytime |
| Full branded name | Opa's Storytime \| Pompom Hills |
| YouTube playlist name | Opa's Storytime \| Gentle Preschool Stories |
| Short playlist name (if a shorter name is preferred) | Opa's Storytime |

**Purpose:** A warm, cozy, interactive story series where Opa tells gentle preschool stories while child characters ask questions, react, guess, and learn through the story. Designed specifically for YouTube audience retention and repeat/playlist viewing.

Every episode should help children and parents recognise a repeatable format:

1. Opa opens story time.
2. Children gather.
3. Opa begins a gentle story.
4. Children ask questions.
5. The story teaches one emotional idea.
6. The episode ends with calm warmth.

---

## 2. Series Identity

Opa's Storytime should feel:

```
cozy, warm, gentle, interactive, calm, book-focused or story-circle focused,
emotionally clear, preschool-safe, YouTube-friendly, repeatable
```

It should NOT feel:

```
like a lecture, like a fast cartoon, like random narration,
like a bedtime-only series (unless the specific episode is bedtime),
like a classroom lesson, like an overbranded show intro
```

## 3. YouTube Playlist Strategy

**Playlist name:** Opa's Storytime | Gentle Preschool Stories

**Playlist description:**

```text
Join Opa and the Pompom Hills friends for gentle interactive storytime episodes.
In each warm preschool story, Opa reads or tells a simple tale while the children
ask questions, wonder, and learn about kindness, patience, sharing, friendship,
feelings, and little everyday discoveries.

Watch more Pompom Hills: https://www.youtube.com/@PompomHills
```

**Playlist purpose:**

- Improve YouTube session time.
- Help parents find calm story videos.
- Make Opa a recognisable storyteller.
- Group longer 60–120 second episodes together.
- Support repeat viewing.
- Create a clear content lane separate from character intros and general learning stories.

Every Opa's Storytime episode must be added to this playlist. See `11-DOCS/09_YOUTUBE_STRATEGY.md` §Playlist Strategy for how this playlist sits alongside other channel playlists.

## 4. Series Opening Rule

Every episode begins with a short recurring opening beat — **not** a long title sequence.

| Rule | Value |
|---|---|
| Opening beat length | 3–6 seconds maximum |
| Purpose | Let children recognise the series immediately |
| Required feeling | "Opa's story is starting." |

Approved opening patterns:

**Option A — Book Opening**
```
Opa gently opens a storybook. Children lean closer.
Opa: "Shall we begin?"
```

**Option B — Story Circle**
```
Children sit near Opa. Leaves or warm light move softly.
Opa: "Come close, little friends."
```

**Option C — Cozy Reading Corner**
```
Opa holds a book. A child asks: "Is it story time?"
Opa smiles: "Yes. Story time."
```

Forbidden: loud music, fast logo animation, big on-screen text by default, long intro sequences.

The opening beat length above (3–6 seconds) is a hard maximum, consistent with the general Opening Bumper Rule in `00-CORE/18_VIDEO_STYLE_AND_RETENTION_STANDARD.md` §4a. This series-specific opening replaces the general one — do not stack both.

---

## 4a. Series Closing Rule

Every episode ends with a short recurring closing beat, following the general Closing Bumper Rule in `00-CORE/18_VIDEO_STYLE_AND_RETENTION_STANDARD.md` §9a.

| Rule | Value |
|---|---|
| Closing beat length | 3–6 seconds maximum |
| Purpose | Warm emotional resolution, series recognition, support repeat/playlist viewing |
| Required feeling | "The story is finished, and it was worth staying for." |

Approved closing patterns:

**Option A — Book Closing**
```
Opa gently closes the storybook. Children smile.
Opa: "And that is our story for today."
```

**Option B — Story Circle**
```
Children settle, calm and warm. Opa looks at them softly.
Opa: "Until next time, little friends."
```

**Option C — Quiet Hold**
```
Opa and the children sit together in a quiet, warm final hold. No new dialogue.
```

Forbidden: loud subscribe requests, "smash like" language, flashing text, long credits, abrupt cut to black — see `00-CORE/18_VIDEO_STYLE_AND_RETENTION_STANDARD.md` §9a for the full forbidden list.

Playlist CTA and end-screen elements go in the description/upload metadata, not inside the video — see §16 and `00-CORE/30_YOUTUBE_METADATA_STANDARD.md` §17 Public Link Rule.

Master videos should avoid heavy on-screen text (see `00-CORE/17_VIDEO_GENERATION_STANDARD.md` §Text Safety).

| Field | Value |
|---|---|

| Placement | After the first cozy visual beat — never as a disruptive cold open |

**Default:** No title card inside the video unless specifically requested. Use the YouTube title, thumbnail, playlist, and recurring opening beat (§4) for series recognition instead.

## 7. Series Structure

Every Opa's Storytime episode follows this structure:

```
1.  Recurring storytime opening beat (§4)
2.  Children gather around Opa
3.  Opa introduces the story
4.  One child asks the first question
5.  Opa begins the masal / story
6.  Children interrupt with simple questions or guesses
7.  Opa continues the story through their questions
8.  Story reaches one emotional lesson
9.  Children connect the lesson to themselves
10. Opa ends with one warm final line
11. Quiet final hold
```

### Interaction Rule

The story stays interactive. Opa never speaks alone for more than 10–15 seconds without a child response (question, guess, small emotional reaction, or simple observation). The story is never a monologue. The lesson is shown through story and interaction, never lectured.

## 8. Locations

Opa's Storytime can happen in multiple approved story locations. **Opa's Tree is optional, never mandatory.** Location choice should match the story mood, not the other way around.

Allowed locations:

- Indoor Reading Corner
- Cozy Book Nook
- Classroom Reading Area (e.g. Learning Room, `POMPOM_HILLS_PRODUCTION/02_WORLDS/LEARNING_ROOM/` — Reading Corner zone)
- Opa's Tree / Story Circle (`POMPOM_HILLS_PRODUCTION/02_WORLDS/OPA_TREE/`, `POMPOM_HILLS_PRODUCTION/02_WORLDS/STORY_CIRCLE/`)
- Outdoor Story Blanket
- Character Home Reading Spot (e.g. Kiko's Home Reading Corner, `POMPOM_HILLS_PRODUCTION/02_WORLDS/KIKOS_HOME/`)
- Rainy-day indoor reading space

Do not force every episode to happen at Opa's Tree. Do not create a new world if an existing approved reading/story location already fits — see `00-CORE/CONTINUITY_RULES.md` §11.1 No Generic Worlds Rule for how to reference an established location by name.

---

## 9. Required Characters

Every episode must include Opa and at least 2 child characters. Recommended maximum: Opa + 2 or 3 child characters — do not overload the episode.

| Combination | Best for |
|---|---|
| Opa + Luca + Mimi | Curiosity, feelings, gentle questions |
| Opa + Kiko + Noah | Colour, play, action, cheerful reactions |
| Opa + Luca + Noah | Curiosity + playful "what happens next?" energy |
| Opa + Kiko + Mimi | Soft, cozy, emotional or home-feeling stories |

---

## 10. Story Themes

Focus on one clear preschool theme per episode. Do not combine too many lessons in one episode. The lesson must be felt through the story, not explained like a lecture.

Allowed themes: patience, listening, sharing, kindness, helping, saying sorry, waiting your turn, courage, friendship, taking care of nature, noticing small things, bedtime calm, feelings, being gentle.

---

## 11. Visual Rules

Because most episodes are reading/storytelling episodes, the masal may be visualized softly but must never become confusing.

**Allowed:** simple picture-book illustrations, soft imagination shapes/glow inside the book, a tiny sparkle or light shift during the story moment, children reacting to the book.

**Not allowed:** full cutaway to another world (unless a specific episode explicitly requests it), sudden fantasy environment, loud effects, scary imagery, fast montage, complicated magical effects, readable text in the book or anywhere on screen.

Camera, colour, lighting and negative-prompt rules follow `00-CORE/17_VIDEO_GENERATION_STANDARD.md`, `00-CORE/CONTINUITY_RULES.md`, and `00-CORE/NEGATIVE_PROMPTS.md` — this document does not duplicate them.

## 14. Thumbnail Strategy

Opa's Storytime thumbnails must be recognisable as a series.

**Must include:** Opa clearly visible, at least one listening child character, a cozy story object (book, story chair, tree circle, or reading rug), warm light, clear emotional expression, simple uncluttered background, story-specific object if possible.

Examples:

- *The Little Cloud That Waited* — Opa holding a book, Luca and Mimi leaning in, small cloud illustration visible.
- *The Little Seed* — Opa with book, child pointing at tiny seed picture.
- *The Sleepy Leaf* — Opa and children looking at a soft sleepy leaf illustration.

**Avoid:** too much text, unreadable text, dark background, too many characters, random objects, generic face-only thumbnail without a story cue.

## 15. Shorts Strategy for the Series

Every episode should produce 3–5 Shorts. See `00-CORE/18_VIDEO_STYLE_AND_RETENTION_STANDARD.md` §15 Shorts Extraction Rule and `00-CORE/30_YOUTUBE_METADATA_STANDARD.md` §13 for the general Shorts rules — this section only adds series-specific naming and CTA.

Shorts should highlight: a child question, Opa's warm answer, the story object, the emotional lesson, or a funny/curious reaction.

Short titles should include either "Opa's Storytime" or "Opa":

```
Opa's Storytime: Why Did the Cloud Wait?
Opa Tells a Story
The Tiny Cloud
Can You Wait Too?
One Gentle Drop
```

Every Short description must include:

```text
Watch the full Opa's Storytime series: https://www.youtube.com/@PompomHills
```

No aggressive CTA.

## 19. Final Principle

Opa's Storytime is a repeatable YouTube-focused Pompom Hills series. It should help parents and children recognise: **"This is the calm Opa story series."**

The series supports: longer watch time, repeat viewing, playlist viewing, emotional learning, character familiarity, YouTube audience retention.

Opa is the storyteller. The children are active listeners. The story grows through their questions. The playlist keeps the audience watching.
