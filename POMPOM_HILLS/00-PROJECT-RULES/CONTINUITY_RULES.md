# CONTINUITY RULES — PomPom Hills

---

## Shot 01

Shot 01 her zaman yeni bir Take Shot olarak başlar.

Önceki sahne referansı yoktur.

Shot 01, kamerayı, mekanı ve karakteri ilk kez tanıtır.

---

## Shot 02 ve Sonrası

Shot 02 ve sonraki her shot, bir önceki shot'ın continuity frame'inden devam eder.

Yeni Take Shot oluşturulmaz.

Aynı mekanda, aynı ışıkta, aynı kompozisyonda devam edilir.

---

## Continuity Frame Seçimi

Otomatik olarak önceki shot'ın son karesini kullanma.

En iyi görsel sürekliliği sağlayan kareyi seç.

En iyi continuity frame son kareden önce olabilir.

Her zaman sürekliliği, en güzel kareyi seçmekten üstün tut.

---

## Continuity Frame Kullanımı

Her yeni shot şunları kullanır:

- Önceki shot continuity frame'i
- Gerekli karakter referansları
- Mevcut dünya

Dünyayı sıfırdan yeniden oluşturma.

---

## Devam Eden Shot'larda Korunacaklar

- Karakter görünümü
- Mekan
- Işık
- Ölçek
- Kamera kompozisyonu

Mevcut mekanları asla yeniden tasarlama.

---

## Camera Scale Continuity

When continuing from a previous shot, the AI must preserve not only the characters but also the visual scale of the scene.

A camera pull-back does NOT mean creating a new establishing shot.

The camera should move only within the existing visual space.

Avoid dramatic changes in framing unless the storyboard explicitly requires them.

Never replace the current environment with a simplified or redesigned version.

Maintain the same tree, same hill, same lighting and same visual identity.

A slow pull-back should reveal only slightly more of the existing environment.

Do not generate a new landscape.

Do not generate a new tree.

Do not reinterpret the location.

The world is already established.

Continue inside that world.

---

## World Identity Lock

Every important location in Pompom Hills is a permanent visual asset.

Locations must not be redesigned between shots.

The AI should treat each world as a recurring film set.

For example:

Opa's Tree is always the same tree.

It is not "a large tree."

It is a unique location with a fixed identity.

Maintain:

- trunk shape
- canopy silhouette
- lantern placement style
- tree house opening
- platforms
- surrounding landscape
- lighting style

Minor perspective changes are acceptable.

Major redesigns are not.

---

## Camera Movement Limits

Allowed:

- slow dolly
- gentle push-in
- gentle pull-back
- small pan
- small tilt

Avoid:

- sudden zooms
- dramatic pull-backs
- extreme wide shots
- new establishing shots
- large environment changes

The viewer should always feel that the camera remains inside the same scene.

---

## Episode 1 Lesson

Episode 1 demonstrated that AI may interpret "pull back" as permission to generate an entirely new environment.

From now on, every camera movement must explicitly state that the existing environment must remain unchanged.

The AI should move the camera, not rebuild the world.

---

## Character Entrance Continuity

Important characters should not suddenly appear between connected shots.

If a character will become important in the next shot, their presence should be established before they become the focus.

Characters should feel like they are already inside the world, not generated suddenly between shots.

Preferred methods:

- show the character small in the background
- show part of the character
- show a silhouette
- show a subtle movement
- show an off-screen sound before entrance
- show the character entering naturally into frame

Avoid instant character appearance between consecutive shots unless it is intentional for the story.

---

## Episode 1 Lesson — Opa Entrance

In Episode 1, Opa was not visible in Shot 01 but appeared at the beginning of Shot 02.

This was acceptable, but future episodes should handle character entrances more naturally.

If Opa will speak or guide the next shot, establish his presence earlier.

Examples:

- Opa is visible as a small silhouette on a branch.
- Opa's glasses catch a small warm reflection.
- A soft owl sound is heard before he is revealed.
- Opa is partially visible among the leaves.
- The camera reveals Opa through a natural tilt or pan.

Rule:

A character reveal should feel discovered by the camera, not created by the AI.
