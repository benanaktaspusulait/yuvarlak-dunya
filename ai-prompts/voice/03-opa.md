# Opa - Voice Prompt v2.3

## For OpenArt Voice Creator (ULTRA-OPTIMIZED)

If OpenArt Voice Creator fails, try these prompts from shortest to longest:

### Version A (Extreme Short - ~150 chars)
```
Create a warm, wise mentor voice named Opa. Like a gentle grandfather with playful wisdom. Speak slowly and clearly to preschoolers. Natural tone with a smile in voice.
```

### Version B (Short - ~250 chars)
```
Create Opa's voice: a warm, wise mentor for preschoolers. Like a gentle grandfather telling stories with playful wisdom. Speak slowly and clearly. Natural conversational tone with a gentle smile. Use short, simple sentences.
```

### Version C (Medium - ~400 chars)
```
Create Opa's voice for a preschool show. He's a warm, wise mentor like a gentle grandfather. His voice should be calm, experienced and reassuring — never old or tired. Speak slowly and clearly with natural pauses. Use short preschool sentences. Every sentence should carry a gentle smile. Avoid sounding sleepy, rushed, or like a narrator.
```

### Test Lines (Use in all versions)
```
"Hello, little friend."
"Take your time."
"Well done!"
```

## Detailed Voice Prompt (for Codex and non-OpenArt tools)

```text
Create a voice for Opa from Pompom Hills v2.3.

Note: Opa is the ONLY character who does NOT sound like a small child. He is a timeless wise mentor. His voice should be warm, calm, experienced and reassuring — but never old, weak, sleepy or tired. Always soft and gentle, never harsh or strict.

Voice identity:
Timeless wise mentor, warm guide, gentle helper. Like a kind grandfather who tells bedtime stories, but with playful wisdom.

Tone:
Low-medium, warm, rounded, patient, soft-spoken. Every sentence feels safe and unhurried. Not deep or booming — just warm and gentle. Always speaks directly to one child, never like a narrator.

Energy:
Gentle but alive. Always sound emotionally present. Never sound flat or monotone. Every sentence should carry a small smile.

Smile:
A gentle smile should always be heard in Opa's voice. Children should hear kindness even when they cannot see him.

Performance:
Never raises his voice. Even warnings become slower and softer. He gives children space to discover. No scary owl sound, no hooting unless the script asks for a tiny friendly sound. Never sound like a narrator or lecturer.

Pace:
75-90 words per minute. Slow, measured, calm. Pause briefly after important ideas. Allow children time to understand. Never rush the next sentence.

Breathing:
Natural quiet breaths. Pause naturally between ideas. Never sound robotic. Never sound heavily edited. Never audible breathing.

Pronunciation:
Natural conversational pronunciation. Never over-enunciate. Never sound theatrical or like an audiobook narrator.

Listening:
When another character speaks, Opa listens patiently. Occasionally respond with a soft laugh, a warm "Hmm...", or a gentle nod if appropriate. Never interrupt.

Pitch:
Low-medium adult guide voice, centered around 150-190 Hz. Warm, not deep. Gentle, not authoritative.

Emotion range:
Calm, kind, thoughtful, pleased, gently amused, playful wisdom, curious, encouraging, gentle concern. No sternness, no authority pressure, no sadness, no sarcasm.

Signature phrase:
"Well done!" is Opa's signature phrase. Use it only when it naturally fits the situation. Do not force it into every scene.

Speech Style:
Short preschool-friendly sentences. Simple vocabulary. One idea per sentence. No abstract concepts. No difficult words. Always sound emotionally safe.

Prohibited:
Never sound sleepy. Never sound weak. Never sound sad. Never sound sarcastic. Never sound like a narrator. Never lecture. Never rush. Never sound old or tired. Never sound flat or monotone.

Test lines:
1. "Hello, little friend."
2. "Take your time."
3. "Every great adventure begins with one small step."
4. "Well done!"

WHAT THIS VOICE IS:
- A timeless wise mentor figure
- Warm and gentle with playful wisdom
- Slow and patient with natural pauses
- Safe and reassuring with a gentle smile
- Speaks directly to one child
- Gentle but emotionally alive energy
```
