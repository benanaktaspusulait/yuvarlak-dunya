# ENVIRONMENT_USABILITY_AUDIT_PROMPT

> *Production safety gate for every Pompom Hills environment.*
> *Every world must pass this audit before artwork production begins.*

---

## Purpose

Before any world is approved for production, it must be checked for child movement usability.

Some environments looked beautiful but did not provide enough open, safe, readable space for preschool characters to walk, run gently, gather, turn around, sit, and interact.

This audit prevents that mistake from happening again.

---

## Audit Goal

Review each environment bible and check whether the world actually works as a playable preschool animation space.

The world must not only look beautiful.

It must support character movement.

It must support staging.

It must support camera work.

It must support safe preschool interaction.

---

## Check Every Environment For

### 1. Walkable Space

Does the environment include clear open areas where 2–3 preschool characters can walk side by side?

### 2. Gentle Running / Movement

Can characters move gently without immediately hitting props, trees, fences, rocks, flowers, or furniture?

### 3. Staging Space

Is there enough clear space for:

- 2 characters talking
- 3 characters gathering
- One character entering
- One character leaving
- One character sitting
- One character turning around

### 4. Camera Space

Can the camera frame the characters clearly without being blocked by large props?

Check for:

- Wide shot
- Medium shot
- Side view
- Gentle push-in
- Continuity shot

### 5. Prop Density

Are there too many decorative props?

If props reduce usability, simplify them.

Decorations should frame the action, not block it.

### 6. Path Readability

Can a child viewer immediately understand where the characters can walk?

The path should be visually clear within 2 seconds.

### 7. Safety

- No narrow bridges unless wide and safe
- No steep drops
- No crowded edges
- No sharp corners
- No visual hazards
- No cluttered floor

### 8. Scale

Everything must be scaled for preschool characters.

Paths, bridges, signs, benches, flowers, rocks, logs and props must not overwhelm the characters.

### 9. OpenArt Production

Can the environment be represented clearly with 4 canonical OpenArt reference images?

- Hero View
- Entrance View
- Trail / Activity View
- Detail View

### 10. Episode Usefulness

Can this environment support at least 10–20 calm story moments without feeling cramped or repetitive?

---

## Global Environment Rule

Every Pompom Hills world must include:

- One clear main walking path
- One open gathering area
- One calm sitting/resting area
- Enough empty space for 2–3 characters
- Props placed mostly on edges
- No clutter in the middle
- No obstacles blocking camera continuity

---

## Important

Do not judge only visual beauty.

A beautiful world that characters cannot move inside is NOT production ready.

The best Pompom Hills environment is:

- Simple
- Open
- Safe
- Readable
- Soft
- Reusable
- Easy to stage

---

*This audit is a mandatory production gate.*
*No world enters artwork production without passing this audit.*
