# Project Principles

---

## Purpose

This document defines the permanent principles of the Pompom Hills project.

These principles are not tied to a specific point in time.

They are not dependent on any tool, technology, or workflow.

They represent the core philosophy that guides every future decision.

Whenever there is uncertainty, this document should be consulted first.

Every production, creative, technical, and publishing decision should align with these principles.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Children Come First

Every decision should improve the viewing experience for young children.

Not the algorithm. Not the metrics. Not the competition.

The child sitting in front of the screen is the only audience that matters.

If a decision does not serve the child, it should not be made.

### 2. Low Stimulation Over High Excitement

Calm experiences are more valuable than loud entertainment.

A child who feels safe will return. A child who feels overwhelmed will not.

Gentle pacing, soft colours, and quiet moments are not weaknesses — they are strengths.

The world should feel like a warm hug, not a sugar rush.

### 3. Kindness Over Conflict

Stories focus on empathy, curiosity, cooperation, and emotional safety.

There are no villains. There is no danger. There is no punishment.

Conflict comes from situations, not from other characters.

The audience should always feel that kindness is the strongest force.

### 4. Simple Stories With Emotional Depth

A simple idea executed well is better than a complicated plot.

One emotional idea per episode. One clear feeling. One gentle resolution.

Complexity is not depth. Simplicity, when done with care, creates the deepest emotional connection.

### 5. Production Quality Over Production Speed

Never sacrifice quality simply to publish faster.

A show that is remembered for years is more valuable than a show that is forgotten in weeks.

Every episode should meet the same standard. No shortcuts. No exceptions.

Quality is not a phase — it is a permanent commitment.

### 6. Build Reusable Assets

Characters, worlds, props, music, and production workflows should all become long-term investments.

Every asset created should support dozens of future stories.

Disposable content wastes resources. Reusable assets compound value.

Think of every creation as a building block, not a one-time product.

### 7. Consistency Creates Trust

Characters should behave consistently. Worlds should remain recognizable. The visual language should remain familiar.

When a child watches Pompom Hills, they should feel they are returning to a place they know.

Inconsistency breaks trust. Consistency builds it.

### 8. Grow Gradually

Do not rush into longer episodes, more characters, or larger worlds until the project is ready.

Growth should follow production maturity, not calendar dates.

The project should feel like it is naturally expanding, not being forced to scale.

Patience in growth creates sustainability.

### 9. Every Episode Should Strengthen the Universe

An episode is not just content.

It is another building block of the Pompom Hills world.

Every story should make the world feel richer, the characters feel deeper, and the audience feel more connected.

If an episode does not strengthen the universe, reconsider its purpose.

### 10. Always Think Long-Term

Avoid decisions that create short-term gains but long-term maintenance problems.

The project should be designed to run for years, not months.

Every decision should be one that the team can still be proud of in five years.

Build for the future, not for the deadline.

---

## Decision Filter

When making an important decision, ask:

| Question | If No |
|----------|-------|
| Does it improve the experience for children? | Reconsider |
| Does it fit the low-stimulation philosophy? | Reconsider |
| Does it strengthen the world? | Reconsider |
| Does it improve production sustainability? | Reconsider |
| Can it be reused? | Reconsider |
| Will we still be happy with this decision in five years? | Reconsider |

If the answer to any question is "no," the decision should be delayed or reconsidered.

---

## Non-Negotiables

These should never be compromised:

- **No overstimulation.** Fast cuts, loud sounds, and visual chaos have no place here.
- **No unnecessary conflict.** The world is safe. Characters are kind. Stories are gentle.
- **No inconsistent characters.** Characters remain the same across every episode, every season, every year.
- **No unnecessary complexity.** Simplicity serves the audience. Complexity serves the ego.
- **No disposable assets.** Every creation should become a permanent part of the universe.
- **No production shortcuts that reduce quality.** Speed without quality is waste.
- **No content that does not serve children.** If it doesn't help a child feel safe, curious, or loved, it doesn't belong.

---

## Closing

These principles are not suggestions.

They are the foundation of everything Pompom Hills creates.

When in doubt, return here.

When there is disagreement, consult these principles.

When the project grows beyond recognition, these principles should still feel true.
