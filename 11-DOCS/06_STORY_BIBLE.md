# 06 — Story Bible

---

## Core Storytelling Principles

1. **One emotional idea per episode** — simplicity is strength
2. **Discovery over instruction** — children learn through observation
3. **Safety over excitement** — no conflict, no villains, no danger
4. **Warmth over spectacle** — soft, gentle, comforting
5. **Repetition over novelty** — repetition aids learning and attachment

---

## Episode Structure

Every 60-second episode follows this structure:

| Section | Duration | Purpose |
|---------|----------|---------|
| **Opening** | 0–5 sn | Establish the world and characters |
| **Discovery** | 5–20 sn | The emotional moment happens |
| **Interaction** | 20–45 sn | Characters respond to the moment |
| **Resolution** | 45–60 sn | Gentle ending, emotional resting point |

---

## Conflict Philosophy

Pompom Hills has **no traditional conflict**.

There are no villains.

There are no dangers.

There are no arguments.

The only "conflict" is internal curiosity or gentle challenge:

- "What is that?"
- "I want to try"
- "How does this work?"
- "I feel sleepy"

These are not problems to solve — they are discoveries to share.

---

## Learning Philosophy

Children learn through:

- **Observation:** Watching characters discover
- **Repetition:** Hearing the same phrases multiple times
- **Emotion:** Feeling what characters feel
- **Safety:** Knowing the world is predictable and kind

Learning should never feel like teaching.

---

## Humor Philosophy

Humor in Pompom Hills is:

- Gentle
- Physical (small movements, ear flops, gentle surprises)
- Never mocking
- Never sarcastic
- Always warm

---

## Dialogue Principles

- Maximum 15–20 unique words per episode
- Repeated phrases for learning
- Simple sentence structure
- Warm, clear delivery
- No shouting, no hurry

---

## Emotional Pacing

Every episode follows an emotional arc:

```
Wonder → Curiosity → Comfort → Peace
```

The feeling should gradually evolve, never reset.

---

## Beginning / Middle / Ending Rules

### Beginning
- Establish the world
- Introduce the emotional situation
- Keep it calm and inviting

### Middle
- Build the emotional moment
- Characters respond naturally
- Gentle interaction

### Ending
- Resolution is always warm
- The audience feels safe
- The emotional loop is closed

---

## Things That Should Never Happen

- Violence or aggression
- Bullying or exclusion
- Punishment or scolding
- Loud or sudden sounds
- Fast cuts or jarring transitions
- Dark or scary imagery
- Sharp edges or dangerous objects
- Complex plots or confusing stories
