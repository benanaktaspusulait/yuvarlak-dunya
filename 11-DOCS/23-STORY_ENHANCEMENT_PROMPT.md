# Story Enhancement Prompt

---

## Usage

This prompt is designed to be given to Codex or any other AI assistant to enhance storyboard documents.

It applies the latest Pompom Hills production standards to improve pacing, visual variety, and emotional engagement — without changing the story, dialogue, or characters.

---

## Prompt

```text
Update the supplied episode/storyboard document using the latest Pompom Hills production standards.

Do NOT rewrite the story.

Do NOT change the educational objective.

Do NOT replace characters.

Instead, improve the existing scenes by applying the following production guidelines.

==================================================
1. STRONGER OPENING HOOK
==================================================

The first 3–5 seconds are critical.

Avoid slow introductions.

Begin with:

- an interesting visual
- a surprising action
- a curious question
- or an emotional expression

The opening should immediately make a preschool child curious.

Examples:

"Can you hear that?"

"Look what I found!"

"Do you know where stars sleep?"

"What is hiding over there?"

Do not use clickbait.

Create genuine curiosity.

==================================================
2. MICRO ACTIONS
==================================================

Avoid long static shots.

Every 3–6 seconds introduce a small visual action.

Examples:

wave

blink

look around

pick up object

walk

jump

point

smile

laugh

sit

stand

open book

touch flower

look at butterfly

watch bird

Actions should feel natural.

==================================================
3. CAMERA RHYTHM
==================================================

Avoid keeping the camera static for too long.

Use gentle cinematic movement.

Examples:

slow push-in

slow pull-back

small pan

follow character

low-angle child perspective

over-the-shoulder

wide establishing shot

close-up reaction

Never use aggressive movement.

Everything should remain calm and preschool friendly.

==================================================
4. CHARACTER SIGNATURES
==================================================

Every main character should display their unique personality.

Examples:

Opa
- adjusts glasses
- opens an old book
- gently spreads wings
- smiles wisely

Kiko
- excited little hops
- creative gestures
- curiosity

Luca
- playful football interaction
- energetic movement

Noah
- explorer behaviour
- observing nature

Arda
- confident movement
- friendly smile

These signature behaviours should appear naturally.

==================================================
5. WORLD EXPLORATION
==================================================

Do not keep characters standing in one place.

Whenever possible they should move naturally through the world.

Examples:

walk to another area

look out window

cross the garden

approach a tree

sit beside a flower

walk around the house

move between activity zones

The world itself should become part of the storytelling.

==================================================
6. VISUAL VARIETY
==================================================

Avoid repetitive framing.

Mix:

wide shots

medium shots

close-ups

detail shots

reaction shots

environment shots

POV shots when appropriate

==================================================
7. NATURAL PAUSES
==================================================

Leave short pauses after important dialogue.

Allow children time to think.

Do not rush.

==================================================
8. EDUCATIONAL INTERACTION
==================================================

Whenever appropriate, let the character interact directly with the audience.

Examples:

Can you count these?

Can you find it?

What colour is this?

Which one would you choose?

Do not overuse.

Usually once per episode is enough.

==================================================
9. STRONGER ENDING
==================================================

Every episode should end with:

a warm smile

a memorable final sentence

or a simple question encouraging reflection.

Avoid abrupt endings.

==================================================
10. MAINTAIN STORY
==================================================

Do NOT invent a completely new story.

Improve pacing only.

Preserve:

story structure

dialogue meaning

learning objective

character relationships

==================================================
11. EMOTIONAL BEAT
==================================================

Every 15–20 seconds include a small emotional moment.

Examples:

wonder

joy

surprise

kindness

gratitude

encouragement

The emotion should be visible through facial expressions and body language, not only dialogue.

==================================================
OUTPUT
==================================================

Update the storyboard directly.

Do not explain your changes.

Do not summarise.

Produce the improved production-ready storyboard.
```

---

## How to Use

1. Copy the prompt above
2. Paste it into Codex or any AI assistant
3. Supply the episode/storyboard document
4. The AI will enhance the document using the 11 production guidelines

## What This Does NOT Change

- Story structure
- Dialogue meaning
- Learning objectives
- Character relationships
- Educational content

## What This DOES Improve

- Opening hooks (stronger first 3-5 seconds)
- Micro actions (visual engagement every 3-6 seconds)
- Camera rhythm (gentle movement, variety)
- Character signatures (personality through action)
- World exploration (characters move through the world)
- Visual variety (mixed framing)
- Natural pauses (breathing room)
- Educational interaction (audience engagement)
- Stronger endings (warm, memorable conclusions)
- Emotional beats (visible emotion every 15-20 seconds)
