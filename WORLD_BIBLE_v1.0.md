## 1. Series Concept

**Pompom Hills** is a gentle, warm animated series for toddlers aged 18 months to 4 years. The world is made entirely of soft, rounded shapes — no sharp corners, no scary moments, no fast movement. Every hill, tree, house, cloud, and character feels like a cuddly toy come to life.

The show follows a small child and their animal best friend as they discover simple things in their cosy valley: finding a lost toy, waiting for a turn, sharing a snack, planting a flower, feeling sleepy, hearing rain. An older, calm guide figure is always nearby — never controlling, always supportive.

The tone is warm, patient, and emotionally safe. The visual style is soft 3D cartoon with pastel colours, rounded shapes, and toy-like characters. The world is rich enough to feel alive, but simple enough to be recreated consistently across AI-generated episodes.

## 5. World Overview

Pompom Hills is a small, cosy valley surrounded by gentle, rounded hills. The valley floor has a central village square with a large pompom tree. Spiral paths curve around the hills, connecting small colourful houses. Everything is soft, rounded, and safe.

The world feels like a living toy set — warm, inviting, and endlessly explorable. There are no cities, no roads, no dangerous places. Just hills, paths, houses, trees, flowers, and sky.

### Key Features
- Soft, rounded hills connected by curved paths
- One large pompom tree in the village square
- Small, cosy houses with round doors and windows
- Spiral paths that wind around the hills
- Round stepping stones along the paths
- Gentle clouds, warm sun, friendly stars
- Soft grass, large round flowers, bouncing bushes

## 6. Geography and Locations

### The Valley
- A gentle, bowl-shaped valley surrounded by soft hills
- Green, wavy grass covers everything
- The central square is slightly elevated, with the big pompom tree

### The Hills
- 5-7 soft, rounded hills of different sizes
- Each hill has a gentle slope, no steep drops
- Spiral paths curve around each hill
- Small houses sit on or near the hills

### The Village Square
- Central round area at the base of the big pompom tree
- Smooth, soft ground (like a carpet)
- Round stepping stones leading to the tree
- This is the main meeting place

### The Houses
- One-storey, rounded, colourful
- Each house has a different colour: red, blue, yellow, green, purple, orange
- Round or arched doors
- Circular or oval windows
- Low, soft fences around some houses
- Small gardens with round flowers

### The Paths
- Curved, soft paths connecting the hills
- Made of smooth, round stepping stones
- No straight lines, everything curves gently
- Paths lead to the square, houses, and hilltops

### The Sky
- Soft blue during the day
- Warm orange/pink at sunrise/sunset
- Deep but gentle blue at night (never black)
- Round, puffy clouds
- Warm, smiling sun
- Friendly, sleepy moon
- Round, twinkling stars

### Weather
- Gentle rain: soft drops, rainbow afterwards
- Light snow: fluffy, round snowflakes
- Soft wind: trees sway gently, clouds drift slowly
- Never storms, never lightning, never scary weather

## 7. Signature Visual Details

### Pompom Trees
- Round, fluffy tops like pompoms
- Thick, soft trunks
- Bounce gently in the wind
- Come in different soft colours (green, pink, yellow, blue)
- The big one in the square is the main landmark

### Spiral Paths
- Paths curve around the hills in gentle spirals
- Made of round stepping stones
- Create a sense of journey and discovery
- Easy to recognise and remember

### Round Stepping Stones
- Smooth, round stones along the paths
- Different soft colours
- Characters step on them one by one
- Satisfying "boop" sound when stepped on

### Round Doors
- All doors are round or arched
- Look like sticker cutouts
- Bright, inviting colours
- Easy to open (no handles, just push)

### Boop Sounds
- Small magical moments have a soft "boop" sound
- When a flower blooms, "boop"
- When a star twinkles, "boop"
- When a character has an idea, "boop"
- Creates a signature audio identity

### Sleepy Moon
- Appears in bedtime episodes
- Gentle, yawning face
- Soft glow, not bright
- Sometimes waves goodnight

### The Big Pompom Tree
- Central landmark, always visible
- Largest tree in the world
- Characters gather under it
- Changes slightly with seasons (flowers, leaves, snow)

## 10. Character Rules

### Design Principles
- All characters have rounded faces and soft bodies
- Big eyes, small mouths, clear expressions
- One main colour per character
- Simple clothing, minimal details
- No complex patterns or textures
- Characters should look like soft toys

### Expression Rules
- Emotions are clear and simple
- Happy: wide smile, raised arms
- Sad: downturned mouth, droopy eyes
- Sleepy: half-closed eyes, yawning
- Surprised: wide eyes, small "o" mouth
- Proud: chest out, big smile
- Curious: head tilted, one eyebrow up

### Movement Rules
- Slow, gentle movements
- Bouncy walking (like on a trampoline)
- Soft arm gestures
- Head tilts for curiosity
- No running, no jumping high, no fast movements

### Clothing Rules
- Simple, solid colours
- No logos, no patterns, no accessories
- Easy to identify and replicate
- Same outfit every episode
- One layer only (no coats, no hats unless story requires)

### Interaction Rules
- Characters never hit, push, or hurt each other
- No shouting, no sarcasm, no mean words
- Confusions are resolved with kindness
- Physical contact is gentle (hugs, hand-holding)
- Always respectful and warm

## 13. Episode Structure

### Standard Formula (2.5 – 4 minutes)

| Section | Duration | Description |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| **Opening** | 10-20 seconds | Familiar routine, same music, same visuals |
| **Discovery/Problem** | 30-60 seconds | Something small happens (lost toy, new flower, rain) |
| **Exploration** | 60-120 seconds | Characters investigate, try, learn |
| **Resolution** | 30 seconds | Problem solved gently, everyone happy |
| **Closing** | 20-40 seconds | Repetition, mini song, or closing phrase |

### Opening Routine
- Music starts softly
- Camera shows the valley
- Kiko appears, waves
- Mimi hops in
- They say hello together
- Title appears

### Closing Routine
- Characters gather under the big pompom tree
- They say goodbye
- Music fades gently
- Stars appear (if evening episode) or sun smiles

## 14. Story Rules

### What Stories Are About
- Simple daily situations
- One small problem or discovery
- One concept or skill per episode
- Emotional learning (not academic learning)

### Examples of Story Types
- Finding a lost toy
- Waiting for a turn
- Sharing a snack
- Washing hands before eating
- Feeling sleepy at bedtime
- Hearing rain for the first time
- Planting a seed
- Helping a friend
- Learning a new colour
- Counting to three

### What Stories Are NOT About
- No villains or bad characters
- No danger or fear
- No punishment or time-outs
- No complex adventures
- No moral lectures
- No competition or winning

### Conflict Rules
- Conflicts are very small (lost toy, different ideas)
- No one is ever wrong or bad
- Solutions come from kindness and trying
- Adults never scold, only guide
- Resolution is always positive

### Repetition Rules
- Repeat key phrases 3-4 times per episode
- Repeat visual moments (walking the same path)
- Repeat sounds (the "boop" sound)
- Repetition builds comfort and learning

## 15. Sound and Music Rules

### Music Style
- Soft, acoustic instruments
- Gentle piano, soft guitar, light percussion
- Warm, major key melodies
- Slow tempo (60-80 BPM)
- No electronic beats, no heavy bass

### Signature Sounds
- **Boop**: Soft, magical sound for small wonders
- **Hoppity**: Bouncy sound when Mimi hops
- **Twinkle**: Gentle sound for stars or ideas
- **Whoosh**: Soft sound for wind or movement
- **Plop**: Gentle sound for placing objects

### Voice Rules
- Kiko: Soft, curious voice, slightly high
- Mimi: Gentle, bouncy voice, friendly
- Opa: Warm, low, slow voice, wise
- No shouting, no fast talking, no sarcasm

### Song Rules
- Simple, repetitive melodies
- 4-6 lines maximum
- Same melody each episode (different words)
- Gentle rhythm, easy to clap along
- About the episode's theme (sharing, sleeping, etc.)

## 16. Ten Sample Episode Ideas

### Episode 1: "The Lost Bunny"
- **Learning point**: Asking for help is okay
- **Word group**: Help, find, lost, here it is
- **Summary**: Kiko loses Mimi's favourite toy bunny. She looks everywhere. Opa shows her to ask for help. They find it together under a bush.
- **Repeated phrase**: "Let's look together!"

### Episode 2: "Rain, Rain"
- **Learning point**: New experiences can be nice
- **Word group**: Rain, drop, splash, umbrella
- **Summary**: Kiko hears rain for the first time. She's scared at first. Opa gives her a small umbrella. Kiko splashes in puddles and loves it.
- **Repeated phrase**: "Splash, splash, splash!"

### Episode 3: "Sharing Snacks"
- **Learning point**: Sharing makes everyone happy
- **Word group**: Share, snack, more, together
- **Summary**: Kiko has three berries. Mimi has none. Kiko shares one. They both smile. Opa says "Well done!"
- **Repeated phrase**: "One for you, one for me!"

### Episode 4: "Sleepy Time"
- **Learning point**: Bedtime is cozy and safe
- **Word group**: Sleepy, bed, blanket, goodnight
- **Summary**: Kiko is tired but doesn't want to sleep. Opa tells a soft story. Kiko yawns, gets her blanket, and falls asleep under the stars.
- **Repeated phrase**: "Sleepy, sleepy, goodnight."

### Episode 5: "The Little Flower"
- **Learning point**: Plants need care and patience
- **Word group**: Seed, water, grow, flower
- **Summary**: Kiko plants a seed. She waits and waits. Mimi helps water it. A flower blooms. Kiko is so proud.
- **Repeated phrase**: "Water, wait, bloom!"

### Episode 6: "Waiting for a Turn"
- **Learning point**: Waiting is hard but okay
- **Word group**: Wait, turn, patient, soon
- **Summary**: Kiko wants to swing but Mimi is swinging. She waits. Opa says "Soon." When it's her turn, Kiko is so happy.
- **Repeated phrase**: "Wait, wait, my turn!"

### Episode 7: "Colours Everywhere"
- **Learning point**: Recognising and naming colours
- **Word group**: Red, blue, yellow, green, colour
- **Summary**: Kiko points at different things and names their colours. Mimi finds something each colour. They make a rainbow together.
- **Repeated phrase**: "What colour is this?"

### Episode 8: "The Soft Wind"
- **Learning point**: Nature is gentle and fun
- **Word group**: Wind, blow, soft, hair
- **Summary**: A gentle wind blows. Kiko's hair flies. The pompom trees sway. Mimi's ears wiggle. They laugh and play with the wind.
- **Repeated phrase**: "Blow, blow, blow!"

### Episode 9: "Finding Shapes"
- **Learning point**: Shapes are everywhere
- **Word group**: Circle, round, square, triangle
- **Summary**: Kiko finds circles everywhere — the sun, a door, a stepping stone. Mimi finds a triangle leaf. Opa finds a square window.
- **Repeated phrase**: "Round like the sun!"

### Episode 10: "Goodnight, Moon"
- **Learning point**: Saying goodnight is a routine
- **Word group**: Moon, stars, goodnight, sleep
- **Summary**: Kiko sees the moon. She waves goodnight. Mimi yawns. They say goodnight to the stars. Opa hums a lullaby.
- **Repeated phrase**: "Goodnight, moon. Goodnight, stars."

## 17. AI Video Consistency Guidelines

### Character Consistency
- Always use the same colour palette per character
- Kiko: Coral Pink outfit, brown hair, big eyes
- Mimi: Soft Blue fur, long ears, white belly
- Opa: Warm Green feathers, round body, big glasses
- Never change their appearance between episodes

### World Consistency
- Always the same green hills
- Always the same blue sky
- Always the same round houses
- Always the same pompom trees
- Always the same paths

### Prompt Structure
Always include in every AI prompt:
1. Style: "soft 3D cartoon, rounded shapes, pastel colours"
2. Characters: exact descriptions with colours
3. Location: specific place in Pompom Hills
4. Mood: warm, gentle, safe
5. Lighting: soft daylight, no harsh shadows
6. Avoid: "no sharp corners, no dark scenes, no realistic textures"

### Reference Images
Create reference images for:
- Each character (front, side, back)
- The valley overview
- The big pompom tree
- Each house colour
- The sky (day, sunset, night)

### Repetition Prompts
Use the same base prompt for similar scenes:
- "Soft 3D cartoon world, rounded hills, pastel colours, warm daylight, gentle characters, toy-like appearance"

## 18. Final One-Paragraph Pitch

**Pompom Hills** is a gentle animated series for toddlers aged 18 months to 4 years, set in a soft, rounded valley where everything feels like a cuddly toy come to life. The world is made of round hills, pompom trees, curved paths, and cosy houses with sticker-like doors. The show follows Kiko, a curious little girl, and Mimi, her rabbit best friend, as they discover simple things in their valley — finding a lost toy, sharing a snack, hearing rain, planting a flower — always guided by Opa, a wise old owl who watches over them with warmth and patience. Each episode is 2.5 to 4 minutes long, with very simple English, repeated phrases, and soft music. The visual style is warm, pastel, and toy-like — richer than Pocoyo but simpler than Disney — with no sharp corners, no scary moments, and no fast movement. Every episode teaches one small emotional skill through gentle storytelling, repetition, and kindness. Pompom Hills is designed to be visually consistent across AI-generated episodes, with clear character designs, a defined colour palette, and a world that is easy to recreate. It is a safe, warm, and lovable world that toddlers will want to visit again and again.
