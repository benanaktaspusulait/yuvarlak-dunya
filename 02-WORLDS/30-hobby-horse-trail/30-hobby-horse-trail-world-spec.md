---

## Purpose

Hobby Horse Trail is a soft nature path where children walk with toy hobby horses — a world of imagination, movement, and cooperation without competition. It is not a race track; it is a pretend-play adventure trail where every step brings joy.

---

## Immutable Identity

The following elements define the canonical identity of Hobby Horse Trail and must never change:

- Curved soft nature path with grass borders
- Colourful bunting flags along the path
- Small wooden bridge
- Flower arch
- Bell pole with round bell
- Hobby horses in 5 colours: coral pink, light blue, yellow, green, purple
- Round bushes and pompom trees
- No racing, no competition, no speed
- Soft, rounded, handcrafted materials throughout

---

## Visual Identity

A winding soft nature path bordered by green grass and round bushes. Colourful bunting flags stretch along the path between small trees. A curved wooden bridge crosses a gentle dip. A flower arch marks the midpoint. A bell pole stands ready to ring. Round bushes and small pompom trees frame the scene. The overall feeling is a "pretend-play adventure" — safe, colourful, and infinitely imaginative.

---

## World Layout

The world reads as one continuous winding path. The entrance has hobby horses waiting. The path curves gently through decorative features — bridge, arch, bell — and ends at a finish sign. There are no separate zones — just one flowing trail from start to finish.

---

## Spatial Relationships

- The entrance always has hobby horses and a welcome sign.
- The bunting flags are always visible along the path.
- The bridge always appears at the path's gentle dip.
- The flower arch always marks the midpoint.
- The bell pole is always visible near the rest area.
- The finish sign always marks the trail's end.

---

## Playable Areas

**Entrance** — Purpose: where horses are chosen and the adventure begins. Visual identity: welcome sign, hobby horses in 5 colours, first bunting. Important props: horses, sign, bunting. Typical use: horse selection, departure.

**Path Start** — Purpose: the beginning of the journey. Visual identity: curved path, bunting overhead, green grass. Important props: path, bunting, grass. Typical use: walking, colour discovery.

**Bridge Section** — Purpose: a small crossing moment. Visual identity: low wooden bridge over gentle dip. Important props: bridge. Typical use: crossing, looking down, transition.

**Flower Arch** — Purpose: the decorative midpoint. Visual identity: rounded arch covered in flowers. Important props: arch. Typical use: passing through, pausing, wonder.

**Bell & Rest Area** — Purpose: interaction and rest. Visual identity: bell pole, round log for sitting. Important props: bell, log. Typical use: ringing bell, resting, story time.

**Finish Area** — Purpose: the celebration point. Visual identity: finish sign with flowers. Important props: finish sign. Typical use: "We made it together!" moment.

## Materials

Hobby horses: soft plush heads on smooth wooden sticks. Path: soft grass with round stepping stones. Bridge: natural wood, low and safe. Arch: flowers on rounded frame. Bell: wood and soft metal ring. Log: smooth round wood. All surfaces read as soft, safe, and handcrafted. No sharp edges, no hard materials, no competitive elements.

---

## Lighting

Primary light is warm golden sunlight. The path is evenly lit with soft shadows from trees and bushes. The bunting catches light creating colourful patterns. The overall feeling is bright, warm, and inviting.

## Scale

Everything is sized for small preschool children. The path is wide enough for comfortable walking. The bridge is low with no steep sides. The bell is at child height. The hobby horses are proportioned for small hands. The overall feeling is accessible and safe.

---

## Atmosphere

Playful. Imaginative. Cooperative. Colourful. Joyful. Every surface reads as soft and inviting. The mood is consistently happy and unhurried — a place where children feel "let's go on an adventure together!"

---

## Consistency Rules

- The path is always curved and soft — never straight, never hard.
- Bunting is always present along the path — never absent.
- Hobby horses are always 5 colours — never fewer, never different.
- No racing elements, no timers, no finish lines (competitive style).
- No sharp edges, no hard materials, no dangerous features.
- The decorative features are always present — bridge, arch, bell.

---

## Generation Considerations

The trail must be generated as a gentle, imaginative path — not as a race track or competitive course. Keep all shapes rounded. Keep the atmosphere playful and cooperative. The trail's identity — its imagination, its joy, its cooperation — must be preserved exactly across every generation.

## Generation Failures — Quick Reference

| Failure | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Race track appearance | Remove all racing elements |
| Timer or scoreboard present | Remove — no competition |
| Sharp obstacles | Round all shapes |
| Speed elements | Slow everything down |
| Real horses present | Replace with hobby horses |
| Fewer than 5 horse colours | Add missing colours |
| No bunting visible | Add colourful flags |
| Sharp edges anywhere | Smooth all forms |

## Visual Richness Layer

> See `02-WORLDS/GLOBAL_ENVIRONMENT_STANDARD.md` § Visual Richness Principle.
> Hobby Horse Trail must never be reduced to "a path with some flags."

This layer protects Hobby Horse Trail from becoming too plain. Keep the
world readable and the winding path silhouette clear — add only the
canon-approved charm details below. Do not add random objects. Do not
remove the imaginative, cooperative, joyful adventure-trail identity.

**Allowed charm details (use, do not omit):**
- HH-ARCH-01, the flower arch at the trail's midpoint — primary landmark, always present in midpoint shots
- HH-BRIDGE-01, the small curved wooden bridge over the gentle dip
- round stepping stones set into the soft grass path
- HH-BELL-01, the wooden bell pole near the rest area
- HH-LOG-01, the round resting log near the story sign
- HH-BUNTING-01, gently swaying colourful bunting flags along the path

**Forbidden clutter:** empty plain dirt path with no decoration, race track lane markings or timing lines, timer or scoreboard or competitive finish-line tape, real or realistic horses, sharp fences or hurdles or angular obstacles, speed or hazard signage, generic park path indistinguishable from any other trail.

### Art Direction Layer (use together with Technical Canon above)

Technical Canon = geometry, landmarks, colours, scale, lighting, forbidden
elements (defined above in this spec). Add this Art Direction Layer whenever
writing or regenerating a Hero View prompt — technical-only prompts tend to
produce safe but boring paths:

```text
premium preschool animation, handcrafted toy-set feeling, cheerful
storybook adventure-trail atmosphere, storybook beauty, thumbnail-appeal
composition, makes a child want to pick a hobby horse and ride off down
the path
```

### Hero View Quality Tests

Before accepting a Hero View, it must pass:

1. **Silhouette Test** — recognisable as Hobby Horse Trail from the winding path shape and flower arch alone?
2. **Colour Test** — recognisable from the bunting and hobby-horse colour palette alone?
3. **Charm Test** — does it make a child want to pick a hobby horse and set off on an adventure?

If any test fails, reject — see Generation Failures table above and
`30-hobby-horse-trail-bible.md` § Forbidden Over-Simplification.
