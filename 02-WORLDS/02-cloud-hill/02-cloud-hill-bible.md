## Overview

Cloud Hill, Pompom Hills'in en yüksek tepesi. Gökyüzüne uzanan yumuşak yeşil bir tepe — bulutların en yakın göründüğü, rüzgarın en hafif estiği yer.

---

## Purpose

Gökyüzünü izleme ve hayal kurma mekanı. Çocuklar buraya çıkarak dünyayı yukarıdan görür, bulutları yakından izler, yağmurun nereden geldiğini keşfeder.

---

## Why This World Exists ⭐

Cloud Hill, çocuklar için "yukarı bakma" anının somut karşılığıdır. Yüksek bir tepeden gökyüzüne bakmak sonsuzluk hissi verir. Yağmur, gökkuşağı, bulutlar — hepsi buradan başlar. Bu mekan merakı besler ve büyük soruların güvenle sorulabileceği bir alan yaratır.

---

## Emotional Purpose

### Why does this place exist emotionally?

Cloud Hill, huzur ve hayal gücünü temsil eder. Yüksekte olmak özgürlük hissi verir; açık gökyüzü sonsuz keşif alanı sunar.

### How do children feel when they arrive?

Sakin, meraklı, özgür. Zirvede durmanın getirdiği sessiz bir tatmin ve "daha neler var?" merakı.

### What stories naturally belong here?

Yağmur ve bulut keşfi, gökkuşağı izleme, gün batımı seyretme, yıldız sayma, rüzgarla oynama.

### What emotional experiences does this world support?

Huzur · Hayal gücü · Merak · Keşif · Sessiz hayranlık

## Visual Identity

### General

- Yuvarlak, yumuşak eğimli tek tepe — sivri ya da sarp hiçbir şey yok
- Tepe düz bir plato ile biter — oturulabilir, geniş, sade
- Açık gökyüzü her zaman görünür — yüzde altmış alan gökyüzü, yüzde kırk tepe
- Büyük yuvarlak bulutlar tepede süzülür
- Yumuşak rüzgar çimenleri ve saçları sallandırır

### Tepe

- Kısa, yumuşak, açık yeşil çimen (#A5D6A7)
- Eğimli yamacın tepesinde düz geniş alan
- Çimler yüzeyden hafif yükselir — tüylü his
- Hiç taş, çıkıntı ya da sivri öğe yok

### Gökyüzü

- Açık, geniş, pale mavi (#E3F2FD → #BBDEFB zirvede)
- 2–3 büyük yuvarlak pamuklu bulut — hiçbiri karanlık değil
- Bulutlar yavaşça sürüklenir — soldan sağa, çok yavaş
- Güneş daima sağ üst köşede, yumuşak ve parlak değil

### Horizon

- Uzakta diğer tepeler pastel siluet olarak görünür
- Hiçbir bina, yol ya da modern öğe ufukta yok
- Ufuk çizgisi yumuşak ve alçak — gökyüzü ezici çoğunlukta

### Rüzgar

- Görünmez ama hissedilir — çimen dalgalanması, saç hareketi, hafif ses
- Sürekli, yumuşak, değişmez hızda
- Hiçbir zaman sert ya da ani değil

## Canonical Prompt Reference Pack

> OpenArt referans sistemi kaldırıldı. Prompt bazlı üretim kullanılır.
> Her view için aşağıdaki prompt taslakları kullanılır.

### View 01 — Hero View

**Purpose:** Master environment referansı. Karaktersiz, saf mekan.

**Framing:** Yüzde yüz ortam. Karakter yok. Hayvan yok. Saf mekan referansı.

**Composition:**
- Ön planda kısa yumuşak yeşil çimen, tepe düzlüğü
- Orta planda ufuk çizgisi ve pastel tepe siluetleri
- Arka planda açık pale mavi gökyüzü, 2–3 büyük beyaz yuvarlak bulut
- Güneş sağ üstte, yumuşak
- Hiç sivri öğe, hiç yapı, hiç modern detay

**Lighting:** Sabah güneşi, yumuşak, soldan geliyor. Gölge yok ya da çok yumuşak.

**Mood:** Huzur, sessizlik, açıklık.

**Must always be visible:**
- Tepe düzlüğü
- Açık gökyüzü (kare yüksekliğinin en az %60'ı)
- En az 2 büyük beyaz bulut

**Must never appear:**
- Karakter
- Yapı, çit, yol
- Karanlık ya da dramatik bulut
- Sivri taş ya da çıkıntı

### View 02 — Climb View

**Purpose:** Yamaca tırmanış referansı.

**Camera position:** Yamaç ortasında, yukarı bakış açısı.

**Used for:**
- Sahnenin açılışı — karakterin tepiye çıktığı an
- "Nereye gidiyoruz?" sorusunun cevabı

### View 03 — Summit View

**Purpose:** Tepede oturma referansı. Gökyüzü bağlantısı.

**Camera position:** Tepe düzlüğünde, gökyüzüne bakış.

**Used for:**
- Bulut izleme sahneleri
- Konuşma ve dinlenme sahneleri
- Yağmur öncesi gökyüzü gözlemi

### View 04 — Detail View

**Purpose:** Doku ve malzeme referansı.

**Camera position:** Yakın plan — çimen dokusu, bulut detayı.

**Composition:**
- Çimen yüzeyi yakın — tüylü, yumuşak, açık yeşil
- Ufukta bulut parçası
- Renk paleti doğrulaması: yeşil + pale mavi + beyaz

**Purpose for prompts:** Malzeme tutarlılığını sağlamak — her sahnede aynı çimen dokusu ve bulut stili.

## World Identity Lock

> These elements define the canonical visual identity of Cloud Hill. Every future generation must preserve them exactly. No reinterpretation. No redesign.

### Locked Elements

| Element | Canonical Definition | Locked |
|---------|---------------------|:------:|
| Hill silhouette | Single smooth dome — wide base, gently rounded top, no peak, no flat plateau edge visible in wide shots | 🔒 |
| Skyline shape | Unbroken soft curve — the hill meets the sky in one continuous arc, never jagged | 🔒 |
| Horizon height | Horizon sits at 35–40% from the bottom of the frame in all wide shots | 🔒 |
| Sky-to-ground ratio | Sky 60–65% / Ground 35–40% in establishing and wide shots | 🔒 |
| Cloud count | 2–3 clouds per frame — never 0, never more than 4 | 🔒 |
| Cloud spacing | Clouds are spread across the upper sky — never clustered, never touching | 🔒 |
| Cloud style | Large, rounded, fully white, soft-edged, cumulus form — never wispy, never storm-shaped | 🔒 |
| Sun position | Upper right quadrant, always — never centred, never behind clouds | 🔒 |
| Grass appearance | Short, even, soft plush texture, open green (#A5D6A7) — no tall grass, no yellow patches | 🔒 |
| Overall composition | Open, calm, sky-dominant — the world breathes upward, not inward | 🔒 |

### Acceptable Natural Variations

These elements may shift slightly between generations without breaking identity:

- Exact cloud shape (clouds may vary organically as long as they remain white and rounded)
- Exact grass blade detail (texture variation is fine, colour must stay within range)
- Horizon silhouette of distant hills (may shift slightly in detail, must remain pastel and low)
- Time of day lighting (morning vs. midday vs. evening — as long as mood rules are respected)

### What Must Never Change

- Sky-to-ground ratio falling below 60/40
- More than 4 clouds or zero clouds in frame
- Hill silhouette becoming pointed, jagged, or asymmetrical
- Horizon rising above 45% of frame height
- Any modern object, structure, or road appearing anywhere
- Grass turning yellow, brown, or oversaturated green

## Camera Identity

> The permanent camera standard for Cloud Hill. All shots — regardless of story — must follow this identity.

### Default Lens

35mm equivalent. Wide enough to show sky dominance. Narrow enough to keep the world intimate and warm. Never wider than 28mm (distorts the hill). Never tighter than 50mm (loses the sky).

### Child Eye Level

All shots default to 0.75–0.80 m camera height — the natural eye level of a standing preschool child. This makes the sky feel expansive and the hill feel safe rather than towering.

| Height | When Used |
|--------|-----------|
| 0.80 m | Standing character, wide and establishing shots |
| 0.75 m | Sitting character, intimate conversation shots |
| 0.60 m | Close-up object or grass detail |
| POV | Cloud-watching only — free height, looking upward |

### Movement Philosophy

The camera on Cloud Hill moves like a slow breath. Nothing is sudden. Nothing cuts hard. The world is still and the camera respects that stillness.

### Allowed Movements

| Movement | Speed | When |
|----------|-------|------|
| Slow pan | Very slow — 3–5 seconds across | Establishing, environment reveal |
| Gentle tilt up | 2–3 seconds | Face-to-sky transition |
| Slow push in | Very slow — 4+ seconds | Emotional close moment |
| Static hold | — | Dialogue, observation, silence |

### Forbidden Movements

```
✗ Fast pan or whip pan
✗ Handheld shake or bounce
✗ Zoom in or zoom out
✗ Crane drop or fast vertical movement
✗ 360° rotation
✗ Drone-style fly-over
```

### Framing Consistency

- Subject is never placed in the centre of frame — slightly left or right of centre
- Sky always occupies at least 50% of frame even in medium shots
- Horizon is always visible in wide shots — never cut off by framing
- Characters are always framed with the sky behind them — never the ground

### Transition Rules

- Scene openings: always begin with environment wide shot before introducing character
- Scene closings: always end wider than the previous shot — pull back to sky
- Between shots: cut or slow dissolve only — no wipe, no flash

## Lighting Identity

> The canonical lighting system for Cloud Hill. Must be consistent across every scene in every episode that uses this world.

### Light Direction

Primary light comes from the upper left in all standard daytime scenes. This is the canonical sun position for Cloud Hill and must not change between shots in the same scene.

| Time | Direction | Quality |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Morning | Upper left, warm | Softest — golden, gentle |
| Midday | Top centre, neutral | Brightest — even, clean |
| Evening | Upper left, golden-orange | Warmest — long soft shadows |
| Night | Top, cool moonlight | Softest — pale blue-white |

### Softness

All light on Cloud Hill is soft. There are no hard shadows, no sharp shadow edges, no spotlight effects. The light feels like it is being filtered through a thin layer of high cloud at all times — bright but never harsh.

### Ambient Fill

A gentle ambient fill always lifts the shadow side of every object and character. No side of any subject falls into true darkness. The darkest shadow value on Cloud Hill is approximately 70% of the highlight value — never lower.

### Shadow Density

| Shadow Type | Density | Notes |
|-------------|---------|-------|
| Character shadow on grass | Very soft, 20–30% opacity | Short — never longer than character height |
| Grass shadow detail | Barely visible | Ground stays bright and readable |
| Cloud shadow on hill | None — clouds do not cast shadows on the hill | This keeps the ground clean and safe |

### Exposure Behaviour

The world is always slightly bright — not overexposed, but lifted. Midtones are open and readable. Nothing is crushed in the darks. Highlights are soft — never blown.

### Continuity Rules

- Light direction must not change between shots in the same scene
- If a scene begins in morning light, every shot in that scene stays in morning light
- Cloud shadows are not cast on the ground — they exist only in the sky
- Character lighting must match environment lighting — no isolated character illumination

## Colour Identity

> The permanent colour profile for Cloud Hill. Every future shot must preserve this profile unless the story explicitly requires a shift (e.g. a deliberate sunset or night scene).

### White Balance

Warm daylight — approximately 5500K. Slightly warm, never cool or blue-shifted in daytime scenes.

### Exposure

+0.3 to +0.5 stops above neutral. The world is bright, open, and lifted.

### Contrast

Low contrast. The distance between the brightest and darkest values is narrow. Everything reads clearly. Nothing disappears into shadow.

### Saturation

Medium-low. Colours are present and readable but never vivid or punchy. The palette feels hand-painted, not digital.

### Pastel Palette

| Element | Colour | Hex |
|---------|--------|-----|
| Grass | Soft open green | #A5D6A7 |
| Sky upper | Pale blue | #BBDEFB |
| Sky lower | Very pale blue-white | #E3F2FD |
| Clouds | Pure soft white | #F5F5F5 |
| Distant hills | Pale grey-green pastel | #C8E6C9 |
| Daisy white | Clean white | #FFFFFF |
| Daisy yellow centre | Soft warm yellow | #FFF59D |
| Sitting stone | Smooth pale grey | #ECEFF1 |

### Highlight Behaviour

Highlights are soft and rolled — they never clip to pure white. The brightest point in any frame is a cloud highlight, which should feel like soft cotton, not a white wall.

### Shadow Behaviour

Shadows are lifted and warm — never cold, never black. The darkest value in any standard daytime frame is a warm mid-grey, approximately #9E9E9E or lighter.

### Forbidden Colour Shifts

```
✗ Cool blue or teal shift in daytime scenes
✗ Desaturation to grey or monochrome
✗ High-contrast or HDR look
✗ Neon or oversaturated colours anywhere
✗ Pure black in any element
✗ Pure white except soft cloud highlights
✗ Grass shifting to yellow-green or olive
✗ Sky shifting to deep blue or dramatic purple
```

## Environmental Sound Identity

> The reusable ambient sound standard for Cloud Hill. These rules apply to every scene, every episode.

### Wind

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Intensity | Soft and constant — never gusting, never silent |
| Tone | Low, smooth, slightly breathy — not whistling |
| Variation | Very slight — rises gently with emotional peaks, drops in quiet moments |
| Forbidden | Howling, sharp gusts, wind that sounds threatening |

### Bird Frequency

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Presence | Always present but distant — birds are below on the hillside, not at the summit |
| Call type | Single melodic calls, not flocks or chattering |
| Frequency | One call every 8–12 seconds on average |
| Volume | Low — never louder than the wind layer |

### Ambience Level

The total ambient sound of Cloud Hill is quiet. It should feel like the quietest outdoor space in Pompom Hills. The wind and birds together sit at approximately 20–25% of the maximum audio level. Dialogue always sits clearly above ambience.

### Silence Ratio

Silence — or near-silence with only the faintest wind — is a valid and intentional sound state for Cloud Hill. It should be used at emotional peaks: the moment a character first sees the clouds, the moment rain begins, the moment a question is asked and not yet answered.

### Forbidden Sounds

```
✗ Traffic, machinery, or any modern sound
✗ Crowd noise or group laughter from the summit
✗ Thunder or storm sounds
✗ Insect buzz (bees, flies)
✗ Sudden loud sounds of any kind
✗ Music that was not authored for the scene
```

### Colour Identity

```
✓ Grass is #A5D6A7 — not yellow, not olive, not dark?
✓ Sky is pale blue — not deep blue, not purple?
✓ Clouds are soft white — not grey, not cream?
✓ No neon or oversaturated colours anywhere?
✓ No pure black in any element?
✓ Overall warmth — not cool-shifted?
✓ Low contrast — no HDR look?
```

### Scale Identity

```
✓ Characters feel child-proportioned against the hill?
✓ Sitting stone is right scale for a small character?
✓ Daisies are small and low — not oversized?
✓ Clouds feel large and close — not tiny and distant?
```

### Atmosphere Identity

```
✓ Scene feels open, calm, and sky-dominant?
✓ No threatening or dramatic visual tone?
✓ Wind implied by grass or hair movement?
✓ No modern objects in any part of the frame?
```

### World Recognition

```
✓ Would a viewer recognise this as Cloud Hill from a previous episode?
✓ Does the hill silhouette match the canonical dome shape?
✓ Does the sky-to-ground ratio feel consistent with other Cloud Hill frames?
✓ Could this frame be placed next to another Cloud Hill frame without jarring inconsistency?
```

## Production Summary

Cloud Hill is a canonical reusable world. Every time it appears — regardless of episode, regardless of characters, regardless of story — it must feel like exactly the same place.

The world has one defining quality: it belongs to the sky. More sky than ground. More open than enclosed. More still than active. Characters come here to look up, wonder, ask questions, and be quietly amazed. The hill itself is the simplest possible form — a smooth dome of soft green grass. Everything else is sky.

For production, Cloud Hill is low-complexity and high-consistency. It has few props, a defined colour palette, a locked silhouette, and a clear camera language. This makes it one of the fastest worlds to generate reliably — but only if the identity rules are respected on every frame.

The single most common failure point is the sky ratio dropping below 60%. Always verify this first. The second most common failure is clouds becoming grey, dark, or dramatically lit. Clouds on Cloud Hill are always white, always soft, always safe.

Every future episode using Cloud Hill should load the Hero View as its master reference, verify the Colour Identity before accepting any frame, and run the Production QA checklist before signing off on any shot. The world does not change. Only the story does.
