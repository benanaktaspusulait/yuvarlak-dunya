## Purpose

Rosie's Rose Garden is a small, intimate flower garden nestled at the eastern
edge of Flower Hill. It is Pompom Hills' most beautiful and gentle outdoor
space — where children discover colour, fragrance, and the quiet joy of
watching living things grow.

---

## Immutable Identity

The following elements define the canonical identity of Rosie's Rose Garden
and must never change:

- Open garden layout — no enclosed walls or buildings
- Roses in three colours: pink, red, and yellow — always rounded, never spiky
- Heart-shaped flower beds as the garden's signature motif
- Low white rounded fence defining the boundary
- Butterflies always present — part of the garden's living identity
- Winding soft dirt path through the garden
- No thorns anywhere — not on roses, not on any plant
- Pastel, handcrafted material feel throughout
- Warm, gentle daylight atmosphere

---

## Visual Identity

A low, open flower garden read instantly as soft and welcoming. Every element
curves — the fence posts have rounded tops, the rose petals are full and
plump, the path winds gently rather than cutting straight. The garden feels
designed for small hands and gentle exploration. Heart-shaped flower beds are
the single strongest visual signature: they appear at the garden's centre and
should read clearly from any camera height. Butterflies drift through the
space continuously, giving the garden movement and life without any sense of
hurry. The overall feeling is a "living gift" — colourful, fragrant, and
completely safe.

---

## World Layout

The garden reads as one continuous flowing outdoor space with soft zones rather
than divided sections. A welcoming entrance with a low white gate opens onto
the main path. The path curves through the rose beds and leads naturally to the
heart flower centre, which is the garden's focal point. A quieter back corner
sits behind the tallest rose bushes — slightly more hidden, slightly more calm.
There are no walls, no buildings, and no enclosed spaces. The sky is always
visible above.

## Spatial Relationships

- The entrance gate is always visible from outside — it signals "you are welcome here."
- The heart centre is the garden's visual focal point and should be readable from both the entrance and the path.
- Rose beds line both sides of the main path so characters always walk among roses, never past them.
- The quiet back corner is partially screened by slightly taller rose bushes but never fully hidden.
- Butterflies move throughout all zones, not concentrated in one area.
- The sky is always visible above — no canopy, no overhead coverage.
- Lily's Lavender Farm is visible as a soft purple haze in the background when shooting from the entrance.

## Playable Areas

**Entrance Gate** — Purpose: welcoming threshold into the garden. Visual identity: low white rounded fence gate, slightly open, round fence posts with ball-top finials. Important props: gate, fence section. Typical use: arrival, first discovery, character greeting.

**Rose Path** — Purpose: the main exploration corridor through the garden. Visual identity: soft winding dirt path, roses on both sides in pink, red, and yellow, petals occasionally drifting. Important props: roses (all three colours), soft path. Typical use: walking, smelling, counting colours, butterfly chasing.

**Heart Centre** — Purpose: the garden's emotional and visual focal point. Visual identity: two large heart-shaped flower beds side by side — one pink roses, one red roses — with a small round yellow flower cluster at the join. Important props: heart beds, centre flowers. Typical use: the "wow" moment, sitting together, quiet observation.

**Butterfly Resting Spot** — Purpose: a calm area where butterflies naturally gather. Visual identity: a low flat stone surrounded by yellow roses, always at least one butterfly resting. Important props: flat stone, yellow roses. Typical use: patient waiting, gentle observation, stillness.

**Quiet Back Corner** — Purpose: a slightly more secluded, calmer zone. Visual identity: slightly taller rose bushes creating a soft enclosure without walls, soft grass underneath. Important props: tall rose bushes, soft ground. Typical use: quiet moments, whispering, gentle discoveries.

## Materials

Rose petals: soft matte, slightly velvety surface, toy-like fullness — never
glossy, never translucent. Rose stems and leaves: smooth matte green, rounded
edges, no visible thorns or spiky texture anywhere. Fence and gate: smooth
matte white, rounded posts, ball finials. Path: soft matte compressed earth,
slightly warm in tone, gentle texture. Grass: soft plush texture, short and
even. Butterfly wings: soft matte with gentle pattern — no iridescent or
metallic finish. Flat stone: smooth matte grey, gently rounded edges. No
glass, no metal, no sharp-edged leaves, no realistic thorns anywhere.

---

## Lighting

Primary light is warm soft daylight. Morning brings the most golden quality —
roses glow warmly and butterfly wings catch gentle light. Midday is bright
and even with minimal shadow, colours at their most vivid. Evening turns
softly golden-orange, elongating shadows gently across the path, giving the
garden a tranquil closing feeling. There is no night scene for this garden.
Shadows are always soft and short — never hard, never dark. Light always
feels like it is coming from slightly above and behind the camera to keep
flower faces fully lit and readable.

---

## Colour Palette

Roses: pink (#F48FB1), red (#EF5350), yellow (#FFD54F) — these three are
fixed and must not shift to other hues. Fence and gate: white (#FFFFFF).
Path: warm light tan (#D7B899). Grass: soft green (#C8E6C9). Butterfly wings:
soft orange and yellow (#FFB74D, #FFF176) with gentle brown markings —
no electric or neon colours. Sky: pale blue (#E3F2FD) with soft white clouds.
Heart bed soil: warm dark earth (#6D4C41), barely visible beneath flowers.
The palette stays within this small, warm pastel set across every zone.

---

## Scale

The garden is intimate rather than vast — a child should feel surrounded by
beauty, not overwhelmed by scale. Rose bushes reach roughly chest-height on
Kiko (approximately 60–70 cm) so flowers are always at eye level for a
small character. The fence is low enough to see clearly over. The heart beds
are large enough to read clearly from a standing character's eye level. The
path is wide enough for two small characters to walk side by side comfortably.
The flat stone is just large enough for one small character to sit on.

---

## Atmosphere

Gentle. Fragrant. Alive. Every frame should feel like a quiet gift. There
is always subtle movement — petals drift, butterflies shift, rose bushes sway
very gently. Nothing is ever still, but nothing is ever fast. The garden
feels like it is always in the middle of a perfect warm morning, regardless
of actual time of day rendered. The dominant emotion is delight — the soft,
wide-eyed delight of finding something unexpectedly beautiful.

---

## Consistency Rules

- Roses are always pink, red, and yellow — no other colours, no white or purple roses.
- No thorns anywhere — not implied, not visible, not suggested by any texture.
- The heart shape of the centre beds must always be readable — never distorted into circles or ovals.
- The fence stays low, white, and rounded — it never becomes tall, spiked, or decorative in a complex way.
- Butterflies are always present — minimum one visible in any wide shot.
- The path always winds — it is never straight.
- No buildings, no structures, no overhead coverage.
- No dark or high-contrast lighting.
- No wilted, broken, or dead flowers anywhere in the garden.
- The garden's boundary is defined by the fence only — not by walls or hedges.

---

## Generation Considerations

The heart shape of the centre flower beds is the most important identity element
and the most common failure point — verify it reads clearly as two distinct
hearts before accepting any generation. Rose petals must look plump and full,
never flat or thin. Keep all fence posts rounded with ball-top finials — pointed
posts will feel unsafe and wrong. Butterflies should be positioned mid-air or
resting, never in aggressive flight poses. Keep the colour palette strictly
within the defined set — do not introduce purple, white, or blue roses even if
they feel decorative. Avoid any hint of thorns in texture or silhouette. Keep
the sky open and pale — no dramatic clouds, no moody atmosphere.
