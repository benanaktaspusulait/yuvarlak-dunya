## Purpose

Lily's Lavender Farm is a vast, round lavender field that functions as the
sensory heart of Pompom Hills. It is where colour, scent, and nature come
alive through the simple beauty of purple lavender rows and gentle windmills.

---

## Immutable Identity

The following elements define the canonical identity of Lily's Lavender Farm and must never change:

- Wide, round lavender field with deep purple rows
- Round wooden bee houses
- Round white windmill with wooden blades
- Stone paths between lavender rows
- Warm golden sunlight
- Soft, handcrafted, toy-like materials
- Calm, aromatic atmosphere

---

## Visual Identity

A wide, fully open farm with no rectangles, no straight edges, no sharp
corners anywhere. Soft matte surfaces give every element a natural,
handcrafted quality. The palette is warm and aromatic, centred on deep
purple lavender with green leaves and warm wood tones. The overall feeling
is a "living toy farm" — approachable, calm, and visually simple enough for
a preschool audience to read instantly.

---

## World Layout

The world reads as one continuous journey rather than a set of separate
areas. The farm gate leads through lavender rows to bee houses, windmill,
observation path, and rest bench. All zones are open and connected without
fences or barriers between them.

---

## Spatial Relationships

- The farm gate is visible from the path.
- The lavender rows can be seen from the observation path.
- The bee houses are visible from the lavender rows.
- The windmill is visible from every zone.
- The observation path provides a view of the entire farm.
- The rest bench is visible from the observation path but feels separate.

---

## Playable Areas

**Farm Gate** — Purpose: welcoming approach to the farm. Visual identity: wooden gate, welcome sign, lavender pots. Important props: gate, sign. Typical use: arrival, first scent.

**Lavender Rows** — Purpose: main lavender field zone. Visual identity: deep purple lavender rows, green leaves, warm sunlight. Important props: lavender rows. Typical use: scent discovery, colour exploration.

**Bee House Area** — Purpose: bee habitat zone. Visual identity: round wooden bee houses, flowers nearby. Important props: bee houses. Typical use: nature observation, gentle buzzing.

**Windmill Zone** — Purpose: wind energy and movement zone. Visual identity: round white windmill with wooden blades, gentle rotation. Important props: windmill. Typical use: movement observation, rhythmic counting.

**Observation Path** — Purpose: viewing the entire farm. Visual identity: stone path between lavender rows, wide view. Important props: path. Typical use: walking, perspective.

**Rest Bench** — Purpose: rest and quiet sitting. Visual identity: wooden bench under shade tree. Important props: bench, tree. Typical use: resting, scent absorption.

---

## Materials

Lavender: soft, round, textured, purple. Bee houses: smooth matte wood,
rounded. Windmill: smooth matte white with wooden blades. Paths: smooth
round stone. Ground: soft natural grass. No hard surfaces, no sharp edges,
no artificial materials anywhere. Everything reads as natural and safe.

---

## Lighting

Primary light is warm golden sunlight. Morning brings soft golden light
through lavender; midday is bright and even; evening turns deep golden-orange
with longer soft shadows; night the farm rests in soft moonlight. Outdoor
lighting is always natural daylight. Shadows are always soft — never hard,
never dark.

---

## Colour Palette

Lavender: deep purple. Leaves: green. Bee houses: warm brown. Windmill:
white with brown blades. Paths: warm grey. Grass: soft green. The palette
stays within this warm, aromatic set across every zone.

---

## Scale

Everything is sized for a small preschool child. The farm is wide but
intimate — lavender rows are child-height, bee houses are low and accessible,
the windmill is modest but visible. The farm feels spacious enough to walk
but cozy enough to feel safe.

---

## Atmosphere

Warm. Soft. Safe. Aromatic. Every surface reads as natural and gentle.
The mood is always calm and inviting — full of scent, colour, and gentle
movement. The farm never feels empty or quiet during the day.

---

## Consistency Rules

- The farm is always a wide lavender field with purple rows — never barren, never different colours.
- Lavender stays deep purple across every generation.
- Bee houses stay round and wooden.
- Windmill stays white with wooden blades.
- No sharp corners, no thorns, no threatening bees.
- No dark or high-contrast lighting.
- The farm's core shape, colours, and proportions are never redesigned or reinterpreted.

---

## Generation Considerations

The farm must be generated as a single wide lavender space with natural
elements, not as a sparse or threatening landscape — this is the defining
trait of this world and the most common failure point to watch for. Keep all
shapes rounded with no sharp corners anywhere. Keep the colour palette
limited to the warm aromatic set defined above. Favor a soft, matte, natural
material language over any glossy, metallic, or realistic rendering. Treat
the farm's shape, colours, and proportions as a fixed identity to preserve
exactly, not to reinterpret, across every generation.

---

## Visual Richness Layer

> See `02-WORLDS/GLOBAL_ENVIRONMENT_STANDARD.md` § Visual Richness Principle.
> Lily's Lavender Farm must never be reduced to "a purple field."

**Allowed charm details:** the round white windmill with rotating wooden
blades as the primary landmark; the round wooden bee houses clustered near
the rows; the long deep-purple lavender rows in soft curved lines; the round
grey stone path winding between the rows; the wooden farm gate with its
round-top welcome sign; the wooden rest bench under the shade tree; and
gentle bee movement plus slow windmill rotation carrying scent on the breeze.

**Forbidden clutter:** thorns, scary or threatening bees, faded or dull
purple, implied bad smell, sharp corners, fast or jarring movement, dark or
high-contrast lighting, large crowds, metal surfaces, and artificial scent
effects.

### Hero View Quality Tests

1. **Silhouette Test** — Is the round white windmill with wooden blades clearly recognisable from shape alone?
2. **Colour Test** — Is the farm recognisable from the deep-purple lavender and warm wood palette alone?
3. **Charm Test** — Does the image make a child want to walk between the rows and watch the windmill turn?

If any test fails, reject.
