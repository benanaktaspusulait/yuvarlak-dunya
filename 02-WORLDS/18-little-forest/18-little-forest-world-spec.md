# Little Forest — World Creation Specification

> Source of truth: `18-little-forest-bible.md` (canonical, creative). This document
> is a production-oriented extraction for AI-assisted world generation. It does
> not replace the Bible and does not duplicate its storytelling, educational,
> character, or workflow content.

---

## Purpose

Little Forest is a small, round, magical forest that functions as the
discovery heart of Pompom Hills. It is where curiosity, adventure, and
nature come alive through the simple joy of exploring trees, mushrooms,
and friendly animals.

---

## Immutable Identity

The following elements define the canonical identity of Little Forest and must never change:

- Small, round forest with soft trees
- Round mushroom circles (magical)
- Friendly birds in branches
- Cute squirrels in tree hollows
- Soft leaf trail on the ground
- Golden light through leaves
- Warm, safe atmosphere
- No sharp corners anywhere

---

## Visual Identity

A small, fully round forest with no rectangles, no straight edges, no sharp
corners anywhere. Soft matte surfaces give every element a natural,
handcrafted quality. The palette is warm and green, centred on round trees
with colourful mushrooms and friendly animals. The overall feeling is a
"living toy forest" — approachable, magical, and visually simple enough for
a preschool audience to read instantly.

---

## World Layout

The world reads as one continuous natural space rather than a set of separate
areas. The entrance leads into the forest, which flows naturally from
mushroom circle to bird watchpoint to squirrel hollow to leaf trail to magic
clearing. All zones are open and connected without fences or barriers between
them.

---

## Spatial Relationships

- The forest entrance is visible from the path.
- The mushroom circle can be seen from the entrance.
- The bird watchpoint is visible from the mushroom circle.
- The squirrel hollow connects to the leaf trail.
- The magic clearing is glimpsed through the trees.
- The leaf trail provides a view of the entire forest.

---

## Playable Areas

**Forest Entrance** — Purpose: welcoming approach to the forest. Visual identity: round trees framing the path, leaves on the ground. Important props: trees, leaves. Typical use: arrival, first discovery.

**Mushroom Circle** — Purpose: magical mushroom discovery zone. Visual identity: round mushrooms in a circle, red caps with white dots. Important props: mushrooms. Typical use: wonder, counting.

**Bird Watchpoint** — Purpose: quiet bird observation. Visual identity: branches with friendly birds, soft light. Important props: birds, branches. Typical use: listening, watching.

**Squirrel Hollow** — Purpose: squirrel habitat zone. Visual identity: tree hollows, cute squirrels. Important props: tree hollows, squirrels. Typical use: animal friendship, following.

**Leaf Trail** — Purpose: natural path through forest. Visual identity: colourful leaves on soft ground, round stones. Important props: leaves, stones. Typical use: walking, collecting.

**Magic Clearing** — Purpose: the most magical spot in the forest. Visual identity: open space with golden light, soft grass. Important props: clearing, light. Typical use: rest, wonder.

---

## Materials

Trees: soft, round, leafy. Mushrooms: soft, round, red caps. Birds: soft,
feathered, colourful. Squirrels: soft, furry, brown. Leaves: soft, silky,
colourful. Stones: smooth, round, grey. No hard surfaces, no sharp edges,
no artificial materials anywhere. Everything reads as natural and safe.

---

## Lighting

Primary light filters through leaves. Morning brings soft golden light
through branches; midday is dappled with leaf shadows; evening turns warm
golden with long shadows; night is soft moonlight. Forest lighting is always
natural, filtering through leaves. Shadows are always soft — never hard,
never dark.

---

## Colour Palette

Trees: various greens. Mushrooms: red caps with white dots. Birds: yellow,
blue, pink. Squirrels: warm brown. Leaves: green, yellow, orange. Stones:
warm grey. The palette stays within this warm, natural set across every zone.

---

## Scale

Everything is sized for a small preschool child. The forest is intimate,
not vast — small enough to explore safely, large enough to feel like a real
forest. Trees are child-proportioned, low enough to see through. Mushrooms
are small and friendly. The forest never feels overwhelming.

---

## Atmosphere

Warm. Soft. Safe. Magical. Every surface reads as natural and gentle.
The mood is always curious and inviting — full of discovery and gentle
movement. The forest never feels dark or threatening.

---

## Consistency Rules

- The forest is always small, round, and magical — never dark, never vast.
- Trees stay round and green across every generation.
- Mushrooms stay round and magical (red caps, white dots).
- Birds stay friendly and colourful.
- Squirrels stay cute and brown.
- No sharp corners, no dark shadows, no threatening animals.
- No dark or high-contrast lighting.
- The forest's core shape, colours, and proportions are never redesigned or reinterpreted.

---

## Generation Considerations

The forest must be generated as a single warm, magical space with friendly
elements, not as a dark or threatening woodland — this is the defining trait
of this world and the most common failure point to watch for. Keep all shapes
rounded with no sharp corners anywhere. Keep the colour palette limited to
the warm natural set defined above. Favor a soft, matte, natural material
language over any glossy, metallic, or realistic rendering. Treat the
forest's shape, colours, and proportions as a fixed identity to preserve
exactly, not to reinterpret, across every generation.

## Visual Richness Layer

> See `02-WORLDS/GLOBAL_ENVIRONMENT_STANDARD.md` § Visual Richness Principle.
> Little Forest must never be reduced to "some trees."

This layer protects Little Forest from becoming too plain. Keep the round
tree silhouette and the mushroom circle readable — add only the
canon-approved charm details below. Do not add random objects. Do not remove
the safe, magical, discovery-woodland emotional identity.

**Allowed charm details:** LF-MUSHROOM-01, the round red-and-white mushroom
circle beneath the canopy, is the primary landmark and must be visible as
the destination of every path through the forest. LF-TREE-01, the cluster
of round, plush-crowned trees in varied greens, defines the forest's main
silhouette. Soft dappled light patterns filtering through the leaves and a
scattering of colourful fallen leaves along the path add surface charm.
LF-BIRD-01 glimpsed on a low branch, a small friendly squirrel peeking from
a tree hollow, and smooth round stepping stones marking the path are the
central reusable small props. Gentle motion — the sway of the tree
canopies and LF-BIRD-01 fluttering off in slow, friendly arcs — keeps the
world feeling alive without adding clutter.

**Forbidden clutter:** a dark, dense, or scary forest, no mushroom circle
visible, large or threatening creatures, sharp branches or thorny
undergrowth, tangled or impassable-looking paths, sharp corners anywhere,
and muted, cold, or desaturated colours.

### Hero View Quality Tests

Before accepting a Hero View, it must pass:

1. **Silhouette Test** — recognisable as Little Forest from the cluster of round, plush-crowned trees alone?
2. **Colour Test** — recognisable from the varied warm greens and the red-and-white mushroom circle alone?
3. **Charm Test** — does it make a child want to peek behind a tree and see what's there?

If any test fails, reject.
