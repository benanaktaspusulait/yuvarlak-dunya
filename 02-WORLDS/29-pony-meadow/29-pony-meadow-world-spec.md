---

## Purpose

Pony Meadow is a wide, round, green meadow where children meet gentle ponies.
It is where tenderness, patience, and quiet friendship come alive through the
simple joy of touching, feeding, and observing gentle animals.

---

## Immutable Identity

The following elements define the canonical identity of Pony Meadow and must never change:

- Wide, round green meadow
- 2-3 small, round, fluffy ponies (cream white, light brown, light grey)
- Low wooden fence with open gate
- Small round wooden hut with green roof
- Soft plush grass
- No riding equipment anywhere
- Warm, gentle atmosphere
- No sharp corners anywhere

---

## Visual Identity

A wide, fully round meadow with no rectangles, no straight edges, no sharp
corners anywhere. Soft matte surfaces give every element a natural,
handcrafted quality. The palette is warm and gentle, centred on green grass
with cream-brown ponies and natural wood. The overall feeling is a "living
toy meadow" — approachable, tender, and visually simple enough for a
preschool audience to read instantly.

---

## World Layout

The world reads as one continuous gentle space rather than a set of separate
areas. The gate leads into the meadow, which flows naturally from pony
grazing area to feeding corner to petting zone to observation hill to quiet
rest spot. All zones are open and connected without barriers between them.

---

## Spatial Relationships

- The meadow gate is always visible from the path.
- The pony grazing area can be seen from the gate.
- The feeding corner is visible from the grazing area.
- The petting zone is visible from the feeding corner.
- The observation hill provides a view of the entire meadow.
- The quiet rest spot is visible from the hill but feels separate.

---

**Pony Grazing Area** — Purpose: ponies roaming freely. Visual identity: green grass, ponies walking slowly, natural setting. Important props: ponies, grass. Typical use: observation, quiet watching.

**Feeding Corner** — Purpose: feeding ponies zone. Visual identity: carrot/apple basket, ponies eating gently. Important props: food baskets. Typical use: feeding, caring.

**Petting Zone** — Purpose: touching and stroking ponies. Visual identity: close pony contact, soft fur, gentle interaction. Important props: ponies. Typical use: petting, bonding.

**Observation Hill** — Purpose: viewing the entire meadow. Visual identity: soft rounded hill, panoramic view of ponies. Important props: hill, view. Typical use: perspective, calm.

**Quiet Rest Spot** — Purpose: rest and quiet sitting. Visual identity: smooth stones under tree shade, ponies in distance. Important props: stones, tree. Typical use: resting, reflecting.

## Materials

Grass: soft, plush, green. Ponies: round, fluffy, gentle. Fence: natural wood,
low, round. Hut: natural wood with green roof. Food: carrots and apples,
round, colourful. No hard surfaces, no sharp edges, no artificial materials
anywhere. Everything reads as natural and safe.

---

## Lighting

Primary light is warm natural sunlight. Morning brings soft golden light;
midday is bright and even; evening turns golden-orange with longer soft
shadows; night the ponies rest. Meadow lighting is always natural daylight.
Shadows are always soft — never hard, never dark.

---

## Colour Palette

Grass: soft green (#C8E6C9). Ponies: cream white, light brown, light grey.
Fence: natural wood brown. Hut: green roof, wood walls. Food: orange carrots,
red apples. The palette stays within this warm, gentle set across every zone.

---

## Scale

Everything is sized for a small preschool child. Ponies are child-height
(chest level). The fence is low and accessible. The hut is small and
friendly. The meadow feels spacious enough to walk but intimate enough to
feel safe.

---

## Atmosphere

Gentle. Soft. Safe. Tender. Every surface reads as natural and kind.
The mood is always calm and inviting — full of gentle movement and quiet
friendship. The meadow never feels loud or threatening.

---

## Consistency Rules

- The meadow is always wide, round, and gentle — never narrow, never loud.
- Ponies stay round, fluffy, and calm across every generation.
- Fence stays low and wooden.
- No riding equipment, no whips, no competition.
- No sharp corners, no fast movement, no scary elements.
- No dark or high-contrast lighting.
- The meadow's core shape, colours, and proportions are never redesigned or reinterpreted.

---

## Generation Considerations

The meadow must be generated as a single gentle space with kind animals,
not as a riding arena or competitive space — this is the defining trait
of this world and the most common failure point to watch for. Keep all shapes
rounded with no sharp corners anywhere. Keep the colour palette limited to
the warm gentle set defined above. Favor a soft, matte, natural material
language over any glossy, metallic, or realistic rendering. Treat the
meadow's shape, colours, and proportions as a fixed identity to preserve
exactly, not to reinterpret, across every generation.
