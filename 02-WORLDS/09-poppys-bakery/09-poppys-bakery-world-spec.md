## Purpose

Poppy's Bakery is a warm, rounded bakery that functions as the sensory heart of Pompom Hills. It is where sharing, generosity, and discovery come alive through the simple joy of fresh bread and cookies. The bakery is the world's "warm heart" — every adventure begins with a scent from here.

---

## Immutable Identity

The following elements define the canonical identity of Poppy's Bakery and must never change:

- Round warm orange-brown bakery building (#D7A86E)
- Large round display window with fresh cookies visible
- Dark wood arched front door (#8D6E63)
- One continuous open bakery interior
- Warm golden lighting throughout (#FFB74D)
- Soft, handcrafted, toy-like materials
- Round wooden counter, shelves, and furniture
- Dark brown rounded oven (#6D4C41)
- No sharp edges, no metal, no cold surfaces
- Scent of fresh bread (visual warmth)

---

## Visual Identity

A single-story, fully rounded bakery with no rectangles, no straight edges, no sharp corners anywhere. Soft matte clay-like surfaces give every part of the bakery a handcrafted, toy-like quality. The palette is warm, centred on orange-brown with wood trim and golden light. The large display window always shows fresh cookies and bread, inviting passersby inside. The overall feeling is a "living toy bakery" — approachable, warm, and visually simple enough for a preschool audience to read instantly.

---

## World Layout

The world reads as one continuous journey rather than a set of separate spaces. The street leads to the bakery front, which leads through the door into one continuous interior bakery space. That interior space contains soft activity zones rather than walled rooms. Exterior and interior are treated as a single flowing environment, not two separate sets.

---

## Spatial Relationships

- The street connects naturally to the bakery front.
- The front door opens directly into the main bakery space — no hallway.
- The counter area remains visually connected to the rest of the main space.
- The window maintains a visual relationship with both the counter and the baking corner.
- The baking corner is visible from the counter.
- The window seat shares its light source with the main space.
- The display window is visible from the street.

---

## Playable Areas

**Bakery Front** — Purpose: welcoming approach to the bakery. Visual identity: large round display window showing fresh cookies, warm orange-brown walls, dark wood door. Important props: display window, door. Typical use: arrival, first impression, scent discovery.

**Entrance** — Purpose: first step inside. Visual identity: warm interior light meeting daylight, door bell. Important props: door, bell. Typical use: arrival transition, welcoming.

**Main Bakery Space** — Purpose: the heart of the bakery, one continuous open room. Visual identity: warm orange-brown walls, counter, shelves with baking supplies. Important props: counter, display case, oven, shelves. Typical use: shared activity, gathering, warmth.

**Counter Area** — Purpose: treat display and sharing zone. Visual identity: long wooden counter with cookie trays, glass display case. Important props: counter, display case, cookie plates. Typical use: sharing, tasting, generosity.

**Baking Corner** — Purpose: preparation and creation zone. Visual identity: round oven, dough bowls, cookie cutters on shelves. Important props: oven, dough bowl, cookie cutters. Typical use: baking, creating, patience.

**Window Seat** — Purpose: rest and observation. Visual identity: large round window with cushion. Important props: window, cushion. Typical use: looking outside, quiet moments, connection.

## Materials

Exterior walls and roof: soft matte clay, smooth and toy-like. Door: smooth matte dark wood. Window frames: smooth matte white. Display: clear glass showing warm interior. Counter and shelves: smooth matte wood. Oven: soft matte dark brown. Floor: warm matte wood. All surfaces read as soft, warm, and touchable. No glass sharpness, no metal, no realistic textures anywhere.

---

## Lighting

Primary light is warm golden. Morning brings soft golden light through the windows; midday is bright and even; evening turns deep golden-orange with longer soft shadows; night is soft with warm light still glowing from inside the bakery. Indoor lighting is always warm, mixing window daylight with a cozy oven glow. Shadows are always soft and short — never hard, never dark.

## Scale

Everything is sized for a small preschool child. The bakery itself is a single, modestly sized building — large enough to feel like a real bakery, small enough to feel cozy rather than grand. The door is child-proportioned, low and round. Windows are round and modestly sized. Furniture (counter, table, chairs) reads as rounded and slightly oversized-soft rather than adult-scaled. The display is intimate, not overwhelming.

---

## Atmosphere

Warm. Soft. Safe. Inviting. Every surface reads as touchable and gentle. The smell of fresh bread is always present (visual warmth). The mood shifts gently with time of day — hopeful in the morning, cozy in the evening — but never loses its warmth or softness.

---

## Consistency Rules

- The bakery is always a small, fully rounded building — never rectangular, never sharp-edged.
- The interior is always one continuous connected space — never separate walled rooms.
- Wall colour, roof colour, and door colour stay fixed across every generation.
- The display window always shows fresh cookies.
- No modern technology, no vehicles, no realistic architecture.
- No visual clutter — each zone stays simple and readable.
- No dark or high-contrast lighting.
- No sharp edges, no metal, no cold surfaces.
- The bakery's core shape, roof, door, and window design are never redesigned or reinterpreted.

## Framing

Display window always visible in exterior shots — it is the primary visual anchor. Interior shots show counter as central element.

| Shot | Max Character % | Window Visible |
|------|:---------------:|:--------------:|
| Wide establishing | 15–20% | Always |
| Medium single | 30–35% | Yes |
| Medium two characters | 40–45% | Yes |
| Interior wide | 40–50% | From inside |
| Close-up | 65–75% | Partial |

Emotional peak = wide shot showing full bakery with warm light.

---

## Navigation

Default flow: Street (approach) → Bakery Front (discovery) → Entrance (threshold) → Main Space (warmth) → Counter/Baking (activity) → Window Seat (rest) → Exit.
The journey from street to interior — the temperature and scent change — should feel natural and welcoming.
**Alternative openings are permitted when story continuity requires them.**

## Generation Failures — Quick Reference

| Failure | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Rectangular building | Reject — round only |
| Sharp edges anywhere | Smooth all forms |
| Cold colour temperature | Warm to 4500K, recheck palette |
| Display window empty | Add fresh cookies |
| Dark or cold interior | Add warm light |
| Metal surfaces present | Replace with wood |
| Messy or dirty bakery | Clean and organize |
| Modern objects present | Remove — no technology |
